THE PLAGUE-STRICKEN

...At first people disappeared rarely. Before that, since the conclusion of vassalage with the cykts, crimes against the person on Earth simply didn't occur, so even rare disappearances were emergencies.

Most of all, perhaps, were stirred up by this the cykts. The little bears on the planet instantly organised into rescue groups, which with incredible persistence began searching for traces of the missing. A week later they found the first. More precisely — what remained of him by this point. The unfortunate young man from the youth organisation "Interplanetary Friendship" had been savagely tortured. His ritual cykt-wife — in sign of mourning shaved the middle of her body and withdrew to the Citadel of Sorrow.

Over the next month the other missing in the first wave were found too. All of them in one way or another belonged to groups of interspecies cooperation. All of them had been tortured.

Ten people is very few by past centuries' measures. That many disappeared without a trace per month in a large 20th-century city. And this was considered absolutely normal. But in the new world, in the new reality this was an emergency of galactic scale. Total observation was established. Satellites scanned every square metre of Earth's surface. Every person was supplied with a personal beacon, and if a signal cut off or sharply shifted — to the incident location immediately flew an emergency-rescue group.

The space around Earth was surrounded by combat ships — so densely a mouse wouldn't slip through, as ancestors said. Not a gram in, not a gram out. The resources engaged would have sufficed for several space wars. But the Community didn't spare them. For the first time in twenty years the empire faced a challenge to its might.

And for the first time in twenty years fear came to Earth.

For some time this helped. For several months people stopped disappearing. And then again almost a hundred people vanished.

The trouble is they all belonged to so-called "refuseniks" — those who for one or another ideological reason refused to wear beacons. There were very few of them percentage-wise — one in ten thousand. Strange, eccentrics, suicidal, paranoid... But for twelve billion of Earth's and colonies' population this meant — a million two hundred thousand potential victims. By this point it had already become known that people were disappearing in colonies too.

The unknown abductors journalists nicknamed Butchers. And not all believed the coincidence of the nickname with one still-living old man was accidental.

François Perrin himself by that moment was already imprisoned in specially restored for this Alcatraz prison. Although his last attack didn't achieve its goal, the cykts decided to no longer risk. However — again showing humanity, since otherwise the retired admiral would be torn apart by his compatriots.

— Do you understand what this means, Chief?

— I understand, I'm no fool. I don't know about terrorists — but this operation definitely cornered us. Without a single shot. Either we wear beacons — meaning bound hand and foot — or we can easily be eliminated, and no one will even understand what happened.

— You think the abductions are the cykts' doing?

— No, Alice. I think some smart-aleck of ours is again playing into their hands. And they're just using this to finish us off.

— But all members of cooperation organisations wear beacons. "Refuseniks" — mostly xenophobes. If Butchers continue abducting them, this won't give the necessary propaganda effect.

— Unfortunately — it will. Precisely because the abductors will inevitably be considered cykts, I think it's someone of the Earthlings. They're not stupid enough to compromise themselves.

— You say "they." But the Community is by no means monolithic. There are different groupings and interest struggles in it. Couldn't it be we've encountered such backroom struggle? And our planet just became an exchange pawn in the anthill game?

— Not excluded. I'll task working out this version.

Several large youth organisations of interplanetary friendship held "Against Terror" rallies. The rallies were guarded by cykt special forces, so even flies didn't fly over the crowd. By the way — this vigilance gave rise to an unpleasant incident:

Some smart-aleck of the students read about the "dove of peace" tradition. As a result, at the culmination, when the organisation's leader finished reading a heartfelt speech about world friendship and peace — a bunch of his organisation's members released snow-white doves.

The poor birds didn't fly further than half a metre from the start points. The vigilant cykt spec ops, absolutely unfamiliar with ancient Earth traditions, methodically annihilated all "potential biological threat," significantly staining the protesters with the blood of innocent birds. Considerable got on Maxim, who was among those releasing doves.

Sorting out the situation, the cykt leadership offered its deepest apologies and as a sign of apology offered all the injured (i.e., those splashed with dove blood) — a ride at the host side's expense to one of the cyktian resort-planets. Luckily the aliens' observation technology easily allowed identifying all the splashed.

Max with pleasure spent a whole month on green as New Zealand before the Wellington Catastrophe Stsisketea, met there several girls from North America, and even picked himself a ritual partner from cykts. Home he headed refreshed, tanned, rested — to with new strength take up studies at the faculty of hydroponic riips-technologies.

He didn't know and didn't see how a subspace interceptor docked with the ship. By irony of fate, exactly the same modules had intercepted Perrin's bomb-ships.

He didn't hear how a battle unfolded in the liner's corridors — furious and merciless, a battle in which cykts for the first time in a good thousand years killed cykts. The crew sold their lives very dearly — for every pilot, mechanic and even stewardess the attackers paid with three of their own. Only they weren't three times more, but ten times.

In the cabin the light simply went out. And the next second his perception went out too.

...Max lay on his back. In principle, fog still stood in his head and it spun nicely. As required to prove — a stunning blow is impossible without concussion.

By sensations — he lay on something hard, oilcloth-like and was completely naked. Quite bright light beat at his eyes from the ceiling. The feeling didn't leave that someone was watching him from the side. Unfortunately, checking was impossible. His head was rigidly fixed by clamps. Limbs were also fixed.

— Hey! He-e-e-ey!!!!! Is anyone alive here????!!!! — Max tried to attract anyone's attention. Silence was his answer. Max called out again. And again.

No effect.

Time stretched like jelly.

Gradually heaviness accumulated in his bladder. Max held as long as he could, but ultimately couldn't take it. What had happened evoked in him the widest gamut of feelings. Moreover, the bigger need also began pressing. With it Max managed to hold a bit longer. Though gradually it also surrendered. Naked, crucified on a strange stand, befouled Max felt, mildly speaking, uncomfortable. Speaking more precisely — he experienced unprecedented frustration and shame.

However, this didn't last so long. The exhausted organism switched off.

He came to in a noticeably more cheerful spirit. The only thing that confused was the appearance nearby of an IV connected to the vein of his left hand. Moreover — he felt himself clean. Apparently, while he was unconscious — they washed him. Max listened to his sensations and understood his stomach was packed tight. Apparently the unknown abductors took advantage of his helpless state — and force-fed him through a tube.

Max jerked and yelled:

— Damned fascists! Nazis! Beasts! Cykto-haters!

While yelling — the first anger passed. Time stretched just as slowly. Max languished. His whole body itched. By sensations, in the area of shoulder blades and buttocks — the first bedsores began appearing: apparently those who washed him didn't bother to dry him.

Time later — he understood it was pressing him again. This time he was ready to fall into the abyss of dishonour much faster, but at the last moment from the side came a tender girlish voice: "What, just like a wild animal?! Under yourself?!"

The voice sounded quite close, and judging by timbre, belonged to a lady of unearthly beauty. Max was again seized by a sense of shame. Judging by the volume of voice and by the voice of an elderly man who answered her ("Don't be surprised — now you'll see everything yourself: he's already tense, noticed?") — the girl was somewhere quite close and looking right at him.

The result was multi-hour struggle with his own organism, supplemented by periodic questions "Well, will he soon?" — "Any moment now, be patient. The monitor shows he's on the edge."

The monitor didn't lie, however Max, all in his father who died together with the battleship "Iowa" in the Belt — was distinguished by stone willpower. It also helped that with peripheral vision he noticed two figures periodically approaching his bedhead. Once he even caught a thin female arm passing through his field of vision, with a doll-like, as if porcelain wrist.

As a result — this time he even broke his previous record, but when the bottom did blow, it blew at both barrels at once.

So vile Max hadn't felt himself in long. Doubly offensive became when girlish laughter sounding like silver bells rang out and the owner of the enchanting voice began dousing him with water from a fire hose. Terribly cold water, by the way.

Vile too was that after the procedure, the enchantress lifted Max's pride with two fingers, after which she unclenched her fingers, allowing the pride with a wet splat to crash down. "Small calibre, hmm..." said the bitch and, judging by the click of heels, departed.

This torture of pride was enough for Max for almost a day of anger. Then he conked out again.

Came to finally from the sensation that many eyes were watching him. Someone was clearly in his field of vision. Max with difficulty focused his gaze and saw a cykt's little face. By facial expression — the cykt was extremely concerned with something he could see from the side.

— Thank God! You found me! — Max was so delighted he even jerked to embrace his saviour. Unfortunately the roach-bear hadn't yet unstrapped him from the torture table, so the impulse was nipped in the bud. Nevertheless — it attracted the cykt's attention.

— You're too labile, young man.

— What???

— You're too labile. Your psyche too quickly gets used to stress and stimulation. That's not interesting and unproductive. You too quickly adapt to the rules of the game.

— I don't understand?!

— The flow from you was ten times weaker than from your predecessor.

— What flow?! What are you on about at all? Will you save me??

— And why? — The cykt, it seemed, was interested seriously.

— But... you're kind? You're... humanists???? I'm one of the "Universal House of Friendship"!

— Categories of kindness or evil are strictly individual, you understand? We are — of course humane. Otherwise not only you would be lying here.

— I... I don't understand!

— We are curious. Even more — we are emotionally poor. We need fresh impressions. Many impressions. At first — you could satisfy this demand of ours with your books and films. But — we quickly grew sated, and new, in the sense of completely new, unique — you almost don't create. The turning point was the presence of one of our doctors in an operating room where, due to a number of unique natural characteristics of the patient, a small operation was conducted without anaesthesia. The doctor — sated himself with impressions to the brim. We — too. — while the cykt said this, he moved into a corner of the room and rattled there, apparently, instruments. About the purpose of those instruments Max tried not to think at all.

The cykt finished his speech finding himself at Max's right thigh. The next second Max howled from unexpected and sharp pain.

— One can use electrostimulation — but that's the lot of poorly educated rabble. I — shall perform on you a fugue of pain with one scalpel and clamps alone.

— You're insane! — Max howled — You're a psycho! You'll be found and terribly punished by the cykts themselves!!!

The last statement seemed so interesting to the furry torturer that he even tore himself away from extracting Max's femoral vein from his leg, and pressed some button. The light of the surgical, shadowless lamp went out for a time. Max's eyes got used to the semi-darkness...

And he saw above him powerful glazing packed tight with plush little cykt faces. Even in alien eyes were read greed and insatiability... And the next second came pain... A fugue of pain...

— And where is he now? — I sipped a small glass of riips milk.

— Undergoing rehabilitation, at one of the sanatoriums in Antarctica. His body we quickly restored, but the psyche will require lengthy rehabilitation. He almost lost his mind. By the way, here's your Sign of Usefulness — a furry paw handed me a flickering holographic star. I reluctantly hung it on my chest. Essentially, this is a reward for betrayal.

Yes, my idea worked. To sew into all "friends" without their knowledge fully organic subspace beacons. And weaken security on transport going to Earth and from Earth. Only thus did we manage to reach the gang of sensual narcotics addicts (more precisely, narco-cykts).

These guys were no longer interested in depth and complexity of emotions — only in their strength. Compared to the usual emotional gourmandism of their kin — it's the same as guzzling pure spirit instead of refined wines. And like any binge — this didn't contribute to rational thinking. They completely lost caution and common sense.

By setting up Maxim, I probably saved hundreds of thousands of future victims. And finally, we pulled him out in time. Almost in time.

But nevertheless — it was a set-up. We couldn't beforehand explain to the human bait what awaited them. Many of them had emotional control, but under torture usual defence methods can break down. And if the Butchers had sensed something off, the whole operation could have gone bust.

And still I don't throw out the Sign of Usefulness. On the whole planet only eight people besides me have the same. This isn't just an order. Not a pretty trinket. It's a document allowing me to travel without restrictions within space mastered by cykts. It's hard to even imagine offhand how many uses the underground will find for it.

— The Butchers... what about them?

— All cykts in the gang were destroyed. All people — delivered to special cells on Alcatraz. I raise a brow, not hiding surprise.

— All cykts to a one? That's about ten thousand! What about your famous humanism?

— A hundred thousand — Shaovera gloomily corrects me. — It's precisely out of pity that we had to destroy them. They're no longer cykts. Sensual narco-degradation isn't just irreversible. It's also monstrously contagious. All the troopers who participated in the operation committed suicide. The Butchers managed to transmit to them the emo-images of a human under torture. Their nervous system already began degenerating too.

Goosebumps run over my skin.

— You mean to say... that any cykt... is capable of becoming a Butcher?

— Yes. It's enough to once see a human in a state of prolonged and unbearable suffering. The addiction statistics first time is sixty percent, second time eighty, third time no one withstands.

— So we are your drug?

— A heavy drug, destroying the organism. And on top contagious. With secondary perception — transmission through another cykt's empathic field, without direct contact with humans — addiction statistics are somewhat lower, but also depressing. In this connection I wanted to ask you a service...

— What kind?

— The human part of the gang. We can't contact them, fearing a new wave of epidemic. But it's very desirable to interrogate them. We want to understand what they were guided by, helping humiliate and torture their own kin.

— Probably cooperated simply for pay...

"Or were fanatical cykt-lovers." But I won't say this aloud.

— Unlikely. They couldn't fail to see what was happening with their cykt accomplices. In a word... how do you look on, with the help of our equipment, trying to make them talk?

I recall how Max thrashed on the white board, and clench fists. Then I get up, grinning predatorily.

— Give me instruments and show how to use them. I'll do it. Not for you, but for myself. And with great pleasure.

A thin fragile body of a naked girl is crucified in a chair resembling a gynaecological one. And a beautiful young man, naked to the waist, looms over her. Behind my shoulders soar two drones, ready to execute any my order — even atomise the captive if I want. Resembles an illustration to a BDSM-fanfic. But this concerns me little.

I'm the first Earthling in twenty years to operate a real, modern combat weapon. But now this also concerns me little.

Because I know perfectly well the girl in the chair.

Victoria Stuttgart, twenty-two. Daughter of President Otto Stuttgart. One of the underground's most outstanding figures — smart, decisive, ruthless. A year ago she "quit," calling all our activity "frivolous" and "child's games." So that's what she was doing all this time!

— Vika, why?!

She raises her head, mockingly smiles:

— Do you really think I'll tell you?

— You will. I studied their equipment. Your scalpels and hooks are primitive, toys worthy of those degenerates you served. This equipment will make anyone talk...

— Ow!

— Don't worry, that's not yet the start of torture. Just an injection.

— "Truth serum"?

— In some sense. This needle introduced into your skull nanomachines that will now begin building alternative neural pathways. In an hour I'll have a complete map of your brain, with the possibility of controlling it. I'll be able to stimulate or conversely block any area. Strengthen or weaken memories, put you to sleep or not let you switch off, stimulate the pain centre or pleasure centre, determine with ninety-eight percent probability whether you tell the truth or lie...

— And aren't you afraid you yourself after me will end up in this chair? You also have something to hide, Sergei...

— I'm afraid. But I have two advantages over you. Firstly, sane cykts now fear contact with a human under torture like fire...

— You think they won't be able to hire a couple of cykt-philes for interrogation?! You're even dumber than I considered...

— And secondly, to get correct answers, even on the most perfect equipment — you need to ask the right questions. I know what to ask you. And no one knows what to ask me. I'm clean.

Actually I lied a bit. On Earth several people know the right questions. But they certainly won't be among the interrogators.

— Don't make me bother, Vika. Just tell why you did this.

— Don't pretend to be a clean one. Bother, do me such a favour. I'm interested in how long I can hold out.

Of course, it's not excitement here. With my spinal cord I feel — she's stalling for time. But for what? A trap within a trap? Just not on Earth.

— Pass to the commander — I address the drone over my right shoulder — Heightened security regime needed. Not excluded that the Butchers planned something.

— Passed — calmly answers the machine.

I calm down a bit. If cykts do something, they do it qualitatively. Otherwise they simply don't know how. Excluding the situation when their nervous system degrades from sensual dependence, of course.

I had to bother for three hours (including an hour for implant formation). Vika did turn out to be a hard nut. She passed the same course of resistance to interrogations as I. Note for the future — in this chair none of us can consider our brains safe.

If we'll have any future at all, after all this. After what she said when she did speak.

— Stupid... can it be... you still haven't guessed yourself? While those idiots in the Centre accumulated information and played terr-r-ibly secret conspirators, I found a weapon capable of destroying them as a race. The only real weapon we have. A weapon that doesn't give a damn about their numbers and their level of technical development. A weapon they themselves with joy drag into their maw!

My breath catches. This isn't hooliganism, not petty revenge. This is a thought-out operation.

— Do you even understand, fool, what will happen if your insane plan works?!

— In less than one of our generations, their civilisation will cease to exist.

— And ours too! — I in fury slap this self-confident idiot across the face. She only mockingly smiles. Battery is such a trifle compared to what I was doing with her five minutes ago... Moreover, for her this is recognition of merit.

She's not an empath, but it seems to me now she's savouring my feelings like a cykt. Possibly picked this up while working with her addicts.

— Our civilisation long ago ceased to exist, Sergei. It's just you don't want to notice this. Perrin understood — that's why he launched the missiles.

— And you decided to follow in his footsteps?!

— No. My blow will be real. Without pathetic gestures.

— Will be?!

I in horror take a step back.

— Oh, I think I said "will"? Sorry, I misspoke. Right would be to say — "is." This is happening right now, Sergei. We started as soon as I was arrested. One hour was enough. But I delayed you for three — just in case. I myself wasn't sure I'd hold out that long.

Repentance by Burnt Offering. Until the Butcher launched the bomb-ships, this idea seemed merely eccentric fantasy. But after, when panic and shame spread across the planet in waves, a certain part of the population considered it... no, not attractive. But necessary.

Cykts without hesitation sacrifice themselves for their civilisation. We must show we can do this too. Several people will take on the sins of the whole race, as Christ once did. Earth in fire entered a new era — and in fire must emerge from it, born renewed.

They thought it all through very competently. The volunteers wore beacons — but all their contacts were quite legal, and monitoring programs didn't pay attention to their actions.

They knew that cykt medicine is capable of healing even the heaviest traumas, not excluding burns. And that rescuers arrive maximum a minute after an alarm signal. Therefore ordinary petrol wouldn't do. They injected themselves with a flammable mixture under the skin via injections. A mixture that burned without oxygen access, wasn't extinguished by water or carbon dioxide. Burned very brightly and hotly, but still gave off insufficient heat to kill them instantly.

The action was conducted at 18:00 planetary time. 18 people who participated in it symbolised the 18 billion burned cykts of Alpha and Secunda. Strictly simultaneously, before the eyes of large crowds of cykts, the suicides clicked their lighters.

With each was a partner. He explained the meaning of the performance while his comrade burned...

— And what... do you intend to do now?

— And what, in your opinion, can be done? With all her eccentricity, your friend is right. We lost. We're leaving this sector of the Galaxy, human. You can celebrate victory or mourn — to taste. Possibly we'll return when we're better ready for contact with beings like you. But in the currently existing form this contact is fatal for both sides.

— But... but there are cykts left on Earth!

— They're all already infected. We can't take them, without violating quarantine. Those still capable of controlling themselves will commit suicide. Those who turned into vampires you'll have to handle yourselves. There are ten billion of you, less than a million of them. They have no heavy equipment, drones are already self-liquidating. You are resourceful beings. I think you'll cope.

The investigator was wrong in one thing. "Coping" wasn't necessary.

The situation quickly changed... Not for the better: the youth loved cykts too strongly, so the idea of sacrificing themselves to satisfy the needs of the Infected — simply couldn't fail to arise, especially considering practically a direct request from the side of remaining cykts...

They say when the decrepit old man sitting in one of the farthest cells of Alcatraz was told all this — he laughed very long and angrily.
