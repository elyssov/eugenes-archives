THE PRICE OF HONOUR

When we signed the capitulation, I was lying in hospital. Not that anything had hit me — far from it. It was simply the only way to hide me from the crowd. People in general very quickly change their idols.

A couple of times reporters found me. These "sharks of the pen" were very interested in how I was feeling, and whether I dreamed at night of the planets of Calypso.

No, I don't dream of them.

When I gave the order to launch the "planetary boosters" — I thought only that it was necessary for my race. We are competitors in this universe. It's tight for us together here.

When the fiery squall enveloped Alpha and Secunda, I didn't even think about how many aliens were at that moment turning into barbecue.

Moreover — I don't think about it now either.

Probably this is a quirk of fate — that the Aliens turned out to be small, fluffy insects resembling at once both bear cubs and kittens. Yes, such six-legged-six-armed kitten-cubs, with a hard chitinous shell under a thick layer of fluffy fur. Warm-blooded to boot.

Honestly — I don't care.

And at that moment, there was no choice at all.

Just think — to discover an alien civilisation in one sector of the galaxy. And immediately learn they occupy the same planets — they too need oxygen, water, comfortable temperature — same as us.

And also — their refusal of direct contact and attempt to wall themselves off, on the edge of hostility.

It's now they explain that as soon as they met us they understood we were deep down "possessed by evil." That they wanted to avoid violence and bloodshed.

Aha.

Though — maybe they really are good white and fluffy. I don't care. The main thing is they're damned insects. Who need the same planets as we do.

In general — all that happened is the screw-up of our intelligence. Well, who knew that those two planets were only a slice of their stellar empire? Who knew they'd settled them ten years before our arrival — just that their technologies allow building cities at the speed Earth bees build hives?

But — it was perfectly clear that we wouldn't coexist.

Besides — the cykts themselves, in contradiction to their own claims, managed to create a precedent for conflict.

Who asked them to land colonists on Shamanka and Eclipse? We were there first! We warned them!

The bees waved it off and settled on the opposite hemisphere.

Now they speak of principles of humanism and that they were sure two races could coexist on a huge planet. Of course.

Naturally tension arose. And considering their refusal of contacts — it only grew.

And of course President Stuttgart gave the order for a military operation.

Actually — we should have understood right away what this smelled of. When a "peaceful colonist" manages to put up worthy resistance to a heavy trooper of the space marines corps — that should already have made us think.

We made the wrong conclusions. And intelligence let us down. To the fullest extent. That's a fact.

But it was precisely the ground battles that became the prerequisite for creating the "planetary boosters." Simply to fight them on the surface of mother planets (at any rate — we considered them such) would have been pure insanity. So — we wanted to resolve the issue at once. With one blow.

No one knew that a fleet from their metropole would follow. No one knew that multi-billion population on planets is the norm for any of their middling colonies. And yes, of course we didn't know they had many such colonies, just not in our sector.

We fought. I defended Earth to the last ship. To the last drop of blood. It was extremely hard for the Aliens to reach Earth.

And still — when the enemy exceeds you both in number and in quality of ships by so much — our resistance was doomed.

We prepared to fight for every inch of the planet or to die in nuclear flame — like their colonists.

We were arming children and old men...

And then they blew up the Moon ("As edification" — as they claim) and made contact...

And suddenly — everything changed.

President Stuttgart shot himself. The press hounded him. Otto — Otto... My friend...

Lads who survived the bloody meat-grinder and took thousands of cykts with them. You are now all executioners and freaks. Each of you remembers the eyes of friends who gave their lives for Earth. After all, only you survived — one in a hundred.

You are all criminals.

And the main war criminal is me. Admiral of the United Fleet of Earth, François Perrin.

Of course, after Otto's death the party of traitors came to power. Conciliators. Hippies. They had never held weapons in their hands. They had never seen the fading eyes of friends, except perhaps fading from an overdose.

But they knew how to speak much, beautifully and floridly.

And they spoke. The damned roaches — as it turned out — very much love this. Continuous negotiations began.

I was in hospital.

Today, I recall those events of two decades ago. The bugs didn't know then that, in principle, we already knew the coordinates of all their planets. They didn't know we already had jump bomb-ships carrying enhanced booster charges (we managed to master many useful cykt technologies)... Very few knew of this in general — this project was supervised personally by me.

Otto shot himself because the situation became hopeless. Yes, undoubtedly we could destroy the worlds of our enemies. Moreover — this time these would definitely be their main planets, not peripheral colonies.

Only the invasion fleet at this time would have turned Earth into another asteroid belt around the Sun.

And one doesn't shake fists after a fight. The cykts said they came in peace. More precisely — in peace, after we signed capitulation.

And we signed it.

For twenty years now we've lived as a vassal colony of the Community of Cykts.

Our masters have forgiven us our sins, not forgetting, however, to remind us of them and of their magnanimity. The new generation of Earthlings repents before the cykts for "crimes" committed by us, their fathers.

We're forbidden to have our own fleet — we use the transport services of our "furry friends." Actually, I'm afraid that should such a desire arise today, we couldn't even build a simple hyperdrive.

We have no army. Why would we, if the masters have proclaimed a "Golden Era of Prosperity." And yes — we are, perhaps, prospering.

Cykt technologies gave us as much food as we wanted, greatly advanced our medicine. And also — cykts welcome creativity. I didn't even think we had so many artists, poets, writers, musicians. Effectively — it's enough to bring any daub to one of the Centres and declare yourself an artist to receive a salary and get into their Registry of Creative Resources.

Actually, the entire population of the planet either draws and makes music or grows riips. The basic raw material for subsequent organic synthesis of all sorts of food in the cykt technological cycle.

But scientists, historians, engineers — we don't have. Well, except in my generation. And soon there won't be any at all.

When capitulation was signed, they wanted to hand me over to the cykts. In the Earthlings' view, this would be logical and just: to give the injured party the "Calypsian Butcher."

The cykts only shook their heads sorrowfully, after which they explained this would not be humane toward me. Moreover — the most terrible punishment for a sentient being, from their point of view, is to leave him alive and let him realise the magnitude of his crimes himself.

It seems to me — they already knew then what they would do with us.

From generosity — they didn't even take our two colonies on Shamanka and Eclipse. There too now are vast plantations of riips.

The cykts themselves can be met everywhere here. They evidently like our planet. All our youth revolves around them. Everyone now under 30.

All sorts of "Clubs of Interstellar Friends," "Societies of Mutual Understanding"...

Our children love the cykts and hate us.

Hate us just because we wanted a different future for them: We wanted to give them the whole Universe.

Now they consider themselves practically a different nation. The cykts themselves constantly emphasise that "there is no evil in them." Perhaps. To me they look more like spineless rags licking furry roach asses.

Yesterday I saw how a beautifully dressed young man, clearly from yuppies, most likely one of the riips moguls, flopped before a family of cykts on his knees. Flopped and fervently began begging their forgiveness for everything.

For two planets burned to ash. For 18 billion colonists. For one and a half hundred ships of the invasion fleet, for which we paid with three thousand cruisers with all crews.

In general — he sprawled on the ground and shook in hysterical repentance... And my chest burned with a small stainless steel key.

By my order, it was cut from the armour of my flagship. Back then — I could still give orders. Those were the last days when old Earth was still alive. When her spirit was alive.

The man who handed me the key, I shot personally. He knew it would be so. Having handed over the key, he stood before me on one knee and to the last second looked up, into my eyes.

His colleagues were shot by my bodyguards. The secret had to be observed.

Otto's body was still cooling in the presidential hospital, the senate was torn apart by faction struggles whose positions diverged only on which spot to lick on the cykts.

And I walked, stepping over the corpses of people who laid down their lives to preserve for our planet remnants of honour.

And still — I could not do what I had to.

For twenty years I wore on my chest this simple, unadorned and unpainted piece of steel with sharp edges.

For twenty years I slept without dreams — simply because I was surrounded by one nightmarish dream.

But today I understood it's time to wake up.

Now I'll finish my black and viscous-as-resin coffee. Here in Vienna, on Trattnerhof street is my favourite café on Earth. I don't know why this came about — they say coffee is brewed much tastier in Paris or Moscow. Perhaps it's a matter of habit. Here was the presidential palace, here, quite close — was the building of the General Staff of Earth's VKS.

A matter of habit, I suppose. In any case — for 20 years now I've come here and drunk a cup of black coffee. The waiters have long stopped asking me anything. Actually — they've long since stopped even trying to talk to me. To kill me, no one on Earth can since the signing of capitulation. But — they can ignore me to the extent possible. On the other hand — I bring their café considerable money. By three in the afternoon, crowds gather to look at the Butcher.

So, now I'll finish my cup of coffee. Then I'll get up and head to an unremarkable house on Braunstadt street. Descend there into the cellar and enter a musty cubby cluttered with all sorts of antique junk.

There and only there I'll take out the key. Brush dust from the lid of the piano standing in the very corner, insert the key into the slot on its lid and turn it exactly a quarter turn.

The lock will click. Somewhere far away, in the Alps, at multi-kilometre depth, in a shaft — one little light will turn on. I know exactly that it will be green.

I'll throw open the lid and begin playing a waltz. Ancient, majestic and flowing music written once by Strauss will flow filling the premises... And from rocket silos hidden across the planet — just as slowly but mightily will emerge twenty streamlined bodies. Having risen to a hundred kilometres' altitude — they will in a bright flash disappear from Earth's atmosphere, generating monstrous vortices on the planet itself...

Where they are destined to emerge — only twenty people knew when I drew my pistol. Today — only I know.

By evening — the whole world will know about this. As an accomplished fact.

For now — I'll finish my coffee. I'm in no hurry. Don't fear the old Butcher. Admire your fill.
