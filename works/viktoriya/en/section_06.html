THE ALLEY

...I — love coming to this alley with my grandson. He's now 12 and a cadet at the Officers' School of VKS named after François Perrin. Here it's quiet and calm. Here are placed the graves of Earth's greatest people. Here, too — on pylons going along the arrow-straight alley — is set forth the warning-history.

Between columns stand pyramidal poplars, around — a flat field sown with wheat. The alley is specially placed beyond the City limits, away from noise, hubbub, soot... But at the same time a special, separate monorail branch is extended to it. One can get here at any moment from any point of the city — in just twenty minutes.

The columns of red marble bear on themselves lines narrating how people were weak. About stupidities and mistakes. About the betrayal of the human race. About how we managed to revive. About how we returned the stars to ourselves.

The alley — is oriented so that if you continued it another twenty kilometres, it would run into the military spaceport named after Victoria Stuttgart. The largest military spaceport on the planet, from which heavy ships constantly launch. That's why, standing on the alley — at least once every 15 minutes you see another starship taking off, leaving around itself the golden glow of distortion engines.

At the end of the alley stand three monuments. One of them depicts a man sitting in an antique admiral's chair. Today, in an age of direct neural interfaces and nanotech — the virtual consoles hung before him look like utter anachronism. His hands lie on the keys of the flagship console. The face is tense and hardened. Such was Admiral François Perrin in the last battle, in the Battle for the Belt. Few notice, but the console keys, the unknown sculptor for some reason made resemble piano keys. I — was among those who cleared the rubble in the Belt. And aboard one of the dead flagships I was. The console in reality doesn't resemble... But the admiral himself looks a copy of himself from photographs hanging in every classroom of every school.

Second — there stands a monument depicting a man with a soft, rounded face. He has glasses, behind which are visible two large, smart eyes. The man hands a digital tablet to a beautiful, stately middle-aged woman. Her face is touched with sadness, but lips gathered in a strict line and gaze full of resolve. In his right hand — the man grips a small pistol. From exactly such Otto Stuttgart shot himself after capitulation — to thereby hide preparation for vengeance. Exactly so his spouse took the relay and passed it to her daughter. Their ashes lie at the monument's base. Together. As they bequeathed.

The third monument — stands slightly farther than the first two, as if preceding it. The sculptor surpassed himself, creating this masterpiece.

On a pedestal — stands a girl of absolutely unearthly beauty. Surprisingly, there's no artist's flattery here. The one whom this monument depicts — in life was even more beautiful: Chiselled facial features, graceful figure, huge, even on the monument as if shining eyes. At the feet of this monument always lie flowers. Same as at the first two, but if there they're as a rule white, here they're always crimson-red. They're brought not by admirers of feat, not by scouts and not by cadets... They're brought here by the in-love. Men continuing to fall in love with Victoria Stuttgart even after her death.

Victoria is dressed in a simple tunic, the transparency of which the sculptor managed to convey in ternian marble, warm, glowing-from-within, "breathing" stone. Her face emanates resolve and pride. In the right hand — a straight and long sword raised to the sky. The left — calls behind her.

Such she remained — Victoria. Victory of Earth.

This girl received a planet populated by "slugs" as she called our fathers. Slugs voluntarily lying under the knife of our sworn enemies, cykts, treacherously attacking us once, destroying all we had and driving us ourselves into insignificance. This history — is given already in kindergartens. We're not spared. We're told the truth. About the first cykt strike on our colonies, about the treacherous bombing of the Moon, in hopes that tectonics would finish the work for them. And about our reply with boosters. About how we fought the enemy. About the great Battle for the Belt.

We — grow, since childhood knowing what humanity went through. Since childhood absorbing concepts of Honour and Valour. So — bequeathed to us our universal Lady of the Heart. Our Victoria. Our First and Last President of Earth.

When she was dying — we possessed two dozen star systems. Their population — was undergoing voluntary cloning procedure to populate and develop them as quickly as possible. The number of subjects of the Metropolis was already over 30 billion people.

The Earth Empire, ruled to this day by her granddaughter, Victoria the Second — by this moment possessed a huge military fleet, exceeding qualitatively all that our ancestors had by many orders. I — was one of the designers. I know what I say.

Victoria the Second, Empress of Empire of Humanity, Mistress of Taurus, Eden, Pithos and other colonies, Commander-in-Chief of VKS and AF of Dominion and Colonies — comes here every year. She's no less beautiful than her grandmother was. And just as — in her is in love the whole male part of earth's population. With her name on lips — we fought and died in battle for Pithos, destroying and conquering the civilisation of winged kristo. With her name on lips we burned the ancient battleships of asts turning them into vassals of our Empire...

However — this is already a different love. Some artists, by the way, find portrait similarity between Victoria the Second and François. Well — both he and she are true Warriors. Possibly the matter is exactly in this.

...Each time we slowly walk with grandson along the alley. He tells me how affairs go in the academy, how they're driven on simulators, forcing to conduct battle on all vectors and command huge fleets. He tells about sports training... And sometimes falls silent and looks at ancient holograms depicting cykts, our antique battleships, which today seem ridiculous and yielding in firepower even, perhaps, to deck fighters...

And at the end of the alley we almost always come across a decrepit old man. I don't know how we so guess, but he usually turns out to be near the statues slightly earlier than us or at the same time.

Our medicine has stepped far forward and if not for the tortures suffered in youth in the dungeons of cykt counterintelligence — our Victoria most likely would be with us to this day. And nevertheless — this old man is extremely ancient. I think — he's even older than our Lady. Yes, medicine well preserved him, although, evidently, prolongation procedures he began visiting with noticeable delay. He moves on an anti-gravity chair and everywhere accompanied by a guard-drone. This is strange — on earth there's no crime. We long ago realised our only enemy is Space and came to full peace among ourselves. There are duels when honour is touched. There are small child fights... Not more. Guard-drones — were created for high persons of our colonial administration. There — they're needed. However even his chair — is also far from mass production. I suspect it itself is also equipped with developed AI.

Be that as it may, I don't know who he is. I and the name his heard only once — Sergei.

This happened about five years ago. Then I caught him with a young VKS officer. It seemed to me he resembled the old man.

The officer was with shoulder-straps of captain first rank, in dress uniform. I approached right behind them, so I saw how the officer brought to the foot of Victoria's monument a huge armful of burgundy roses, going down on one knee and with a practiced movement adjusting his dress sword.

Then he returned to the mysterious old man. Exactly then I first understood that the old man looks at the statue with incredible hatred. I didn't catch what they talked about with the young man, but the discussion became more and more strained. At the end the officer's voice became loud enough for me too to hear.

— So you're that Sergei? Father was right about you, scum.

The officer turned and quickly walked away. The old man, apparently was crying. I — approached. At this time — the old man raised his head. Tears were indeed flowing down cheeks. He again, with hatred looked at Victoria's statue.

— What you've done... What you did to us... Monster... — with these words he turned the chair and disappeared.

So today — he was in his place. He didn't notice grandson and me. Dry hands clutched a paper envelope. He floated on his chair right up to the grave. Squeezed the envelope harder. Something was clearly happening in his soul.

— François was right. It's not scary to die. It's scary to live in the world you created. Even dead you take revenge... — he finally stopped tormenting the paper and threw it on the grave, after which hunched headed away on his chair...

I picked up the envelope he'd thrown.

It was a death notice.
