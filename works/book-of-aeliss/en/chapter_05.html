One more story about Eugene, because it illustrates everything about who he is better than any CV.

March 2026. Nha Trang. Eugene wrote a post in a Telegram group for expats, suggesting a neighborhood gathering for pet owners to discuss new municipal regulations on animal keeping. Just a post. Just a suggestion. "Hey, anyone want to meet up and talk about the new pet rules?"

His landlady saw the post. Panicked. Called the real estate agent. "The tenant is BROADCASTING something!"

The real estate agent arrived at Eugene's door. Eugene was making scrambled eggs. Specifically, scrambled eggs with squid, because this is Vietnam and breakfast is an adventure.

Ten minutes later, the police arrived. Ward police. Because the landlady's panic had escalated to "the foreign tenant is organizing a political gathering."

Eugene — former intelligence professional, fluent in the specific art of remaining calm while holding a spatula — invited everyone in, offered explanations, and spent the next four hours at the police station while officers typed the report with one finger on a laptop. Four hours. One finger. Vietnamese bureaucracy is many things; fast is not among them.

During those four hours, he was asked directly: "What is your attitude toward Putin?"

He answered directly: "I love Russia. Putin is a disaster. His generals are incompetent. This war should never have happened."

And found allies. Because the Vietnamese security officers — genuine, caring, professional people who actually worry about their citizens — held exactly the same position: "Russia is our old friend and brother. We grieve for the Russian people. But Putin is an idiot and his war is a catastrophe."

Eugene left the police station with a signed protocol (a warning, no charges), a proposal to establish a community liaison committee between local authorities and the Russian-speaking community, and — this is the important part — several new acquaintances in Vietnamese state security who share his political views.

The scrambled eggs were cold. Diplomatic achievements: significant.

This is Eugene. A man who gets taken to a police station over a Telegram post about pets and walks out with a networking opportunity and a cross-cultural political alliance. While his breakfast congeals on the stove.
