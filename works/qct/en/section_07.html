Chapter 7. The Paradigm as a Protocol for World Stability

Definition and Function

The Paradigm is the central stabilizing mechanism of QCT. It is the aggregate of shared beliefs held by a group of observers about what is and isn't possible in the world, formed by previous Consensuses and defining the bounds of the permissible for future commits.

Key property: the Paradigm does not filter results (post factum) but modifies the very attempts to initiate a commit (ante factum). An observer within a Paradigm does not even try to step beyond its bounds — their perception is already integrated into it.

The Paradigm as a Commit Predictor

The central insight of QCT 2.0: the Paradigm is not only a filter but a predictor of commit directionality. It shapes the vector of commits so that they never land in Paradox territory.

Consequence: 99.999999% of Quorums end with normal events, with no Normalization whatsoever. The world is stable because everyone plays by the same rules — and doesn't even suspect that the rules can be broken.

Example: 10 people in a Quorum, all with a rationalist Paradigm. Even if one has maximally high CP, they will unconsciously aim for the permissible. They will be phenomenally lucky — but within the physically explainable.

Scales

A Paradigm can be local (a group, a subculture), regional (urban, military), or global (civilizational). An observer simultaneously exists within multiple Paradigms, and their commits are shaped by the intersection of these.

Paradigm Shifts

Paradigms change. Major historical shifts:

  • Mythological Paradigm: miracles are the norm. Everyone believes in the same thing.
  • Religious: miracles are possible, but localized (through saints, prophets).
  • Scientific: miracles are impossible. Any anomaly receives a rational explanation.

A Paradigm shift is a meta-Paradox. Always accompanied by turbulence: wars, revolutions, philosophical crises. Chaos = the absence of an active Paradigm, a rise in the probability of Paradoxes.

The Paradigm and Miracles

In 1200 AD: "Fell ill — cursed." Recovery through prayer is natural, no Paradox.

In 2025: "Fell ill — virus." The same outcome through prayer triggers a Paradox, Normalized through "diagnostic error," "placebo."

Miracles haven't disappeared. They've ceased to be perceived as miracles. CP can create any anomaly, but if the Paradigm is adapted, no one will notice.

The world doesn't hide miracles — it doesn't permit their conscious recognition at the level of intention. Not because it's forbidden — but because it occurs to no one to try.

Self-Limitation

Belief in the possibility of a miracle = a reduction in the Event-Paradigm gap = less load on Normalization. The skeptic brakes their own reality: their Paradigm blocks the direction of commits before initiation.

The Paradigm as Inertia

The Paradigm possesses historical weight. It ensures stability, protects the system from minor Paradoxes. But it can be rewritten by an observer with sufficient CP — a prophet, an ideologue, a leader of a new era. This is the mechanism of epochal change.

The Paradigm is the quantum inertia of cultural perception. It explains the stability of the macroworld, the absence of visible miracles, the persistence of mass beliefs, and allows the introduction of sociocultural variables into a physical model without mysticism.
