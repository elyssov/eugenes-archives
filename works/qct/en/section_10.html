Chapter 10. The Evolution of Consciousness and Quorum Dynamics

From Mythic Thinking to Scientific

In the early stages of civilization, thinking is mythic. People believe not in models but in personified meanings. Fire has a spirit, misfortune has a cause, lightning has the will of Zeus.

What does this mean in QCT terms?

The Quorum of observers, even with a small number of participants, has high cognitive coherence. Everyone believes in the same thing, with almost no internal conflicts. "God is wrathful" — and everyone in the village feels this as truth, not as hypothesis.

This amplifies resonance. Even with low individual CP, the quorum operates as a phased beam — converges, resonates, splashes a "miracle" into reality.

The Scientific Revolution and the Collapse of Coherence

Then rationality enters the stage. The scientific revolution, analytics, mathematics, objectivism.

Consciousness begins to fragment. People no longer look at the world as the will of gods. They calculate, measure, model.

What does this yield in QCT terms?

The aggregate CP of consciousness grows — but resonance vanishes. Observers are increasingly misaligned. Their quorums become noisy, contradictory, and Consensus assembles with enormous inertia.

One sees a miracle. Another sees an optical illusion. A third sees a fake. And the system simply records: "rollback and normalization."

The Paradox of Progress

The smarter we become, the fewer miracles we see.

Paradoxical? No. Simply the physics of observers.

The Scientific Paradigm blocks not only the interpretation of miracles — it blocks the very initiation of commits directed beyond the bounds of the "permissible." Science paradoxifies miracles: an event that in the Mythological Paradigm would be a "sign" is rejected by the quorum at the formation stage in the Scientific one.

And what happens when a local quorum hits a Paradox generated by the Scientific Paradigm? It normalizes — through a "rational explanation." But if the Paradox was real, traces remain: déjà vu, Mandela, the sensation of a "glitch."

The Turning Point

This is not the end. QCT asserts: if the scientific mind regains coherence — its CP will become truly monstrous.

If consciousness recovers its alignment — not through ritual, but through conscious management of the quantum vector of will — we will obtain a reality in which miracles are once again possible. But no longer as "divine will" — rather as engineering practice.

This is the hypothetical era of quantum operators: observers who consciously manage quorums and their own CP.

Conclusions

In antiquity, miracles were the consequence of primitive but coherent faith.

In the Middle Ages — the result of localized hierarchies of faith.

Today — everything is diffuse. Only residual artifacts leak through.

Tomorrow? Perhaps — an era when we understand how CP is built, how the vector of will is controlled, and how coherent quorums are engineered.
