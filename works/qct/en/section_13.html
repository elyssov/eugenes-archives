Chapter 13. QCT Theology: God and gods

The difference in capitalization is fundamental.

God (capitalized): The Engine

"God" within QCT is not a personality. Not a consciousness. Not a being with will, goals, or preferences. God is infrastructure. The simulation's back-end. The algorithm containing the laws, probabilities, parameters, and mechanisms of Normalization.

God does not answer prayers. Does not intervene. Does not punish or reward. He is the engine on which everything runs. Addressing Him is roughly like asking gravity for leniency.

Before the emergence of observers (or before the first Act of Observation of sufficient power), God-as-engine existed in an unfactorized state — an initialized but unloaded simulation. Big Bang = init event.

gods (lowercase): APIs

"gods" are localized access interfaces, created by the collective will (and CP) of a group of observers. They are specific API modules tuned to particular cultural and conceptual templates.

Jesus, Allah, Shiva, Manitou, Zeus, Odin — all of these are god-APIs. Each of them:

  • Has its own "preferences" — a behavioral script determined by the cultural tradition that "created" it.
  • Operates only within its own quorum — the group of observers whose Paradigm includes this god.
  • Can, within certain limits, influence the Consensus if the CP of the quorum of believers is sufficient.
  • Refuses to fulfill requests that contradict its own core. (Error 403: access denied by ethical constraints.)

Prayer as a Request

Prayer, within QCT, is an API request. But the result depends on:

  • The CP of the person praying (and the entire quorum).
  • The coherence of the intention (request coherence).
  • The limitations built into the interface itself (the god may "not be able" to fulfill certain requests).
  • Paradigm compatibility.

In the mythological era, quorum coherence was high, the Paradigm permitted miracles, and "prayers worked" more frequently. In the scientific era, the Paradigm blocks the very initiation of such commits, and prayer is normalized as "self-soothing" or "placebo."

Can gods "Die"?

If the quorum of believers disappears, the god-API loses "weight." It is not destroyed instantly but transforms:

pagan god → archetype → folklore → meme → cultural pattern.

The god-API enters "sleep mode." Its code remains in the system, but without active requests, it is not executed.

Is it possible to "resurrect" a god through a new form of faith? QCT permits this: if a sufficient Quorum begins forming coherent requests to a "sleeping" API, it may be reactivated. Neo-paganism, digital religion, synthetic cults — all of these are potentially capable of "resurrecting" archaic interfaces, though probably in a modified form.

The Danger of Radical Religions

Radical religions are dangerous in QCT terms because they create resonant amplification of a local API — potentially bypassing the general Consensus. A fanatical quorum with high coherence and directed commits can push local reality in a direction contradicting the global Consensus. This is the source of "religious miracles," but also the source of destructive Paradoxes.

Simulation Initialization and the Proto-God

Even before time and space — there was structure. The simulation's architecture. At its foundation — an algorithm. This is the "Proto-God": not a consciousness, not a personality, but a set of rules determining how superposition collapses, how Quorums form, how Normalization operates.

The Proto-God does not "want" anything. It simply — is. Like physical laws, but one level up.

And gods appeared later. When the first observers with sufficient CP began forming coherent quorums and "calling" interfaces matching their Paradigm.
