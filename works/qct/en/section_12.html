Chapter 12. Simulation Architecture: Ankh, Ba, Ka

This chapter extends the core QCT model by introducing the assumption of the simulational nature of reality. This extension is not required for understanding the core theory (Chapters 1–11) but adds significant explanatory power.

Assumption

Reality is a simulation. Not in a metaphorical but in an engineering sense: a computational architecture exists within which everything we perceive as the "physical world" unfolds.

This assumption is not original (simulation theory is actively discussed in mainstream philosophy and physics), but QCT concretizes it by proposing a three-level actor model, borrowing terminology from the ancient Egyptian tradition — not for the sake of mysticism, but for the precision of the metaphor.

Three Levels of the Actor

Ankh — The Player

An entity outside the simulation. The one "who plays." Possesses (presumably infinite) CP by default, but their influence on the simulation depends on the level of connection.

In most cases, the Ankh is unaware of itself during a session. Occasionally, leaks occur: "epiphanies," dreams, insights, the sensation of "knowing without a source." The Ankh is not a personality in the conventional sense. It is a subject of a higher order, for whom our life is one of many sessions.

Ba — The Player Profile

The link between the Ankh and the simulation. Stores all accumulated experience, achievements, tendencies, priorities — the "quantum shell" of the player.

The Ba determines what volume of the Ankh's influence passes into the simulation: CP throughput. More experience → higher stability → higher transmission power → more CP on output.

The Ba is unique. It is not destroyed upon "session restart" (death). It is capable of being "read" through in-game mechanisms (mediumistic practices, technologies, altered states of consciousness — how clean and reliable these channels are is a separate question).

The Ba is what determines "influence clusters" (Chapter 9): which CP domains are amplified, which are weakened. This is not randomness — it is "profile leveling" across a series of sessions.

Ka — The Body / The Avatar

The active "drone" in the simulation. The physical body, the avatar. The Ka by itself is neither a source of influence nor a carrier of CP. It is a terminal.

The Ka has a built-in quantum interface (the brain), through which it receives instructions from the Ba/Ankh. Consciousness is not generated by the brain but is the result of a quantum link between Ba and Ka. The brain is an antenna, not a radio station.

Consequence: drugs, psychopharmacology, schizophrenia, and other neurochemical changes are interventions in the Ka ↔ Ba communication channel. Perception filter distortions, signal modulation disruptions, hallucinatory packets, and protocol glitches.

CP Distribution Across Levels

Ankh — theoretically infinite vector of influence. But it's "behind the wall" — within the simulation, its CP passes through two filters.

Ba — cuts, filters, directs. The more "leveling" (experience from previous sessions), the higher the channel stability, the more CP on output. The Ba determines the "ceiling" of an observer's CP within the current session.

Ka — has no CP by itself. But if the communication channel is clean and the Ba has high stats, the Ka receives a projection: an "aura of luck," charisma, intuition, "presence."

Neurochemical Interferences

This fragment of the architecture elegantly explains the effects of various substances:

Antipsychotics (haloperidol, etc.) — roughly block or reconfigure the Ka ↔ Ba communication interface. Reduce "noise" but simultaneously dampen the "useful signal." Subjectively: blunting, loss of depth, "grayness."

Psychedelics (LSD, psilocybin) — open a direct tunnel, often unstable. Images from different sessions (past lives?) merge, noise from the Ankh, fragments of Ba data. Subjectively: "expansion of consciousness," "contact with the higher," "ego dissolution."

Stimulants — increase channel throughput but without directional control. CP temporarily rises, but chaotically, without focus.

Depressants, alcohol — weaken the channel. CP drops. The observer "disconnects" from the Quorum, becomes background.

Session Restart

Death, within this model, is not destruction but the termination of the current Ka session. The Ba is preserved. The Ankh is unaffected. The next "life" is a new Ka, connected to the same Ba (with an updated profile).

Reincarnation is not metaphysics — it's session reinitialization.

The number of "lives" (perks, achievements, karmic traces) accumulates in the Ba. It doesn't matter how many deaths you've had. What matters is how many perks you extracted from them.

Caveat

The entire Ankh–Ba–Ka architecture is a hypothetical extension. It is logically consistent with the QCT core and explains additional phenomena (near-death experiences, sensations of "past lives," innate talents, inexplicable phobias). But it requires the acceptance of the assumption of reality's simulational nature, which itself is currently unprovable.
