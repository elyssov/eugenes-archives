Chapter 5. Quorum and the Mechanism of Consensus

Quorum: Composition and Dynamics

A Quorum is the totality of all observers involved in a specific event at the moment of its resolution. It is the Quorum that determines which of the potential superposition outcomes will be realized.

The composition of a Quorum is not fixed. It forms dynamically: every observer whose Act of Observation encompasses the given event is automatically included in the Quorum.

Local and Global Quorums

Local Quorum — an event within a narrow situation: a shop queue, a battle, a lottery drawing. The number of participants is small, and each observer's CP has an immediate and pronounced effect. This is where one person's "luck" is directly observable.

Global Quorum — the scale of geopolitics, history, collective beliefs. Millions of observers participate. An individual CP's influence dissolves in the mass, but aggregate vectors form trends, cultural paradigms, retrospective normalization. The Global Quorum determines the "background" of reality.

Between them exists an interface zone. When a local event attracts the attention of a Global Quorum (through media, culture, ideology), an "interface collision" occurs: the Global Quorum begins influencing the interpretation and consequences of the local event, which can lead to enhanced Normalization or anomalous outcomes.

Mechanism of Consensus

Consensus is not an instantaneous discrete act but a continuous wave process:

1. A superposition arises — a multitude of potential outcomes.

2. A Quorum forms — the totality of involved observers.

3. Each CP contributes to the overall vector.

4. Vectors are summed accounting for weights and directions (intentions, expectations, emotional charges).

5. The resulting vector determines the realized outcome — the Consensus.

Consensus is not "chosen" by conscious voting. It is an automatic process in which the overwhelming majority of participants are unaware of their contribution.

Conflict Within a Quorum

When observers with high CP pull reality in different directions, a conflict of realities arises. QCT does not allow splitting — the conflict is resolved by Consensus with Normalization, through vector summation.

An exaggerated example: two roughly equal heavyweights enter a game of chance with opposite vectors. The most probable Consensus is a compromise outcome: roughly speaking, they'll split the jackpot approximately in half. Reality "cannot" satisfy both fully, but neither can it ignore either one.

In acute conflicts between heavyweights, a Paradox arises — a point of maximum tension in the probabilistic fabric. Either one "pushes through" reality (if their CP is significantly higher), or Normalization is triggered. One might hypothesize that heavyweight conflicts are "gravitational waves in the observer field": large-scale perturbations manifesting as "coincidences," "fateful events," or historical bifurcation points.

Actors with Low CP

Background observers do not determine eventfulness — they merely confirm an already-formed Consensus. In a Global Quorum, they constitute the overwhelming majority. Their aggregate contribution creates the "inertia of reality" — a stable background, extremely difficult to shift.

This explains the persistence of mass beliefs, even false ones: the enormous aggregate CP of background observers, aligned in one direction, creates a Consensus that no individual heavyweight can budge.

Empty Observers

Completely passive observers — apathetic, detached — "stay silent in the Quorum." Their CP doesn't vanish but is not realized in directed commits. Open question: does the unrealized CP of passive observers drain into the systemic background — a quantum analogue of "dark energy of observers"?
