Chapter 15. Falsifiability: A Stress Test by Popper

The Criterion

According to Karl Popper, a scientific theory must be falsifiable: there must exist a possible (though not yet realized) experience that could disprove it. A theory that "explains everything" in fact explains nothing.

QCT is aware of this danger and addresses it directly.

The Problem: "Spider and the Wardrobe Syndrome"

The main criticism: if any event can be interpreted through "the consensus decided so," then the theory is unfalsifiable. One sees luck — "high CP." Another sees bad luck — "lost the Quorum." Everything is explained, which means nothing is explained.

QCT's response: this is true for the theory's current state as an explanatory model. But unfalsifiability is not a permanent sentence. Many scientific models passed through a stage where the tools for their verification did not yet exist. The Schrödinger equation in 1930 also lacked full experimental confirmation — but its predictive power became evident later.

QCT at this stage is an explanatory model, analogous to the early heliocentric system: it doesn't predict better, but it explains more simply and logically.

Potential Directions for Falsification

1. Analysis of Anomalous Probabilities

If CP exists, statistically significant deviations from expected probability distributions should be observable in the presence of certain observers. Specifically: in controlled conditions, the same probabilistic experiment (coin toss, random number generator) should yield statistically significant deviations with "heavy" observers compared to a control group.

If no deviations are found under any conditions — this is an argument against QCT.

Note: similar experiments were conducted within the PEAR program (Princeton Engineering Anomalies Research) and showed weak but statistically significant deviations. Interpretation of these results remains disputed.

2. Retrospective Verification of Historical Events

If Historical Normalization is real, "stratifications" in the interpretation of events should be discoverable, correlating with changes in the composition and CP of the global Quorum. Specifically: events well-documented by multiple independent sources should resist reinterpretation; poorly documented ones should undergo it more frequently.

3. Laboratory Attempts to "Boost" CP

If CP depends on awareness, focus, and emotional involvement, it should be possible to experimentally amplify a subject's influence through meditation, hypnosis, or group coherence. Controlled experiments: a meditating group vs. a control group, a random number generator, statistical analysis.

4. Correlation Between Group Coherence and Anomalous Outcomes

If Quorum coherence amplifies the effect, group events with high emotional alignment (rituals, concerts, mass prayers) should correlate with anomalies in random number generators.

Note: The Global Consciousness Project (GCP) at Princeton University has been collecting such data since 1998. Results are ambiguous but statistically interesting.

5. Correlation Between Belief/Confidence and Probabilistic Outcomes

A direct test: does an observer's subjective confidence in an outcome affect the statistics of actual results? A double-blind study measuring confidence levels and actual outcomes.

What Cannot Be Considered Refutation

  • A single case of a heavyweight's "bad luck" is not a refutation (CP is domain-specific; the result depends on the entire Quorum).
  • The absence of "flying people" is not a refutation (the Paradigm blocks the commit itself).
  • Non-reproducibility in laboratory conditions is not an automatic refutation (if conditions don't account for the Quorum composition and the experimenters' Paradigm).

The Danger of Unfalsifiable Grandiosity

QCT honestly acknowledges: if one forgets that this is still a theory at the intersection of philosophy and science — and begins using it as an "explanation for everything" — it transforms into "a wish book on quantum steroids."

The line between "an explanatory model with potential" and "mysticism in a lab coat" is thin. QCT stays on the right side exactly as long as it:

  • Does not refuse criticism.
  • Does not claim engineering status without an experimental basis.
  • Proposes specific directions for verification (listed above).

Popper's Verdict

Falsifiability — with caveats — exists. Through analysis of anomalous probabilities, retrospective verification, laboratory experiments with coherent groups.

The danger of unfalsifiable grandiosity — exists. If one forgets that this is still a hypothesis.

And for now — it is a model, a philosophical-scientific system, explaining what before it was explained only by shamans and Hawking, through different hemispheres of the brain.
