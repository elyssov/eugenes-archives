Chapter 16. Open Questions

QCT is a living theory. A number of questions remain without definitive answers and represent directions for further development.

1. Who or What Determines CP?

If observers have different CP — who/what determines this "weight"? We speak of cognitive density, awareness, social topology — but where is the "counting center"?

Options:

  • Within the simulation model: the Ba (player profile) determines the CP ceiling; the current state of the Ka and context determine realization.
  • Outside the simulation model: CP is an automatic projection of observer activity, requiring no external "auditor." Just as gravity requires no "controller" — mass curves space by default.

Can an observer assess their own CP? Probably only indirectly — by the world's reaction: "the blind person feels their strength by the echo."

2. Passive CP Loss

Can CP decline without internal conflict? The observer doesn't doubt, doesn't fear — but their CP decreases. Possible causes:

  • Deprivation (isolation from Quorums).
  • Normalization by the global Quorum "against" them.
  • Conflicting API: the observer appeals to one system (a god-API), but the global Consensus has already been reconfigured to another.

Does "passive zeroing" exist despite active will? An open question.

3. The Nature of Influence Clusters

Are clusters (CP domains) an objective metric or a convenient model?

  • Can they be sharply delineated? Likely not — the boundaries are gradient, not discrete.
  • What determines them? Cognitive structure? Ba experience? Resonance between subject and probabilistic fabric?
  • Are there internal barriers between clusters?

Most likely — clusters are determined by a combination of internal predisposition, external context, and Quorum dynamics.

4. Heavyweight Conflict

When two observers with high CP pull in opposite directions — what happens? QCT does not allow splitting (we're not in the many-worlds interpretation). Options:

  • Compromise Consensus (vector summation).
  • Paradox with subsequent Normalization.
  • Domination by one with sufficient CP advantage.

Can heavyweight conflict be called "gravitational waves in the observer field"? As a metaphor — it works. As physics — still speculation.

5. Empty Observers and "Dark Energy"

Completely passive observers "stay silent in the Quorum." Their CP is not realized. But where does it go?

Hypothesis: the unrealized CP of passive observers drains into the systemic background — a quantum analogue of "dark energy of observers." If so, then mass apathy is not neutral — it forms "background pressure" that reinforces the inertia of the current Consensus.

This would explain why "nothing happens" in societies with high levels of apathy — not because nobody is trying, but because the background CP of passive observers pushes toward the status quo.

6. The Interface Between Local and Global Quorums

When a local event attracts global attention (through media, viral content), how does the "interface collision" occur?

  • The Global Quorum begins influencing the interpretation and consequences of the local event.
  • Could this lead to "super-normalization" (the event is erased from memory)?
  • Or to an anomaly (the event "jams" between two levels of Consensus)?

Examples: high-profile murders, terrorist attacks, viral incidents — all pass through this interface.

7. Cameras, Recordings, AI — Are They Observers?

A camera does not possess CP. But it is a potential conduit for a future Act of Observation (when the recording is viewed). Is AI an observer in the QCT sense? Does it possess CP?

If AI ever reaches the state we've called "cognitive singularity," it may become an observer with CP. But this lies at the boundary of current understanding.

8. Can CP Be "Engineered"?

The central applied question. If CP depends on awareness, focus, and coherence — is "will engineering" possible? The creation of technologies for:

  • Measuring CP (even indirectly).
  • Amplifying CP (through training, meditation, neurofeedback technologies).
  • Forming coherent Quorums (conscious groups with directed commits).

This is the path from philosophical model to engineering one. The path from QCT-as-theory to QCT-as-practice. For now — open.

9. Dark Matter and Dark Energy

QCT does not yet provide a complete explanation for these cosmological phenomena, but proposes a direction: if the unrealized CP of passive observers creates "background pressure," could dark energy be the macroscopic manifestation of the aggregate unrealized CP of all observers in the simulation?

Dark matter — a "framework" ensuring the simulation's structural integrity? Or "computational resources," distributed but not engaged?

Speculative — but elegant.

10. What Lies Beyond the Rabbit Hole?

This text describes reality from the inside — through the eyes of observers. But if reality is a simulation, then what lies beyond?

Who are the Ankhs? What is their "reality"? Is it, in turn, a simulation? Is recursion possible — a simulation within a simulation?

QCT offers no answer. But it formulates the question — and that is already no small thing.

We are not merely passengers of existence. We are parts of the algorithm for negotiating reality. But not all observers are equal. And if we understand how CP influences, how it is built, and how coherent quorums are formed — we will step beyond blind chance. Ahead lies scientific magnetism. Quantum psychophysics. Conscious probability modeling. And for now — listen. Perhaps you can already feel how the Quorum bends to your choice.
