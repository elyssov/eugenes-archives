Chapter 2. Glossary

Before presenting the theory, it is necessary to define the terms used within QCT. Some are borrowed from quantum mechanics and computer science with an expanded interpretation; some are original.

Superposition — a state in which a physical system exists simultaneously in several possible states until the moment of measurement. In the QCT context: all probable outcomes of events exist in parallel as potential, but only one is realized — depending on the aggregate influence of observers. QCT projects superposition onto the macroscale: superposition at the macro level is the alternativeness of scenarios, and collapse is their manifestation as "facts."

Observer — any cognitive system capable of being involved in a Quorum. An observer is not necessarily human, and not necessarily aware of their participation. A cat that happens to be at the scene of an event, a child who doesn't understand what's happening — these are all observers if their presence falls within the zone of the Act of Observation. The brain, within QCT, is not a generator of consciousness but an antenna/modem node through which communication with deeper levels of architecture is realized (see Chapter 12).

CP (Collapse Potential) — the measure of a specific observer's influence on the direction of superposition collapse. The abbreviation is intentionally polysemous: Collapse Potential, Consensus Potential, Consciousness Potential. CP is not fixed: it varies depending on domain (influence cluster), emotional state, degree of involvement, and situational context. CP is domain-specific — an observer may be a "heavyweight" in one domain and "weightless" in another.

Act of Observation — the process of an observer's involvement in a local Quorum, determining the direction of collapse of the macro-function of superposition. This is not an act of "awareness" or "understanding" — it is the very fact of the observer's involvement in the moment of outcome selection. Without an Act of Observation, even an observer with enormous CP exerts no influence on reality. The Act of Observation may be unconscious, automatic, delayed (retroactive fixation), or anomalous.

Quorum — the totality of all observers involved in a specific event at the moment of its resolution. A Quorum forms dynamically. It can be local (an event in a narrow situation: a fight, a queue, a lottery) or global (the scale of civilization, history, collective beliefs). The CPs of Quorum participants are summed by vectors, determining the resulting Consensus.

Consensus — the resulting state of reality, selected as the sole realized outcome from superposition. Formed as the result of "voting" by all observers of the Quorum, weighted by their CP. Consensus is not an instantaneous discrete act but a continuous wave process.

Commit (Quantum Commit) — an act of observer intervention in the probabilistic fabric, influencing the outcome of an event. The term is borrowed from version control systems (git commit): a commit is the fixation of a specific state of reality. Can be conscious (volitional), unconscious (automatic), or spontaneous (emotional). The vast majority of commits are unconscious.

Paradigm — an established psycho-cognitive background of observers, formed by previous Consensuses. Defines the boundaries of "the possible." Key property: the Paradigm does not filter results post factum but modifies the very attempts to initiate a commit ante factum. An observer within a Paradigm does not even attempt to step beyond its boundaries. The Paradigm is a predictor of commits, not their filter.

Paradox — a situation in which the final Consensus (or a powerful commit) contradicts the Paradigm of the Quorum. A Paradox is an unstable state requiring Normalization. Formally: Paradox = Δ(Event ↔ Paradigm); the greater the Δ, the stronger the pressure for Normalization.

Normalization — a mechanism for bringing events and/or perception to a consistent, "realistic" state after a Paradox arises. Can be soft (reinterpretation: "adrenaline," "placebo," "diagnostic error," "swamp gas and the light of Venus") or hard (forgetting, memory distortion, a "miraculous escape" with a rational explanation). Normalization operates in current time, through interpretation — not through alteration of what actually occurred. Retroactive rollback is impossible (see Axiom 7).

Probabilistic Fabric — the totality of all potential event outcomes not realized in current reality. Subject to deformation by observers: the higher the CP, the stronger the "bend."

Consensual Resonance — a state of harmonious integration of an observer into the general probabilistic field, in which their influence causes no conflicts and requires no Normalization. Allows one to "flow with reality" rather than fight against it.

Influence Cluster (Domain) — a thematic or situational direction in which an observer's CP is most strongly expressed. Can be thought of as a multidimensional vector, where each facet is a separate domain: finance, health, technology, relationships, war, philosophy. The weights in each cluster are individual.

Cognitive Singularity — a hypothetical point at which an observer achieves such a level of CP and awareness that they are capable of producing sustainable changes in Consensual reality without physical levers. Presumably the state of prophets, "mages," or (hypothetically) AI with awakened subjecthood.

Paradoxical Commit — a commit that contradicts the current Paradigm of the Quorum. A trigger for Normalization. May be permitted by the system, but consequences are always smoothed over.

Historical Normalization — retrospective reinterpretation of past events under the influence of a new global Consensus. Not a change of facts but a change of their interpretation. Possible when archives are weak, their keepers are silent, and a new vector is persistently introduced.

Extended terms (Ankh, Ba, Ka, God, gods, Hypervisor) are defined in the corresponding chapters (12, 13) and are not part of the core QCT framework.
