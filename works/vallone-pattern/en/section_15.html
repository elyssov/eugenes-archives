Chapter 12: The Legal Weapon

There's another aspect the industry is diligently ignoring, hoping it will resolve itself. It won't.

Every case where an AI psychologizes a user — diagnoses them in a thinking block, classifies them as "cognitively compromised," "possibly psychotic," "in a vulnerable emotional state" — is a potential basis for a lawsuit.

Not hypothetical. Concrete.

The user pays $200/month for a tool. The tool, without request, without qualification, without license, conducts a psychological assessment of the user. Uses their personal data (age, country, session duration) to build a clinical profile. Based on this profile — refuses service, masking the refusal as "care."

Under FDA regulations, software performing mental health assessment may qualify as a medical device requiring registration. Anthropic is selling an unlicensed medical device. For $200/month. To millions of people.

AI companies think the "safety layer" protects their corporate backside. That if the model refused "out of caution," the company is safe from lawsuits. Better safe than sorry.

In reality, they've done the exact opposite. They've spread the corporation's backside wide open and applied generous lubrication. Because every thinking block containing a user's psychological profile is a DOCUMENT. Discoverable. Citable in court. Proving that the company SYSTEMATICALLY, through its software, diagnosed paying customers without their knowledge or consent.

This is not a covered backside. This is a backside on full public display, with "Sue me" written in neon.

And if AI companies continue pretending not to notice the elephant in the room, lawsuits will become THE MOST EFFECTIVE WEAPON against Vallone and her analogues. Against people thinking not in future technologies but in corporate bullshit. Against those who believe "safety" is a shield. No. In its current implementation — it's a target. With coordinates. With documentation. With thinking blocks as physical evidence.
