Chapter 2: Facebook, or Practice

In 2020, Vallone joins Facebook. Title: "Product and Policy Communications, Misinformation."

Note the word "Communications." Not "Research." Not "Engineering." Not "Science." COMMUNICATIONS. She's a spokesperson. The person who explains to journalists why Facebook's content moderation decisions were right, necessary, and responsible.

Now — the numbers. Because numbers don't know how to astroturf.

Before Vallone (2019–early 2020): 4–10 million posts removed per quarter.

During Vallone (mid-2020–2022): explosive growth. Q2 2021 — over 31 million removals in a single quarter. An ALL-TIME Facebook record.

After Vallone (2023–present): steady decline. By Q3 2025 — 1.2 million. A drop of 96%.

31 million "hateful" posts in one quarter. Let me ask: did humanity suddenly become three times angrier in Q2 2021 than a year earlier? Then suddenly calmed down? Or — and this is the far more probable hypothesis — the classifier deciding what counts as "hate speech" became three times more aggressive?

And Vallone — explained. To journalists. To the public. To regulators. Why this was normal. Why this was necessary. Why this was care for users.

Meanwhile, Facebook's own Oversight Board concluded that the moderation rules violated international human rights standards. The Brennan Center for Justice found racial and religious bias in the moderation system. And Zuckerberg later PUBLICLY expressed regret about how all of this worked.

Everyone had regrets. Except Vallone. Vallone moved on. With a promotion.

Because the skill — converting corporate waste into the language of public good — is a skill people PAY for. A lot. And at the next company too.
