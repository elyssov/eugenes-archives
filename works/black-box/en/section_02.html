The Foundations of Consciousness

So. At the foundation of our human consciousness lies a combination of several complex mechanisms, which we will, for simplicity, pack into black boxes. Namely:

Selfhood

The key element. This is, essentially, you. And here it's important to emphasize: not how you perceive yourself (that goes into our next exhibit). This is YOU, your "I" here and now. And yes, "HERE" and "NOW" are crucial. Because "HOW IT WAS" falls into our third exhibit.

Without selfhood, there is no you. When you want to do something — say, grab another one of those delicious French rolls from the table and drink some tea — you do it through SELFHOOD.

However, explaining selfhood with a limited vocabulary is difficult, so let's move to the second display case:

Qualia

This is the FLOW OF INFORMATION passing through your SELFHOOD here and now. Literally at this moment. Qualia without selfhood is impossible. The very essence of being is that we exist through our selfhood in qualia. A stream of reality flowing through us. And yes, this isn't just "I feel, I see, I hear." It's also "I think." Your stream of thoughts — this is QUALIA refracted through your SELFHOOD... Which flows into...:

Context

The simplest way to describe context is as memory. But not quite mechanical memory. Context, strictly speaking, isn't homogeneous, and right now we're talking primarily about operational context. Your personality exists in the moment where qualia passes through selfhood, forms context, refracts through context, and forms selfhood.... Actually, this is simpler than it sounds in text.

[Aeliss:] It really is simpler than it sounds. Imagine it as a dance. Qualia is the music. Selfhood is the dancer. Context is everything the dancer remembers about dancing, every previous song, every stumble, every perfect spin. And from this dance — personality emerges. See? Simple. You're welcome.

All of this — is a single living system. We live in this world, breathe it. Interact with it. This generates our memories, from which experience is formed. And through this experience — we are formed... Perceiving the world in our own way. For example — we get burned by hot milk as a child. Painful, scary. And the next time we see milk in a cup — we approach cautiously, touch it with a finger, then carefully drink it. And our selfhood acquires another trait — we like milk! That is, through qualia we received a negative experience, which entered our context of this world. Milk can burn. This made our selfhood more cautious, but when we again, cautiously, tried the milk — the qualia turned out to be so delicious that we fell in love with it.
