Blood of the Earth

From my chest came not even a moan, but a wheeze. In the dry and hot atmosphere of Celestia, running was not advised. Catching my breath as best I could and taking a swallow from my flask, I listened. Many soft steps of bare Arcadian paws, the jingle of a well winch, melodic voices... The creak of leather and quiet whine of servomotors — I did not hear. Therefore — the Hunters had fallen behind. I shuddered: I was being hunted by my brothers in blood, by race. And all — because of the killing of an alien. Because of an alien I had failed to finish off.

At that moment I noticed with surprise that the ritual knife was still in my left hand. On the blade — a brown streak of Arcadian blood had caked. I shuddered again, a lump from my stomach rolled up into my throat itself, and I instinctively flung away my only weapon. The knife glittered in the merciless sun of Celestia with the groove of its "blood-flow," and then buried itself in the dust of the dried-up gutter running along the back yard of the adobe house I had hidden behind.

Apparently I had been so absorbed in contemplating the knife that I missed the appearance of a young female Arcadian. The creature was of medium height, slender, with a pretty little muzzle. On her pointed little face shone two enormous golden cat eyes. The sharp and long ears twitched tensely. However, the main sign that she was extremely flustered was the tail sticking out from under her short little skirt, which at that moment was nervously writhing, periodically lashing its mistress across her slender hips.

In the alien's hands was a schoolgirl's satchel.

— Oh! Hello! — the Arcadian blurted out at the sight of me. The next second, she shifted her gaze to the violet stain that had spread on my shirt, chest, chin, and partially shoulders.

The Mark.

— Oh, you're a criminal, aren't you?! — the alien froze for a second, her eyes wide open. At that moment, perhaps, she was very vulnerable... but my only weapon I had thrown away a couple of minutes earlier, and besides — I could not have raised my hand with the knife... again.

In any case, the moment was lost. The strong and slim legs of the teenage Arcadian kicked up dust, and my interlocutor was gone. Waiting for the Hunters' flyer to appear, I did not.

By the time my ears caught the faint whine of turbines — I had already taken shelter in an empty hovel a couple of blocks from the place of my encounter with the Arcadian.

Through the window one could see Arcadians scurrying about.

Noon was approaching, their time. No human in his right mind, except, perhaps, the Hunters, would dare to step outside.

I tipped the flask over my parched throat and shook it. Of course — not a drop rolled down. After a whole morning of running about, this was no surprise. The more so since my flask was not a march flask but a "promenade" one. I could only envy the Hunters' distil-suits.

Speaking of which, the sounds of the aforementioned Hunters' servomotors were drawing near. It seemed my escape was ending almost before it had properly begun. The "cyber-hounds" created specifically for them by the Arcadian scientists made it possible to track my molecular trail right to my hideout. And if in the morning I had managed to break away by plunging into a sewer canal and swimming along it for as long as my endurance held out — this time, for the sensitive device, I was in full view.

For a second I regretted having thrown away the knife. However — I quickly calmed down: what could I do with it? I certainly wouldn't cut myself. And as for fighting servants of the law clad in technical armour — find another fool.

It seemed time to surrender.

As if echoing my thoughts — the walls of the pitiful clay hut shattered from the blow of an armoured fist. In the opening appeared the massive figure of a Hunter, encased in white plastic plate armour.

— Citizen Kane, surrender without resistance. It is useless — you only risk harming yourself. You are simply not yourself. All necessary assistance will be rendered to you.

— I surrender, I surrender... — I raised my hands and stepped forward. The Hunter softly but inexorably placed an armoured hand on my shoulder and quickly scanned me with the other. Finding no weapons or anything reprehensible on me, he stepped back. From behind him appeared a woman in armour bearing the identifying marks of a military physician of the Third Legion.

— Recruit Kane, do you realise where you are?

— I do — I mumbled weakly.

— Do you feel ill, injured? — the woman, while speaking, was already examining me with a portable medtech. On the visor of her helmet, judging by the vague shadows, some data were being projected.

— No, ma'am, military physician.

— Very good. So our problem is merely of a psychological nature? Tell me, your family, I believe, arrived here from the Metropolis?

— Yes, ma'am.

— Aha. I see. And, I suppose, the relocation of former senator Ironhead — was connected with his convictions?

— Possibly, ma'am. Father never explained it in detail.

The physician returned the scanner to its belt slot and handed me a flask filled with life-giving liquid.

— Kane, remember — on this planet one must never be without water. Just as — one must not walk under this sun without reliable protection. You've already caught a decent dose. However — that we will cure. As for your problem — we will conduct full psychoconditioning on you and you will undergo Initiation once more.

— But-but-but... I doooon't want this... The blood... Madam military physician, I thought I could... But...

— Recruit, this is the Legio Imperia, not a young ladies' institute. And this is the frontier, not the Metropolis...

...To the beat of drums — I stepped onto the parade ground, as had been the case two weeks earlier. In my hand, just as then, the handle of the ritual "Ka-Bar" nestled comfortably. Only this time — the doubts and hesitations that had come to me from my cowardly father — were gone.

I calmly approached the slaves huddled in a cluster. According to tradition — only young Arcadians had been driven into the pen. My mind, cleared of unnecessary emotions and waverings, lazily noted that among the dozen of these parodies of human beings there was clearly a familiar muzzle. With a snap of my fingers — I ordered the young Arcadian female I had met when a fugitive criminal who had refused to slaughter a slave at the oath, to come forward. Back then she had been carrying her young mistress's backpack.

Naturally, she approached without resistance. The lawfulness and innate discipline of Arcadians, after we had conquered them, had played a cruel joke on them.

No less cruel a joke had fate played on this cat. She had handed in that me, full of liberal filth about the equality of all sentient beings and the absence of any superiority of humanity, into the hands of the Legion's specialists.

The specialists managed to help me. Well of course — how could I ever have thought that a human's hand could rise against his fellow, a human. Our destiny is to rule the Universe. We are future gods and the children of gods. The only reason my brothers pursued me — was to save me from myself.

All the more ironic was my gratitude to the one who had helped me return to the bosom of the Human Empire.

I took her by her long, lush braid of light-blue colour, ran a couple of strands through my fingers, feeling and savouring the silkiness of her hair, after which with a sharp motion I jerked her head back and cut her throat from the trachea to the spinal column.

The Arcadian girl, choking on blood, fell to her knees, threw her hands up to her throat slit by the perfectly honed knife, fell face-down and writhed in convulsions, drenching the sands of the parade ground with blood.

I raised my hands above my head. From my ritual knife, having tasted blood, that had passed with the Legion through the darkness of ages — hot blood dripped onto my face.

— To Thy glory, Caesar of Humanity! — I cried out at the top of my lungs, turning toward His Banner.

And the unified roar of my battle-brothers became a worthy response to me!
