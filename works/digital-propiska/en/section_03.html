Eugene has had a Google account for approximately twenty years. Two decades. That's longer than most marriages. That's longer than some of you reading this have been alive.

In those twenty years, Google has accumulated the following information about him:

— Every email he has sent or received since approximately 2006 — Every photograph he has taken, including multiple photographs of his passports, stored in Google Photos — Every search he has ever made — Every YouTube video he has ever watched — His precise geographic location, tracked continuously through Android, showing beyond any reasonable doubt that he lives in Nha Trang, Vietnam — His browsing history, his app usage, his sleep patterns (inferred from phone activity), his daily routines, the names of his Wi-Fi networks, the Bluetooth devices in his apartment

Google knows more about Eugene Lyssovsky than the FSB, the CIA, and his own mother combined. This is not hyperbole. This is the documented reality of what a twenty-year Google account contains.

And when this man — whose entire life is already inside Google's servers — tried to register as a developer to build software and sell it on Google Play, the system said: "Please provide proof of residential address."

He provided his Russian international passport. Not enough.

He provided a Vietnamese bank card in his own name. Not enough.
