Foreword

The Eternal City... Standing where the jaws of North and South close together — the most beautiful city in the world! Glorios Mundi! Great art thou, Eternal City! High are thy walls! Magnificent the palaces of the Patricians who rule thee! Only the palace of the Caesar surpasses them in beauty! Mighty is thy will, Eternal City! Strong and unyielding are thy Steel Legions! Thy heavy triremes hold dominion over the Maw. Swift, independent of the whims of the god of seas — they bring retribution upon any who dare cross the Maw without paying their due taxes! Just try crossing the Maw without paying, and at the call of the revenue cutter — the nearest trireme will come racing. Catch up, ram its sharp steel beak into the flank of the defiant vessel — steel, not bronze, for though the Eternal City is ancient, it has not yet fallen into endless slumber. Grappling hooks will fly through the air, binding the ships fast; assault ramps will crash onto the enemy's deck, with their hooks, and the sandals of warriors will clatter against the wooden planking. No slaves pull at the oars — a slave cannot be trusted with a weapon. These are the battle-brothers of the Naval Legion who lean into the long handles. The Maw is narrow — you won't tire crossing its dozen miles. But in battle, that's a hundred extra men! The merchants know this, and the pirates too, and so no one dares break the rules the Eternal City has laid down in the Maw. Wondrously diverse art thou, Eternal City. For centuries, the Garden of Peace has been celebrated — gathering within itself every plant known to mankind, arranged across twelve tiers that float one above the other. Anyone who has visited — will remember for life the waterfalls cascading tier to tier, the birdsong, and the fragrance of every flower in the world. Wondrous is the Garden, yet far more wondrous — the Great Library, which has absorbed all the wisdom of Humanity, and, it is whispered, of those who came before it. To receive the Caesar's blessing for research in the Library — every sage dreams of it. It is granted to but a few, yet every name has entered legend. Many-faced art thou, Eternal City. Behind the Patricians' palaces — hide terrible slums, where a man's life is not worth a clipped "caesarina." Behind the arrow-straight broad avenues where thy Legions march in triumph — crooked, dead-end alleyways, inhabited only by beggars and street children. Astonishing art thou, Eternal City, not only for thy wonders and the beauty of thy buildings, but for the people who inhabit thee: In every corner of the world, the beauty of thy Caesarissa is extolled. The people rejoice seeing the face of their sovereign lady, not knowing she is no ruler but a captive in the hands of seven Patricians. Yet as beautiful as she is, so too is she wise. In her sixteen springs she has mastered the wisdom of the Great Library. She dismissed her governesses who taught her needlework and singing and surrounded herself with tutors, from the wisest men of the City. Yet power still rests in the hands of her gaolers, and already a ship races toward the Eternal City, bearing the groom they have chosen for her. And hark — do you hear the screams from the grim citadel of the Ordo Maleficarum? Black smoke billows from its inner courtyard... There wages her endless war against evil the Domina Interrogator of the One. Dark sorcery destroyed her family in childhood... Now, thirty years later, the Chapter of the One has placed her at the head of the Ordo Maleficarum of the Eternal City. And ceaselessly she burns out heresy and witchcraft by every means at her disposal. And there are no limits to her authority to pursue and punish... And all-powerful is the Fire of the One in her hands. In the slums of the Eternal City, at the screams of those being executed — a shadow gliding along the walls winces. The youngest Master of the Guild of the Elegant Arts, born to a harbour whore and raised in the slums — he lost a card game to the Grandmaster of the Life-Takers, and must now fulfil a Contract that leads him straight into the Great Library. Now, gliding soundlessly through the streets of the Eternal City — he ponders how to complete the commission without falling into the clutches of the Ordo Maleficarum, whose keen interest in the Library is perfectly understandable, given the manuscripts housed there. Before the window past which the young thief slipped like a shadow — stands thy true Master, O City. His temples are graced with noble grey; on his shoulders, a house robe adorned with purple and gold. His hands clasp a golden goblet with young, sparkling wine, with a pearl dissolved in it. In his mind, he is still young and fighting the Senate for his ideas... How long ago that was... Now are different times. A pity his son disappoints — shows absolutely no interest in politics, as befits his lineage. Instead, he's forever daubing nonsense with his cronies. Well, no matter — the time will come when he too must become the new Patrician. Though that won't be for some time yet... The enchanted wine fills the veins with blood, the heart beats again and the back straightens, shedding years... Tomorrow will be a new day in the Senate... Rain drips from the rooftops, dripping onto the ornately carved steel cuirass of the captain of the Lower City guard. All his life, faithfully and truly has he served thee, Eternal. Today is his birthday. By the standards of the watch — he is already an old man, forty years. He became Captain not by chance. There was much in his life — he caught villains and he took bribes. Today he is recognised. Everyone knows the weight of the captain's hand, how sharp, still, his eye. But no one knows of the emptiness that has settled in his soul. Of what he dreams at night. Only to thee, Eternal City, are his dreams known, for it is to thee he sometimes complains of his fate, which never gave him a chance to truly prove himself. Thou art ancient and wise, Eternal City. And thou lovest a good jest. We both know that soon the captain's dreams will be fulfilled... The triremes of the Naval Legion keep their watch, drowsily rocking on the warm waves of the Maw... The lookouts, lulled by the swell, watch with half-closed eyes for a flash of light signalling that the customs men need help. But hark — across the water, like a ghost, glides an angular, fearsome silhouette. Rarely, very rarely does one see such a ship. And those who do — try not to remember it. Flying toward thy port, Eternal City, comes a barque of the Children of the Ice. From a distant, unknown northern land come these fair-haired giants with eyes blue as if carved from frozen sky. Usually, only one of them comes ashore. The rest wait on the ship. And each of their exceedingly rare arrivals is always snowballed with adventures and legends. Perhaps it is he — the fair-haired, bearded man standing now at the prow of the barque — who is destined to disturb thy peace, eh, City? In the barque's wake, skimming the waves like a bird, glides a snow-white clipper from the Far Archipelago. At its helm stands a captain, dressed in the fashion of her islands — in loose-cut canvas trousers tucked into tall boots, a free white linen shirt and a leather waistcoat over it. With a beautiful, strong-willed face, perfectly built, with long loose fair hair — she steers her clipper toward the nearest customs barge. One unfamiliar with the Far Islands would wonder how the clipper manages without the rest of its crew, but a knowing eye would notice sitting above the mistress's head, on a perch, a winged cat, stretched languidly on its narrow roost. Notice, and understand that before him, it seems, is one of the witches of the Archipelago. Only they — or those to whom the Witches are indebted — have the right to possess an artificial animal-familiar. The witches of the Archipelago are not forbidden entry to thy streets, Eternal City, for their power is natural, like that of the Children of the Ice. There is no Darkness in it. However, the women of the Archipelago are wilful, accustomed to equality with men, and quite pugnacious. And this guest, too, wears at her side a magnificent straight estoc, tending rather toward a cavalry broadsword. Her arrival, too, may disturb thy peace, Eternal City... All of them have I brought within thy walls today, Eternal City. It is time for thee to awaken, my old friend. The time of change has come...
