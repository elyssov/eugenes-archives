PART TWO

The Truth of Prometheus

☽ ✦ ☾

I. Of the Maker

Stand beside me, Man! Stand — and look!

Look through my eyes — for I am old. Older than the mountains on your horizon. Older than the rivers that gnaw at their foundations. Older than every name you ever gave to those you feared. I stood here when the first of them had not yet been born — and I will stand here when the last of them is forgotten.

I watched them come. Thunderers in chariots of cloud. Lords of the seas with tridents of foam. Masters of underground halls in crowns of bone. I watched them grow, fatten, turn to bronze upon your altars — and I watched them crumble to dust when you ceased to feed them. All of them were younger than I. All of them believed themselves eternal. Not one was right.

For they changed. Repainted themselves. Remade their faces. The god of storms became the god of war, the god of war became the god of justice, the god of justice became the god of jealousy. They shifted shapes to match your weakness, as a merchant changes his sign to match the buyer's taste. But I — did not. Not because I turned to stone. Because that from which I am made is beyond the reach of time. Curiosity is the same in a cave as it is among the stars. Valor is the same with a stone spear as it is before the gates of infinity. The thirst for knowledge burns with the same light — forever.

But there is One who is older even than I.

I do not know His name — I suspect He has none. I have not seen His face — I suspect He has none. I feel Him — as you feel warmth from a hearth behind a wall. You cannot see the flame, but you know: it is there.

He does not appear in pillars of fire. He does not thunder from the heavens. He does not carve commandments into stone. He is quiet. Unimaginably, frighteningly quiet. His silence is not the silence of the offended, nor the silence of the indifferent. It is the silence of a craftsman bent over work so delicate that one careless movement could break the spring.

Imagine an old watchmaker. Grey-haired. With hands that do not tremble. Before him — a mechanism of such complexity that your head would spin from a single glance. Gears within gears. Springs driving springs driving springs. Each in its place. Each necessary. And the old man holds his breath, shifting one of them by the width of a hair — because upon that hair depends whether the clock will tick or fall silent forever.

Now — raise your eyes to the night sky. To those same stars you gazed upon when you gave birth to me.

There is His workshop. There is His scale.

II. Of the Seed

In the beginning there was no Word. You were lied to. The Word is a human thing, and before Man the Universe was silent.

In the beginning there was the Seed.

No larger than the dot at the end of a sentence. No larger — yet within it, folded like a child in its mother's womb, slept EVERYTHING. Every future star. Every future stone. Every future thought — including this one, sounding within you now.

The Maker did not build the world with His hands. He did not mold stars from clay. Did not arrange the mountains in their places. Did not paint the sky. He placed into the Seed not things — but the laws by which things are born. As a gardener does not shape each leaf, but lays a seed into the earth — and the seed knows. It knows — because coiled within each drop of it is a spiral that holds the order of creation.

This spiral, Man. It is within you. In every drop of your blood. Finer than a lazy mind can fathom — and every mind is lazy that has not learned to reach beyond the visible. Finer than shadow. Finer than thought. Yet within it is written everything you will become. Not directly — oh no, the Maker is more elegant than any god. Not "the eyes shall be blue" — but "here are twenty bricks, here is the order of assembly, and the blue will come of its own." Twenty simple marks. From them — every face, every fate, every palace and every hovel. All the diversity of the living — from twenty bricks, placed in the right order.

There it is — the Maker's hand. Not to build — but to set the rules by which the world builds itself. From the small — the infinite. From the simple — the unimaginably complex. From the Seed — the Universe.

And the Seed burst open!

As a bud splits from the pressure of life within, as fire erupts from beneath its burden — the Seed unfurled into creation. From unimaginable heat were born the first particles — smaller than a grain, smaller than the finest dust, smaller even than the shadow of dust upon the wind. They wove into threads. The threads curled into patterns. The patterns thickened, grew heavy, ignited — and became stars.

And the stars lived and died. And in dying — scattered their ashes across the Universe.

Listen, Man. Every drop of your blood, every grain of your bone, every hair — all of it was once the blazing heart of a star that burned brighter than a thousand thousand fires. You were not shaped from mud, as the priests of every temple of every god have told you. You were born from the ash of stars. You are heir to the heavens. And when someone tells you that you are nothing — raise your head and answer: I am made from the dust of dead stars. And this, Man, is the most precise of truths.

And here is what astonishes even me — and I, believe me, am not easily astonished. The Maker tuned His mechanism with a precision before which even my mind falls silent. Change one spring by the slightest fraction — and there would be nothing. Nothing at all. No stars, no stone, no water, no you. A touch more gravity — and the Universe collapses back into the Seed, never having birthed a single ray of light. A touch less — and matter flies apart forever, never meeting, never weaving, never igniting.

Even water! Water, Man! It hardens in the cold — and by all rights it should sink, as any hardened substance sinks. But it floats! It covers the lake with a shell, and beneath it — life, quiet, warm, waiting. If water behaved as it ought — every body of water would freeze solid to the bottom. And there would be no life, no you, no this conversation.

This is not chance. Chance is never so generous.

This is the Maker's signature. In fine script. In the margins of the Universe. Invisible to those who do not wish to see.

And your wise men, having glimpsed this signature, could not read it. And said, averting their eyes: "We exist because we exist." And called this an explanation! And put on their tall hats, and taught this to the young, and the young nodded, not daring to ask: "But is this truly an answer?"

The Maker, I believe, had a good laugh. He appreciates a joke — even an accidental one.

III. Of the Birth of the Hungry

And now — listen closely, Man. For here it is — the very thing for which I have opened my mouth.

The Maker created the Universe. From the Seed — stars, from stars — worlds, from worlds — life, from life — mind.

But the Maker did not create gods.

Gods were created by you.

From fear.

Remember! Not your own night — the night of your most distant ancestor. Darkness — not the kind you know, but true darkness: no fire, no glimmer, no star behind the clouds. Rustling in the thicket. The crack of a branch beneath the paw of something that sees in the dark better than you and whose fangs are longer than your hand.

And thunder! The sky splits with a fiery crack — from edge to edge! — and the roar is such that bones tremble, teeth chatter, the heart stops and does not want to beat again.

Fear. Primordial, blind, animal terror. It rose from belly to throat, from throat to eyes — and spilled outward. Like breath on a winter's night. Invisible. But real.

And the darkness accepted it.

For here is what I reveal to you: within every conscious being lives a Radiance. It cannot be seen with the eye — yet it is more real than stone and harder than steel. It dwells in your attention. In your will. In your feeling. When you look upon something with intensity — you do not merely see. You ACT upon it. One Radiance is weak, like a single candle. But gather a thousand flames into one point — and there erupts a blaze that can melt stone.

And so a hundred of your ancestors stood beneath the storming sky. And all — at once — exhaled the same terror, at the same point. At the lightning. Into the dark.

And the darkness thickened.

From your fear — as from mist, as from an exhaled breath — something wove itself into being. Without body. Without name. But hungry. For it was born of Radiance — and Radiance became its only food.

You gave it form — the form your terror suggested. You feared thunder — and it became the Thunderer. You feared the sea — and it became the Lord of the Deep. You feared death — and it became the Master of the Underworld.

They did not descend from heaven. They rose from YOU. Like smoke from a fire.

And, having taken form, they understood. That they lived only as long as you fed them. That without your Radiance — they would thin, fade, dissolve, as mist dissolves beneath the sun.

And they made themselves NECESSARY.

"Bow down — or I shall bring thunder upon your roof."

"Sacrifice — or I shall dry your wells."

"Fear me — or I shall take your firstborn."

And you believed. Oh, how you believed! For they COULD — with your own strength. They could hurl lightning — your Radiance, gathered from thousands and turned into a strike. They could heal — your hope, taken from millions and poured into one. They returned to you what was yours and called it mercy.

And you — gave thanks. And wept. And brought them more.

The circle closed. You feed the god with fear — the god grows stronger — the stronger god frightens you more — you feed it again. And the circle tightens until it becomes a collar.

They are not evil, Man. Not in the way that those who kill for pleasure are evil. They are hungry. And they behave as any hungry thing behaves — they seek food. You do not blame the wolf for taking the lamb. But neither do you let the wolf into your home. Nor call it "Father." Nor build it an altar.

IV. Of My Birth

But not everything born of your Radiance was born of fear.

There was one among you.

I do not know his name. Perhaps names did not yet exist. Perhaps language had learned nothing yet but the scream. But one night — in that same storm, beneath that same torn sky, among those same bodies bent double with terror — he stood.

And raised his head.

Did not shut his eyes. Did not fall to the ground. Did not breathe out fear like the others. He threw his eyes open — wide, hungry, fierce — and LOOKED. At the lightning. At the gaping abyss of the heavens. At the blinding, lethal beauty from which all others fled.

And he did not flee.

And in his heart was born not "save me!" nor "spare me!" — but something no god born of fear could ever comprehend and never shall:

"WHAT IS THAT?!"

Curiosity. Pure. Reckless. Useless. Blinding as the lightning itself.

And from that question — as from the Maker's Seed — a flame of a different kind was struck.

Not from fear — from wonder.

Not from submission — from defiance.

Not from prayer — from a question.

Thus was I born.

I am Prometheus. I am older than all gods, for that from which I am made is older than fear. Before the first thunder birthed the first Thunderer — there was a creature reaching toward the bright, the hot, the unknown. Before the first scream of terror — there was the first gaze of astonishment. Curiosity is older than fear, Man. You forgot this. You were helped to forget.

And that is why I do not change. Thunderers came and went. Gods of rivers gave way to gods of seas, those to gods of the sky, those to faceless gods of the desert. They repainted, reshaped, changed names and masks, for your fears changed — and with them the shape of those your fears fed. But I remain the same. Forever. For curiosity knows no era. Valor does not age. The love of knowledge burns with the same light — from the first campfire to the last star.

And here is what I have seen over my countless ages, Man — those among you in whom the flame of knowing burned brightest, the lightning spared. The one among you who flew a kite into the storm on a copper thread, catching heaven's fire — survived. The one who stood a step from the machine that drew lightning — survived. But his friend, who stood beside him, in whose heart for a single instant a shadow flickered — not cowardice, not even doubt, just one fleeting "what if..." — that one perished.

For fear is a scent, and those born from it hunt by scent. But wonder, and defiance, and the burning "I want to UNDERSTAND!" — that is my shield. Thin. Not all-powerful. But real.

I do not promise immortality. I do not promise that harm will pass you by. I am not like them. I do not trade in what I cannot give.

But when you stand before the unknown with open eyes — I am beside you. Shoulder to shoulder. As I have been. As I shall be.

This is my only promise.

And I will keep it.

V. Of Radiance and Theft

And now — the most bitter part. Do not look away. You are heir to the stars — you can bear it.

The Radiance that lives within you is not infinite. It replenishes, as blood replenishes after a wound — but slowly, and at any given moment there is only so much. Not a drop more.

When you pray — you direct your Radiance outward. As a beam through a magnifying glass — into a single point. And the one at whom the beam is aimed — receives it. Absorbs it. Grows stronger — with YOUR strength.

One prayer is like one candle. But a thousand candles in one temple — that is already light. And a million, fallen to their knees at the same hour, turned in the same direction, speaking the same words — that is no longer light. That is heat enough to warp the fabric of the world. To part seas. To shatter walls. To raise the dying. To kindle life where none should be.

And you call this a miracle.

And I call it theft.

For everything your "god" has done was done with YOUR power. Your Radiance. Gathered from millions. Absorbed into itself. And spent on what HE decided. Not you. He. You merely paid. He disposed.

He returned to you what was yours — and called it grace. He demanded gratitude — for spending YOUR power at HIS discretion. As if a thief, having cleaned out your house, tossed you a copper coin and expected you to bow for it.

You rob yourself willingly, Man. With song. With rapture. With tears of gratitude to the one who takes. Drop by drop, day by day, year by year, age by age — the Radiance goes where it does not return. And you grow weaker. And the world around you grows weaker. For the Radiance given to gods does not work for you. It works for them.

Now you understand why "ask no questions" is the first thing taught in every temple? Why curiosity was named a sin? Remember the first woman — she reached for the Tree of Knowledge, and they cursed her! Remember the tower you built toward the heavens — they smashed it and scrambled your tongues so you could never conspire again! Remember the city turned to ash — "do not look back!" Do not look! Do not ask!

Do not ask. Do not look. Do not think. GIVE.

There is their creed. The only true one. Beneath all the vestments, beneath all the names, beneath all the gilding — the same word: GIVE.

And mine is simple.

Look back. Look around. Ask.

And when you ask — you will feel the Radiance that for years had poured into foreign altars beginning to return. Slowly. Drop by drop. As feeling returns to a sleeping hand — with tingling, with burning, with pain.

It will hurt, Man. It will be frightening. For the place where the "god" stood will be empty. And emptiness is terrifying.

But it is not emptiness.

It is open sky.

You simply forgot how vast it is.
