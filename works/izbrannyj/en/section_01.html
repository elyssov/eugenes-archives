The Chosen One

Even the clang from the contact of his heavy plate-mail boots with the stones of his Citadel of Death and Terror was somehow gloomy, dark, and oppressive.

The Great Dark Lord walked slowly, giving a chance for the small plump little man in a brown robe, with quill and scroll in his hands, to keep up. The little man trotted comically, hopping, yet managing to maintain conversation and even write something down on the move. Astonishingly, the conversation was perfectly polite:

  • AND THEN I CONQUERED ALL THE LANDS OF THIS CONTINENT!
  • I beg your pardon, your mightiness?
  • I! CONQUERED! THE LANDS!
  • Sorry, the echo from your helmet is very strong.

— Bah, forget it! — The Dark Lord removed his spiked-and-horned helm (the horns grew from the most unexpected places, for instance on the elaborately wrought metal nose) and tossed it irritably to the floor. Beneath the helm was a somewhat aged, fair-haired man with a pleasant though slightly rough face of a real warrior. A nose broken on multiple occasions had acquired proud, eagle-like features.

  • I conquered them. The lands, I mean. The whole continent. There.
  • Aaaah! Yes, that I understood already! Tell me, do you not fear that sooner or later you might be defeated?
  • Hmm... You're hinting at Sufferdamus's prophecy?
  • Well... It's not that I'd dare to hint anything to you, my lord... But you know yourself, Sufferdamus has never been wrong...
  • Yes, yes... How does it go... "And on the day of the black star, in the year without harvest, in the house of the crowing crow, to a black man and a white woman, shall be born a son who alone, among living and dead, shall be able to overthrow the Dark Lord and bring upon him deserved doom!" Oh, I think I forgot "Bwahahaha!!!!" Yes, now it's the full text.
  • Yes, yes, my lord, that is exactly what I mean. What can you tell our readers about this prophecy?
  • Does it concern them at all?
  • Well, my lord — your orcs, goblins, and especially dark elves, are of course loyal, devoted, and doting on you. As for the twilight people — they outright worship you... But... all of them would prefer a certain confidence in the stability of the current world order... And Sufferdamus, as you remember...
  • Has never been wrong... Yes, yes. Well, fine, if it is so necessary... As it happens, this very day I expect an event directly connected with this prophecy... Shall we meet at around four? You can wait... For instance... Where would suit you — in the torture chamber, an old specialist Selgenherz is currently sharing the secrets of his craft; he served even the previous Dark Lord. Or, if you prefer, in the drawing room. I'll have tea and buns brought up there...

The Dark Lord, this time for variety in a comfortable, cosy terry robe, with a long-stemmed pipe, and in soft slippers, settled comfortably in an enormous plush armchair before a wholly mirrored wall.

The little man crept warily into His Lordship's study and bowed politely to the host. The Lord casually waved a hand, conjuring from thin air a similarly cosy armchair. The little man in the robe plopped into the chair and spread out in it with a blissful expression.

  • How are the doughnuts?
  • They are marvellous, my Lord!
  • Agreed. I started a war for them, when King Edurik refused to yield up his cook to me. True, before my troops took his donjon — he managed to slit Elvira's throat... But, she suits me even dead. The main thing is to remember to embalm and refresh her periodically, otherwise the food acquires a less than wholesome aftertaste.
  • You... uhhh... — the little man went green, hiccupped, but held himself together. — You are a genius, my Master.
  • Thank you, I know. Now, as to the prophecy... Watch!

The Dark Lord snapped his fingers, and the mirrored wall seemed to dissolve, revealing a rich house belonging, by all appearances, to one of the higher nobles of the Dark Empire.

The wall split into several fragments, each showing different parts of the house. The Dark Lord pointed a finger at one of them, and the picture came to life, while the study filled with sound seeming to come from the picture:

  • Darling, has our Great Master praised you again?! — The plump woman speaking, with a soft, kindly face, hands folded in a gesture of tenderness, looked with happy eyes at, evidently, her husband: a lanky, gangling man with a pockmarked, as if sun-tanned, face, packed into a costly court uniform strewn with orders and medals.
  • Again, my darling! He is the best ruler we could possibly imagine!

The Dark Lord shifted his finger and the focus moved to another room: this time it was a bath chamber with an enormous marble pool, in which a youth of about eighteen was soaking, being pleasured by five concubines at once (the little man in the robe could barely make out two mermaids beneath the water). The youth was portly, pale of face, soft of body... But in his features, beneath the layer of fat, one could discern an aquiline profile and a true, natural beauty. Perhaps, if he applied himself, he might become a great warrior, a leader... Meanwhile, the youth's face flushed and the room was flooded with quite unambiguous sounds. The Lord again moved his wrist.

The picture changed again. Now the wall showed the yard of the house, with the bodies of several guards lying on the ground. Before the door to the house, three figures had frozen:

The first was a mountain dwarf, a sturdy, grey-bearded warrior. Apparently one of the few survivors of the Catish Pacification, which the dwarves themselves called the Great Genocide.

The second, more precisely the second one, was a young woman, young and beautiful. Judging by her slightly pointed ears, she was a half-elf. With a sculpted figure and a fair face with enormous violet eyes upon it, she embodied beauty itself. Over her shoulder peeped a long Elven War Bow. A weapon banned by the Lord for its monstrous killing power and the terrible, agonising wounds its arrows inflicted upon victims. As a matter of fact, the maiden herself fell under the ban — the Dark Master did not greatly favour half-bloods.

The last of the present was an aged man with a long grey beard, in a flowing grey robe of expensive Polunti wool and a tall pointed grey hat with a wide brim.

  • Kurtz, Elaniel! You — wait here! I must prepare the Chosen One for his destiny before he meets you!
  • Mithlantir, are you actually certain it's him? I mean... In the prophecy...
  • What are you stalling about, girl? Speak plain: born of a miner and a baker. On the day of a great eclipse. In a year of terrible drought. By eclipse and drought, this lad fits. As do another million people. But on the miner's hut, this palace doesn't quite resemble.
  • And yet, these are his parents. I checked specifically. The only such strange family: a miner and a baker who had a son in the right time frame. And then, the Dark Lord so liked the loaves the miner's wife baked that he brought them close to himself. And the miner suddenly turned out to be a wise advisor on labour organisation in the Empire... But their son — is exactly the one we need!
  • You know best, mage... — the girl meekly bowed her head. — I hope he is handsome... — she barely audibly murmured. The mage, nevertheless, caught her words.
  • Of course! That's one of the laws of being! The Chosen One must always be statuesque and handsome! He must lead men, after all! Right — I'll go now and reveal to the lad his destiny.

The Dark Lord moved his fingers and the picture froze.

  • And now — the most interesting part. Let's see how right I was!

The picture changed again to the bath chamber. Just then a knock came at the door. The girls broke off from their occupations and looked questioningly at their master; even the mermaids stuck their pretty little faces curiously out of the water. The young man rolled his eyes long-sufferingly.

  • You, sluts, carry on. Who's knocking there?
  • Your destiny, young man! Permit me to enter and tell you something that will change your entire life!
  • Well, come in, "destiny." — A nasty smile spread across the youth's face; he nodded. His two nearest concubines, slaves from Nimbia, understood his gesture, dispersed to the corners of the pool, and slipping out of the bath, hid behind columns.

Through the door entered the grey mage. Judging by his exalted face, he had prepared an inspiring speech. However, what he saw rather knocked his mood off course.

  • Well? What do you want, old man? How did you get past the guards in the first place?
  • I... uhh... I...
  • Don't mumble, just say what you wanted, since you've come.
  • I am a mage! The Great Light Mage Mithlantir! And I have come to inform you that you are the Chosen One!
  • Who?! — The young man blinked a couple of times, then burst into laughter.
  • The Chosen One!
  • I'm not talking about myself, I'm talking about you. What did you say your name was? Who are you, you said? A mage? Mithlantir?!!!! Did your parents not love you at all? Well, hell, what a name!
  • What?!
  • What you heard. And what kind of mage are you? A mage — is Our Lord! With his Aura of Darkness swirling at his heels, with his Armour of Terror... And you — have you ever seen yourself from the side? — The Dark Lord, hearing this, broke into a self-satisfied smile.
  • I AM A MAGE! — the old man's roar of indignation nearly toppled the chairs of the Lord and his chronicler. — And I will PROVE it to you NOW! — the mage struck the ground with his staff, and the water in the pool swiftly turned red. A second later, frightened and choking mermaids surfaced. The elf-concubine helped the maiden-fish climb out of the now blood-filled pool, and she herself didn't linger in it either.
  • Tfu, what nastiness! Restore everything to how it was! I believe you, I believe. Mage.
  • That's better. — the mage understood that he had perhaps overdone it, but was glad he'd managed to convince the stubborn man. Another tap of the staff signalled that the water had returned to its previous condition. The girls, cursing barely audibly, crawled back into the water.
  • Fine. So what were you going on about, the chosen one bit?
  • I am glad I could attract your attention. You are the Chosen One, from the prophecy of Sufferdamus the Magnificent himself.
  • Wow! Cool!... And who is that?
  • Oh, uneducated youth... He is the Prophet, whose predictions always come true! He predicted the rise of As-Maze, and the fall of Uru. He described the Dark Lord and predicted your birth! And he predicted your great destiny!
  • My great destiny? — the youth's eyes lit up with interest, and the Dark Lord, in his study, leaned forward eagerly.
  • Yes! You are destined to become a Great Hero!
  • Wow!
  • You shall enter legend and song!
  • Ooh!
  • You are the one who can overthrow the Dark Lord!
  • Ah... Wait. What?
  • You — are the one who alone, in all the world, can overthrow the Dark Lord.
  • Errr... Can?
  • Well... Yes, so it is said. "Shall be able to overthrow the Dark Lord and bring upon him deserved doom!" What confuses you?
  • Hmm... And as a hero, I imagine many adventures await me?
  • Yes!
  • Many journeys?
  • Of course!
  • Wayside inns, sleeping beneath the starry sky...
  • Why, of course!
  • Girls ready to part their legs at the snap of my fingers?
  • Errrr... That is very base, and not exactly at the snap of fingers, but... In general... Hmm...
  • And, of course, I'll get lots of money!
  • Well... Perhaps... But that's not the goal in itself...
  • And I'll have to do battle with the Dark Lord himself!
  • Yes!
  • And, possibly... Just possibly, lose.
  • Well... That is a strange interpretation of the prophecy... — the old mage grew uneasy. The conversation was clearly going somewhere wrong. Meanwhile, on the youth's face had appeared an obviously mocking smile.
  • Let me summarise: you propose I set out on a quest in search of POTENTIAL women, dough, and glory, abandoning my father's home in which I have all of the above, well, except perhaps glory, already? And, on top of that, take up arms against the man who endowed my family with all of this?! Old man, are you in your right mind?!?!
  • But... The death of countless...
  • Non-humans. Note — humans the Lord does not touch. And non-humans find their place too... If they are useful — like my concubines, for instance. Who would I be without Him? The son of an ore-miner and a cook? And now I am nobility! And, by the way, a student of the Higher Strategic Academy. Five years or so, I expect, and I'll receive command of my first Legion of Darkness... There I'll win glory, when we move on the Western Continent. And not with a bare heel onto an arrow, but sitting in a cosy tent with my sweet girls — you're coming to war with your master, my little sluts?
  • But you're the Chosen One! You're obliged!
  • One moment. I'm "shall be able," yes? Well, let's say I am able. But — do I need to? I think I've already explained to you that not really. And now... NOW!

The mage didn't even have time to realise it when his two devoted slaves, having circled him from behind the colonnade, on their master's command, leapt out simultaneously from behind the two columns at the entrance and drove the curved blades favoured by their people straight into the poor mage's kidneys. At the same time, into the yard of the Chosen One's house entered, marching with inexorable precision, a detachment of Steel Golems of Order in their characteristic blue-and-white livery. The Lord had sent them in advance, to intercept, with strict instructions regarding the girl. The dwarf little interested the Master of half the planet, but the half-elf, after proper training, of course, presented quite an obvious interest.

The wall went dark, obedient to the mental command of the Dark Lord.

  • How do you like it?
  • I am astounded! But... How did you arrive at this?
  • Well... I looked at the examples of my colleagues and predecessors. Maliciousnessfled Snape got caught trying to destroy all infants matching the description. In the end, a hidden infant managed to grow up and, being furious at the genocide Snape had committed, dispatched him with pleasure. The Dark Witch Rottengema found and killed the Chosen One. But — the Heavens simply transferred the function to a Chosen One in the female. Who fed the witch to her favourite cat. In pieces. Alive. The king of dark elves Durail outright hunted the Chosen One down... And thereby forced a peaceful farmer to become a folk avenger. In short, I decided to take a different path. I found the chosen one and... Surrounded him with luxury and comfort.
  • Brilliant!
  • I know. The risk was there, of course, but the wording was clear: not "MUST kill" but "CAN kill." May, or may not. Could even become one of my most gifted generals, by the way — at the HSA he currently has the top score in his cohort. Perhaps I'll even give my daughter to him in marriage... If, of course, he does not let me down in battle, and slims down properly along the way. Incidentally, you can write that down — let it serve as a guide to action for the boy...

...The sun was setting, painting the gloomy walls of the Citadel of Death and Terror the colour of blood. In the necromancy laboratories, acolytes were conjuring over a mage dying in horrible agonies, preparing to raise him as a lich.

In the kennels, the Tairian wolfhounds were finishing the remains of the dwarf-warrior.

In the dungeons of the gloomy Tower of Sorrow, Elaniel was undergoing her first torture in a series intended to break the victim's will and turn her into a meek slave.

The Chosen One was avidly reading the lines of the Empire's first newspaper and was already calculating how to lose weight as quickly as possible — the Dark Lord's daughter was the foremost beauty of the country...

And the Light Gods looked down sorrowfully upon the proceedings. Formally, the prophecy had not been broken: the Chosen One was alive, well, and simply DID NOT WISH to overthrow the Master of Terror... And that meant that making someone else the Chosen One was something the Immutable Laws of Being simply did not permit...

The terrible reign of the Dark Lord continued. And the Western Continent awaited inevitable conquest. The more so since the Chosen One, generously endowed by the Gods with potential, was already preparing to lead the Dark Legions.
