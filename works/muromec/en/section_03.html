Chapter 3

...It's very strange, really: treating a girl who lives inside you. The premise alone sounds insane. And when it comes to execution...

So here I am, sitting on the sofa, relaxed as anything, controls in Vasilisa's hands. And she's holding a tub of Baskin Robbins ice cream. And she's eating it with obvious pleasure. "Rum Raisin," no less. And the true madness of the situation is that SHE's eating — but it tastes good to ME.

Christ, I don't even know how to explain this coherently. Obviously, she's eating the ice cream with my mouth. But that's exactly where the total absurdity kicks in.

Anyway, fine — whatever keeps the baby happy and not crying. Besides, she's considerate — eats carefully so I don't get brain freeze. And it gives me time to think about how to explain to her just how much everything around her has changed. She hasn't bombarded me with questions yet, but her curiosity is practically oozing out of my ears. Mixed with anxiety, naturally.

— Vanya, I wanted to ask you something... Don't be offended, but I need to know: where did you get the dollars in your wallet? Are you a speculator?!

— Uh... No. I'm a legitimate entrepreneur. Well, more precisely — a technical director.

— What director???? And where are the dollars from? And those colourful bits of paper...

— Euros?

— What's a "euro"?

— Well, the common European currency.

— Common???? Did they organise a Soviet Union too?

— Well... In a manner of speaking... Not Soviet exactly... Look, Vas, where should I even start?

— Start at the beginning. What's going on here?

— Well... For starters, the Soviet Union doesn't exist anymore.

— Wh... WHAT?!

Vasya actually jumped in her seat, nearly crashing to the floor due to the unfamiliar centre of gravity. I had to "take the wheel" back.

— Easy. The USSR fell apart about six years after your death. We've had a democratic and capitalist state since then.

— But... How... Why...

Apparently, it had hit her again. I don't know why, but the news about the USSR she took, it seemed, far harder than the news about her own death.

— Bro... Br... Broke up?! How does that work?

— Well, into republics. Kazakhstan separate, Georgia separate, Ukraine separate...

— Ukr... UKRAINE?! SEPARATE?!... Vanya... I need to be quiet for a bit... And please, if it's not too much trouble... take me for a little walk...
