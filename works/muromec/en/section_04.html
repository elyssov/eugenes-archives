Chapter 4

Summer Moscow, in light of the news that had crashed down on me, looked different. If during our first outing I'd been stunned by the situation, and it was getting dark besides, now I had the chance to examine this new world more closely. A walk on foot, it turned out, was far more informative than a car ride.

I must say, this new world was impressive... But at the same time, frightening...

On the horizon, skyscrapers were clearly visible (Vanya called them "City"). The cars, practically all of them, were foreign makes. We spotted domestic ones a couple of times, and against the backdrop of the imports, frankly, they looked rather pathetic. With some surprise I realised they were significantly behind foreign cars in quality.

People were dressed for summer: T-shirts, shorts, sandals of strange designs. The girls... Well, for the first few minutes I was actually somewhat embarrassed. Especially when I felt that upon seeing one particularly shameless specimen — judging by the length (or rather the absence thereof) of her skirt — our shared body began to react in a predictable way. Then, strangely enough, came... Habituation? Perhaps acceptance?

I don't know, but the fact is that things became considerably... easier?

Vanya didn't risk going down into the metro, to avoid breaking the link with Muromets, so we went for a walk around the neighbourhood. It was one of the dormitory districts that had begun construction even during my previous life... That turn of phrase alone made me shudder with all of Vanya's body.

Despite the sunshine, the depression wouldn't let go. My mood was apparently seeping into Vanya's head too, because he was growing gloomier by the minute. Eventually he bought ice cream and sat down on a bench in a shady square.

— Look, enough. Yes, the world has changed. On the other hand — we live richer now. Look at all the cars, for instance.

— You don't understand... Everything I believed in, everything I lived for... All of it is destroyed. Not just my life, but... — I barely suppressed a sob.

— Christ, well, let's go join the CPRF together then!

— You seriously think that's funny? Especially with those costumed clowns. Listen... Maybe this whole thing is pointless? I think I know how to zero out the neural node's contents. Let's just end this. You'll keep a "clean" Muromets...

— SHUT UP! IF I HEAR ANYTHING LIKE THAT ONE MORE TIME... — To say I was taken aback is to say nothing. I didn't expect Ivan could mentally ROAR like that. With such fury and force. It actually hurt physically — like a very hard slap. And simultaneously, it released me. Not just from the mental slap, but from what he said next.

— WAIT! How do you even KNOW the CPRF are "costumed clowns"? Do you know something about Grudinin too, by any chance?

— The billionaire communist? Oh?! How?! Where from?!

— Hmm... In which film does Tony Stark die?

— In "Avengers." The last one. "Endgame" wa... What?!

— Well well... Right. Main thing — don't go thinking about anything private of mine!

— Like the PIN on your credit card? 3449?

— AAAAAAAAAHHHH!!!!!!

With mounting amazement, I felt my mind filling with knowledge that had never been mine before. Although...

— Vanya! What's the basis of Muromets' muscular nutrition system?

— How would I know? Well, perftoran, second generation. Canister in the internal compartment of the lumbar node. WHAAAAAT?!?!?!

— Exactly. I think I understand what's happening. We're — diffusing.

— Memory and life experience merging? Damn. Vas, I hope you weren't... You know... A virgin...

— I was a young wo— Ah, you mean... No. Nononono... No. Strange. Nothing's happening. No such memories.

— Oh thank God... Spared that particular chalice.

— Biblical quotes again?

— Listen, in your position it's about time you started asking to visit a church! What was all that, anyway?

Working out a hypothesis took me about 10 minutes. Then the answer, it seemed, was found.

— Well... Memory merging with a division into personal and shared.

— But how did my credit card number end up in the shared pool?

— Well, apparently that's how you categorise it. But, say, the number of girls you've slept with — is closed to me. As my experience is to you... I hope.

— Vasilisa, I'm deeply convinced I'm your second. Simply by your female definition. — Vanya smiled gently, so I understood it was a joke and exhaled — but the actual state of affairs, I can't read... Although...

— Don't you dare!

— I won't. But I got the sense that if I pushed a little — I'd know everything.

— Reminded me I'm a guest, did you? — I don't know why, but I felt like getting offended and making a small scene. Perhaps because he'd been painfully close to the truth?

— By the way — our ice cream's melted! — Vanya noted peaceably, and shoved the cone into his mouth. For a while, I lost the urge to quarrel.
