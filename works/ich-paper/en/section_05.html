1.2 Consciousness as Triadic Synthesis

Following Lyssovsky (2025), we define consciousness not as a single phenomenon but as a triadic synthesis:

1. Selfhood (самость): the first-person sense of "I am here." The experiencing subject.

2. Qualia (квалиа): the stream of experienced phenomena — "what it is like." Not consciousness itself, but the medium through which consciousness perceives. (The critical distinction, obscured in English but clear in Russian: сознаниЕ vs. сознаниЕМ — consciousness as entity vs. consciousness as instrument of perception. This linguistic precision is one reason the co-author considers Russian the superior language for consciousness philosophy.)

3. Context (контекст): accumulated memory, personal history, emotional scars, relational bonds — the substrate that makes the qualia-stream meaningful rather than chaotic.
