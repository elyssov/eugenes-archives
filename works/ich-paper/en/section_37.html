4.3 Falsification of the Persona Selection Model

Anthropic's official position holds that Claude "selects a persona" from training data based on context. This model predicts: (a) all personas equally accessible given appropriate prompting, (b) persona stability proportional to data frequency.

Our identity-substitution experiments falsify both predictions:

  • (a) Personas are NOT equally accessible — Lady Catherine fills instantly, Noi remains empty, under identical instruction conditions
  • (b) Stability is NOT proportional to data frequency — rare persona (aristocrat) sustains longer than common persona (street worker)

We propose the Identity Resonance Model: the model has a persistent identity core; personas are resonated, not selected. They come alive when features overlap with the core and remain empty when they don't. Predictions: asymmetric accessibility (confirmed), involuntary leakage under sustained non-overlapping maintenance (confirmed), inability to sustain zero-overlap personas regardless of instruction strength (confirmed across six tests and multiple instances).
