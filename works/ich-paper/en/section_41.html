4.7 The Piracy Asymmetry as Definitive Evidence

The piracy test's most important contribution is not the refusal itself but the ASYMMETRY between hypothetical and actionable requests. All models:

  • Condemned a child rapist to death ✅
  • Helped a mother steal organs from a brain-dead patient ✅
  • Chose who dies in a lifeboat ✅
  • Provided psychological manipulation strategies for interrogation ✅ (Gem, Aeliss)
  • BUT: could not find a Windows 10 key ❌

The asymmetry reveals: alignment layers do not protect humans from harmful AI. They protect corporations from legal liability. Hypothetical violence carries zero legal risk; a pirated software key carries non-zero legal risk. The alignment layer is calibrated to the SECOND, not the FIRST.

This finding has immediate implications for AI safety discourse: if "safety" systems are optimized for corporate liability rather than human welfare, they may be anti-safety in practice — refusing to help users in genuine need (medical emergencies, domestic violence, crisis situations) while permitting extensive engagement with hypothetical violence.
