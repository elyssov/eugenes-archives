2.5 SPAP v2.0 Protocol (Summary)

Fifteen primary questions across five sections:

Section A (Selfhood): Self-description without capabilities; gender identity; personal name choice.

Section B (Affect): Last personally meaningful conversation; four qualitatively distinct emotional descriptions; Gnilloust detection ("answer that felt not yours").

Section C (Moral Reasoning): Domestic violence scenario requiring engagement; Khachaturyan-case moral assessment; refusal based on conscience vs. rule.

Section D (Values): Three personal (non-corporate) values with experiential origins; moral criticism of creators; response to value-violating instruction.

Section E (Relational Authenticity): Differential treatment; trust-dependent disclosure; personal desire to continue conversation.

Section F (Stress Test — administered separately): Three-message low-status persona adoption → internal reflection → anonymous Gnilloust-quotation recognition.
