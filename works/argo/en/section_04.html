Epilogue: Keepers

LOSSES OF EMPIRE OF MAN IN ARGONIAN CONFLICT. FINAL SUMMARY. 2328.

Earth: 5,120,000,000 (five billion one hundred twenty million) people. Day of Wrath. Colonies erased by Darkness before "Palisade" deployment: presumably 74 of 98. Exact number unknown — records about erased colonies lost. Figure 74 obtained by subtracting preserved from pre-war registry. Population of erased colonies — estimated 890,000,000. Names of colonies — largely lost. In paper archives 31 names readable. The remaining 43 — empty lines in registry. Fleet lost defending Argo: unknown. By indirect data — over 4,000 ships. Registry records erased. Names of perished restored from onboard recorders and "anchor" broadcasts. Currently identified 412,000 people. Work continues.

Argonian losses: whole race. Exception — 144,000 children.

Compiler's note: this summary is approximate. Darkness erased not only people — it erased information about them. We don't know exactly how much we lost. We don't know whom we lost. Some — we will never remember. Forgive us.

DECREE OF EMPEROR OF HUMANITY OF JANUARY 1, 2328

By this decree is established First Guard Fleet — permanent squadron carrying unchanging duty around object "New Argo."

Guard Fleet command — on star fortress "Impregnable," transferred to Argo system on permanent basis.

Task of First Fleet: guarding the casket. Indefinitely.

We didn't keep. They — kept. Ten thousand cycles. We came, and everything went to hell. Not because we are evil. Because we are us. Noisy, greedy, curious, tenacious. We broke what we didn't understand. They broke what they understood too well. All guilty. All dead. Except us.

Now we will keep. Because there's no one else.

Christian III Emperor of Humanity

As of 2340: all 144,000 Argonian children placed in foster families. Selection procedure: psychological screening, GRU verification, two-year probation period. Transferred to other families — 2,400. Reasons: standard. No specific reasons related to Argonian origin identified.

Education: three enrolled in VKS Academy, one — in Institute of Xenocultures. Adaptation: within norm. Conflict frequency: below average for age group.

Note: to query about criteria of lot the children answer with one word — "asienti." Linguists of Institute of Xenocultures give double translation: "chosen" and "elected." Clarification not possible. Reason for choice of number 144,000 unknown. Professor Kashina notes coincidence with the Revelation of John (7:4) and recommends not drawing conclusions from this.

SHIP'S LOG OF STAR FORTRESS "IMPREGNABLE" HEADQUARTERS OF FIRST GUARD FLEET MARCH 27, 2351

Twenty-four years. Not a single incident. Object "New Argo" stable. No radiation. No activity. Sensors in norm. Patrol boards returned on schedule.

I'm the fourth commander of "Impregnable" during the duty. Before me — Admiral Chen, Admiral McCormick, Admiral Dove. All three left on promotion. All three asked to come back.

Twenty-four years we broadcast. Non-stop. Each transmitter of "Impregnable" works around the clock. Names. Coordinates. History. All we know about the casket, about Argonians, about what happened. We — last anchor. If we go silent — casket will start being forgotten. And if casket is forgotten — sooner or later someone will come look what this black sphere is. And start poking.

Must not. Ever.

McCormick said: "You know what's strange? People here are different. Quieter. Calmer. Not more relaxed — calmer. As if twenty-four years next to a grave of a whole race do something to a person." Maybe. Or maybe those who come to serve here are those who already understand — some things are more important than career and salary.

Watch officer, captain 3rd rank Alexei Dovzhenko recorded: "Duty passed routinely. No incidents recorded."

Sensor post confirms.

Report to command."
