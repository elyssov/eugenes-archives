Act One: Brothers

They came unexpectedly, like thieves in the night. They turned out so similar to us that this misled us. We didn't immediately understand they were merely a disfigured reflection of ourselves. If our Emperor was the wisest among us as a ruler, then theirs — was merely a military chieftain. If we in everything strove for beauty and harmony, then they — exclusively for functionality and profit. We let them into the very heart of our space, hoping and believing we could exchange wisdom; they brought us their parody of culture. We, open to all new, didn't immediately notice that our exquisite dishes began being displaced by their hamburgers, our musical instruments — by their primitive synthesizers... Our culture was losing the meeting with their pushy and brazen pseudo-culture. The veil fell from our eyes. They were not our brothers — they were a virus, a plague of the universe! And again we failed to account for their treachery — assuring us of eternal friendship, they created a huge military fleet. Their army developed and became ever more powerful. At that we were assured this was all for the defense of our commonwealth. What fools we were!

When we tried to break ties between us — the full might of Humanity crashed onto our heads. All of us fought worthily, but what could we do against their monstrous fleet? But still we managed to learn something this time too. The iron heel of the Empire of Man hung over our home, Shining Argo. The Last Battle is coming!

Second Archon of Capella Argo Asmani Peali "Aureal Immapuli" (lit. "The Last War") (entered into the archive of Capella Argo in 2327 by the chronology of EH).

We came with laurel branch in hand. When the crew of "Grant" emerged at Argo from an unscheduled jump into hyper, we were shocked to find our brothers lost in the depths of the universe. Our friendship developed exponentially. Charmed by Argo's ancient culture, we began the widest exchange of knowledge. Our merchants ensured powerful trade turnover. For over a hundred years nothing portended conflict. Mutual penetration of cultures was simply incredible. Our women loved iridescent fabrics from Argo, our scientists greedily absorbed Argonian knowledge of semi-living materials, Argonians with pleasure mastered earthly cultural heritage. Literally in all fields of knowledge we with Argonians found something to borrow from each other.

Luckily, we humans are very inert in our convictions, and the fact that instead of some race of "double-breathing spiders" hostile to humanity, we, contrary to all sci-fi predictions, met a race of brothers and friends, did not reduce our paranoia nurtured by eternal human wariness of the unknown. We developed our military forces with no less, and perhaps even greater zeal and energy. After all we knew that now we'd have to defend not only ourselves but also our Argonian friends, who despite all wisdom had no military fleet, and the hour of meeting with those very "spiders," as we were sure, approached with each opened star system.

Trouble, however, came not from unexplored depths of space. On October 28, 2321 at 12:45 GMT to the metropolis from hyper emerged a whole fleet of Argonian coasters. Having answered with standard identifiers on the "friend-or-foe" query, these transports stood in docking glide to station "Trade-1," the largest orbital construct. At 13:28 all ships synchronously began descent into Earth's atmosphere. If defense service hadn't been confused and opened destruction fire immediately — most likely consequences wouldn't have been so monstrous. Nevertheless critical minutes were spent on attempts to contact the cargo-ships. When it became obvious that landing glides of coasters led to major earth cities, undertaking something was already too late. By heroic efforts of PKO forces three coasters were destroyed, whose debris generously strewed streets of London, Rome and Moscow, but couldn't damage Genshtab, Imperial Administration and Palace. The Emperor remained alive... But over 5 billion people — no. At altitude of 5000 meters Argonian ships exploded with power around 100 Mt each.

Another 5 minutes later was published the famous Emperor's decree "On the Great War," you can find it in the appendices to this book. Shaken by treachery but not broken, the Emperor announced universal mobilization and ordered closure of Human space from Argonian ships. This saved our colonies. On that long day Argonians couldn't perfectly synchronize emergence to all worlds of Humanity and didn't account for recently-deployed VKS hyperlink. Thus, news of the Emperor's decree even on Gehenna, the most remote colony, became known 10 minutes from its transmission from Earth. Dozens of Argonian coasters were burned at the moment of emergence from hyperspace. A column of 28 transports was burned over Eden at the moment of their formation into docking glide. In all cases Argonians were offered to immediately leave the space of the Empire of Man. In all cases the response to the offer was the afterburner mode of transports. And then our guns made an offer there was no one left to refuse...

Lieutenant-General of VKS Mikhail Totsky "The Argonian War" 2329. Excerpts.
