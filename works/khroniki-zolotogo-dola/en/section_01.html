Introduction

Feudal countries are very interesting phenomena. Some have a king who is merely first among equals. Some are ruled by landlords with a king of symbolic status. Some even have a royal demesne smaller than that of his vassals.

Ksarpathia was the only country where the holdings of all its lords, including the king, somehow got lost against the background of the holdings of the illustrious dukes of Zlatodol.

This mysterious perturbation was explained by an extremely non-progressive system of throne succession and King Gorlion II's great love for his younger son.

The result of the collision between Ksarpathian legislation and customs, which insisted on passing the throne to the elder good-for-nothing, with the cunning and legal nimbleness of King Gorlion, who believed that only his younger son could handle state administration (the boy spent all his time at his father's right hand, unlike the elder — a womanizer and hunter) — became the astonishing geopolitical phenomenon called "the Duchy of Zlatoy Dol" (Golden Dale), to which the rest of the kingdom adhered like a sad afterthought. This twist, fixed in law, may have caused quite some emotion in its time among the descendants of the direct heirs of King Gorlion II — Gorlions III, IV, V and so on through VIII inclusive. Nevertheless, with each new generation the sharpness of conflict decreased. Not surprising, since the duke of the Dol not only traditionally acknowledged himself as vassal to the current Gorlion, paid taxes and even did not scorn to visit his crowned relative at first request — but also carried on his back the whole burden of state administration. So it's quite understandable that with such an approach by the ruling dynasty to the prevailing situation, in the end a certain sympathy even arose.

So, about two hundred years after King Gorlion II's cunning political feint — this distribution of forces in the country became not merely natural but the only acceptable one.

And probably Ksarpathia would have lived on in peace, tranquility and prosperity, year after year increasing its wealth (and it must be said that with wealth in this country everything was excellent — fat fertile land, mild climate, rich mineral deposits in the mountains...)

If not for neighbors.

Yes, this sad truth has long been known to all living beings — from naive plowmen to elder sages: most problems usually arise from not-too-fortunately situated neighbors...

The Duchy of Golden Dol bordered on the east with the rest of the kingdom of Ksarpathia. On the north stretched the Karldilier mountain chain, partly in the Duchy's possession and partly under the rule of the Subterranean Workers' Republic. On the south began the Great Pummikan Desert, named after that idiot-mage who decided to conduct experiments with mass-destruction spells in a once flowering and densely populated area, rather than somewhere in the far north.

And on the west was Concordia Danaan. And this was the main headache of the Duchy — and along with it, of all Ksarpathia — for nearly 150 years... Now, my friends, settle in comfortably. I'll tell you the story of this war, beginning with the first conflict...
