Chapter 3

The face in the copper disk's focus grimaced and twitched its mighty, meaty nose. — I take it you're tearing me from important state affairs not to report you've decided to gift us the southern spurs?

— Definitely not earlier than you manage to defeat me in "Royal Reward"! — the duke smirked. — I'm also glad to see you, Hans!

The Secretary General of the Workers' Republic Hans Hermstag Heavy Axe, or simply Hans (for old buddies) — bared his teeth in response:

— I'll get you sooner or later, but you'll still wriggle out!

— I'll wriggle out — Duke Ariette agreed compliantly — but you first win even once. OK, Hans, I'm actually on business.

The dwarf cast an attentive glance at the duke from under his shaggy overhanging brows. The dwarven primordial good-naturedness gave way to even more primordial thoughtful and thorough approach to business. 120 years of unchanged rule of the subterranean republic told — based on indirect data and minor signs Hans came to needed conclusions so quickly for a dwarf, that this excellent rational thinking resembled the intuition in principle absent in dwarves.

— Oho? Judging by your physiognomy being even gloomier than usual — it's clearly not about new trade agreements.

Ariette smiled sadly.

— Alas no, my old friend. I have war on the horizon.

— War?! Who in their right mind, besides us of course, could risk tangling with the Brilliant Golden Dol? Although... A moment, surely not the elves?!

— Them.

— Nothing of itself! — Heavy Axe, hearing of war, gathered himself — and now before the duke sat not an old close school-buddy, but the ruler of a friendly and quite powerful state. A ruler smart and competent. — Kernia they digested 50 years. When they ate the Region of Holy Faith that buffered for you — we expected a respite of at least 40 years, it's almost as big as Kernia in territory, and even denser in population. And it turns out ten years sufficed?

— Apparently. As I understand — their assimilation and integration of captured areas becomes more effective with each year. The ultimatum was issued until tomorrow. We're objectively not ready for war.

— Weapons, supplies? — businesslike clarified Hans, preparing to record the most necessary for allies. The thrifty dwarves were inclined to creating strategic reserves "in case of some alarm," in which the ruler of the Dol over long years of cooperation verified more than once. In principle, with their reserves one could hold out in siege for quite a long time. Other thing is that such variant did not at all suit Mark. No, both supplies and of course weapons will be needed all the same, even at best development of events, on which the soberly-thinking Ariette didn't particularly count. But alliance with such a people as dwarves gave more serious profit.

Generally speaking, the subterranean republic was a state of a completely special type — in principle possible, as the duke strongly suspected, exclusively due to the unique mentality of gnomes. A dwarf — any dwarf! — gave 300% in work (actually, that's why the maximum workday was a mere five hours) — and at that worked completely free. But all material and other benefits proportional to results of his labor he had for free. Always. Money really had no circulation inside the country. The main value was social recognition, which was natural result of conscientious labor. Other-type status in the subterranean world simply did not exist...

So finding more responsible and by-and-large reliable comrades was hard. Of course with other states trade went very well indeed. For practically the whole continent these guys regularly supplied not only minerals and rarest gemstones in industrial quantities, but also some very original technical developments. So economically, as in many others — the Subterranean Workers' Republic was more than prosperous.

With magic — yes, there were problems. But religion the dwarves had. But again — special. The dwarven equivalent of temples were Halls of Ancestors; the Great Ancestors themselves were almost innumerable in quantity — and essentially, every dwarf had a chance someday to add to this number. Great became the most glorified in their generation dwarves, and stories of their deeds were passed from mouth to mouth. For help and support every dwarf could turn to the Greats personally, without mediation of priests, which in reasonable subterranean lands simply did not exist. Other thing is that they appealed for help rarely, in the most exceptional cases — so as not to upset the Ancestors for nothing. But as Ariette assumed — in the near future this help might be needed too...

The duke crookedly smirked and shook his head.

— Well no, you know me — Mark slightly adjusted the glowing communicator. — Rather people. I, at any rate, by my estimates, have a completely non-combat-ready army. We haven't crossed swords with anyone for almost 100 years practically. Anyone more or less combat-capable I have only on the border. They in principle will hold the "stone ring"... Well, for some time. The elves' tactics you know. Considering that in one "litter" they birth five-six future soldiers... In general I wouldn't refuse help of allied troops.

— So, who's at the army's head?

— Global strategy is on me, tactical command — the sovereign agreed to take personally.

— Oh! That's the very first of the house of Amriette in the latest 200 years who managed at Freiberg Long Beard to get 100 out of 100 in the "Kobayashi-Maru" test for "Field Command"?

— The very same. Nephew.

Hans nodded a bit ironically. Of Long Beard among both people and dwarves circulated legends. Old Freiberg had been the terror of all students for not one hundred years. Distinguished even by dwarven measures by quite small stature, he compensated this small shortcoming by his unusually high professorial cap (the top of which reached middle of chest of his even tallest pupils), as well as by highest demands. Earning even 50 of 100 points for the mentioned test managed only very few. No less than by his glory of most harmful and finicky teacher of three universities, Freiberg prided himself on his again even among gnomes outstanding, descending much below knees beard — and in all seriousness took the recently spread among young Herzstadt aristocrats fashion for light and not very stubble, which all the girls took to — as quite natural respectful imitation...

The innocent delusion of the deserved professor was quite forgivable. The beard among dwarves was always their distinctive feature and matter of special pride. It began growing in earliest adolescence and by twelve-thirteen years already reached palm length, if not more. In adult gnomes the beard was at least to the waist; careful care for it took no last place in daily dwarven worries, throughout all three hundred and sometimes four hundred years of their lives. However, beard as long as Freiberg's not every grew. Still not for all tasks such decoration was convenient. But cute, ginger-haired, plump dwarfesses much valued it. By the way, if from the standpoint of people the dwarves' appearance was rather characteristic than beautiful — dwarfesses were to the taste of many human connoisseurs quite attractive. Mixed marriages were encountered, though not very often (here arose complications of purely juridical character), and children from such marriages turned out, due to genetic closeness of races, quite successful, inherited dwarven longevity (getting at output two hundred to two hundred fifty against ordinary human one hundred fifty years) and strength, which in dwarves exceeded average-human by six-seven times, and at that this concerned women too. Hybrids were distinguished by less massiveness, higher stature and less calm temper. Overall they more resembled people and lived, due to their unbecoming-of-gnomes restlessness and inclination to adventures, most often in human settlements; and their offspring in turn bore quite insignificant trace of subterranean ancestors.

Gnomes themselves from generation to generation led the same measured, though not always free from risks and surprises, way of life. Skilful masters and most persistent workers, they coped quite easily with any difficult and dangerous work, for example in mineshafts. Costs of course were. Thus, these guys quite logically, on the level of centuries-long habit, almost did not use the word "last." "In the latest two hundred years, yes-yes" — mentally smiled the not-prone-to-superstition duke.

...So, the boy Artex would lead command... In Hans's gaze read approval mixed with disbelief. Well — these feelings could be understood. In Heavy Axe's memory was no case when anyone of the royal house of Ksarpathia distinguished himself by special courage, intellect and any interest in state affairs. Artex was the first who at least tried...

— So where is he now, I mean His Majesty? — inquired Hans, trying through the narrow shoulder of his old friend to discern whether the king was in his study. — Isn't he with you?

— His Majesty deigned to go dine — with smile replied the duke. — Young growing organism, you understand... And besides — he spent so much energy today on not revealing incognito and not blowing to dogs the whole elven delegation along with ambassador! So — the nephew needs to restore his strength... A couple hours, I think, will suffice him.

— Actually an hour should suffice — estimated the secretary general. The legendary dwarven attitude to questions of food — even with correction for wartime — once again amused the duke. Dwarves themselves could be extremely moderate and unpretentious if necessary, but in ordinary conditions (for maintaining in shape a powerful seven, sometimes nine-pud body!) — their meals were really something. At a dwarven feast, having stocked up on time and appetite, at least once in life worth being. True, having preliminarily — well calculated own forces...

— By the way, Mark, why do you call him nephew? Considering events of century-old prescription — I strongly suspect, you're not even relatives anymore for a long time.

— Well firstly, crown-princess Ellisun's adventures so far nobody proved... Though neither refuted, which on the whole — is unessential. Secondly — there's suspicion that with his bright general head my nephew owes precisely those very events. And thirdly — it's simply tradition. Formally our houses are still brotherly. And who is whose nephew — is simply determined by age. If older is the duke — then he's uncle, and the king nephew. If reverse — then reverse. Well, if same age, then we're outright brothers.

— People. Love complicating simple things — muttered the dwarf, raising eyes to heaven. — Fine, duke. The Subterranean Workers' Republic doesn't abandon friends in trouble and sacredly honors its allied obligations. And besides — we well remember how regiments of your grandfather perished, holding hordes of gnoblings, while our women and children retreated into safe caves' depths, and we pulled up fresh troops. Prepare to receive reinforcement. No later than tomorrow at noon you'll receive at least five operational legions. And I'll eat my beard if at least one of my boys retreats in battle with the foul intruders!

— Five operational legions?! — to say the duke was shocked meant to say nothing. Essentially, the PWR was sending out all available troops without consideration of mobilization. And since even one operational legion represented, essentially, a completely autonomous and lethal army — the Concordians could only be commiserated.

— Well you understand, I suddenly thought, maybe it makes sense not only to repel the attack but to solve the elven problem definitively. They won't calm down. Get hit, waste demographic resource, dump demographic pressure and crawl away licking wounds. And the way they breed — they'll restore in 50 years... Well and it'll all go anew. So — better we'll right away try to solve the problem in one fell swoop — Heavy Axe spoke slowly, weightily, considering each word from all sides. — By the way, maybe call someone of the eastern kings? They can't fail to understand that Ksarpathia is now buffer between them and the elves. Moreover — the only one capable of restraining them?

— Thanks for advice, old friend. Though — you know these paranoids... — sighed the duke heavily. — Well then — on that we'll settle. We'll provide reception of troops in the forward fortresses of the ring.

The dwarf majestically nodded his head, and his image in the copper disk crumbled into golden dust, immediately absorbed into the Communicator of Anton Flip — one of the duke's favorite personal artifacts...

Mark leaned on the table with elbows, put head on hands and with unseeing gaze stared somewhere through the Communicator. The old friend raised a really important question — does a race so dangerous to all neighbors as elves have the right to existence? Especially considering they came to this world from somewhere outside much later than people and even other ancient races.
