Chapter 2

In the arrival hall (also the reception hall) the delegation for meeting high guests was already loitering. The approaching steps of the duke served as a signal for all present — on-duty mages, guards, and ministry representatives — to hastily take their accustomed positions on either side of the wide semicircular steps approaching the throne. Since the arriving elven embassy was royal, that is performing "voice of the sovereign" functions — protocol required the duke's presence. The young monarch's presence at such a reception would already be overkill. Hence at the hall entrance Artex picturesquely wrapped his face in his cloak, symbolizing his "incognito" status. While in human politics this gesture was indeed purely symbolic (simply because all high-ranking persons knew each other one way or another "by face" and "cloak masking" really resolved nothing) — with elves the factor "all people look alike" worked, so the person under the cloak might well remain unrecognized. By the way, with mountain dwarves such ceremonies would have been altogether unnecessary. Dwarves simply don't bother with such trifles...

The duke seated himself on the throne just a minute before the transport seal glowed bluish. The on-duty mage-transporter raised his hands and muttered the reception formula. Over the seal arose a silver dome of transfer. On its sides distorted reflections of the departure point could be discerned. The duke's personal guard raised crossbows. The duke himself, as if casually, adjusted the Torosius pendant on his chest. Artex involuntarily tensed. From previous experience the king knew that from this moment and for the next five minutes — all types of throwing or magical weapons against his uncle were useless. Though such invulnerability had its weaknesses too — especially for those who forgot about such a trifle as a banal air supply inside the created force field... About which the monarch had the honor personally (and quite painfully) to verify some ten years ago. The dome shuddered once more and disappeared. In its place stood the Concordia delegation.

Anyone who has even once used the magical transport network's services knows that, as a rule, immediately after transfer a passenger experiences some disorientation. Dizziness of various degrees, accompanied by loss of balance and even less pleasant side effects — is a common thing. The elves, however, were apparently unfazed by the teleportation. At any rate they demonstrated no signs of disorientation. Noble bearing as well as unflashy grace of movement of the tall long-eared guests were evident. The retinue, in light and soft-looking robes slightly resembling drapery of ancient Greek tunics, in unison took the "on guard" position around the ambassador. The ambassador majestically took four (sacred number!) steps forward, after which no less majestically bowed his head before the duke. The duke's guards with practiced movement put crossbows in "shoulder arms" position and froze at attention. The duke himself, slightly birdlike tilted his head to his shoulder and questioningly raised an eyebrow.

From behind the ambassador with flying, dancing step appeared a very young elf maiden in finest tight multicolored tights. Coming out two steps before the ambassador, she with elusively fast, fluid motion lowered to one knee, slightly bowed her head, rose to her feet, threw her head up, after which with loud and ringing voice announced:

— His Excellency, ambassador of great Concordia Tuatha-de-Danaan, second leaf of Queen Runiel, possessor of the harbors of the Silver Moon, keeper of the wooden blade of the Throne, Spair-ta Quarandil!

Completing the introduction of her patron, the elven herald with the same gliding motion vanished into the ambassador's retinue. The initiative was picked up by the duke's herald. Unlike the common practice of selecting practically children as heralds with pure ringing voices — the duke had chosen for this purpose an old veteran sergeant who had previously fulfilled his military duty in the garrison stationed in the immediate vicinity of the palace. Such an original choice was far from accidental. As Lord Ariette repeatedly told friends afterwards, this extremely successful appointment allowed him to solve several tasks at once. First, this undoubtedly shocked guests who'd requested audience, breaking their patterns and making them appear before the cunning ruler of Golden Dol in not-too-good diplomatic form. Plus — the appearance of the brave herald in any case made an indelible impression and gave the duke, prone to non-standard solutions, the appearance of certain eccentricity. And finally, the duke got the opportunity to hear the sergeant's voice only at receptions of high-ranking guests and not for long, rather than under the windows of his own bedroom, daily and until noon from six in the morning.

So, toward the delegation stepped a beastly-looking herald, dimensions of a battle-hardened bear and with the face of a veteran who'd seen much, crossed by a deep scar from forehead to chin. The dapper herald's tunic was worn over ideally polished cuirass, and on his side hung a thoroughly unceremonial broadsword of terrifying size. But the strongest impression was made not even by this, but by the valorous hero's eyes. Eyes in which shone genuine inspiration and awareness of the full importance of his mission — moreover this expression grew as his gaze caught new and new details of armament of the involuntarily-closed-ranks elven retinue. Clearly stamping each step, imprinting his heel into the gold-pink marble floor — the herald came to the middle of the way between the duke and delegation (as if casually closing with his heroic shoulder-spread the duke, and partly Artex), after which opened his vast maw and announced:

— The ruler of the Duchy of Golden Dol, first protector of the kingdom, Lord Karldilier Markus Ariette am Golden Dol greets the ambassador and offers his entire embassy to accept his disposition and hospitality!

Literally from the first rumblings of the sergeant's bass, which on the battlefield as in the training camp overpowered any noise and if necessary blew away with directed wave especially troublesome privates — the elves got bad. Refined and exalted beings, connoisseurs of the beautiful including music — were stunned by the unreal stream of decibels falling on their finest hearing. The sergeant's voice, must be said, was clear, without particular wheezing. But so low, deep and loud that the ambassador barely had endurance not to plug his ears. Some of the elven retinue, as it turned out, didn't possess such diplomatic endurance, which the duke noted not without pleasure. The mages present in the hall — once again paid tribute to the audio-blockade available to them. To the right of the throne, in quarter-voice, Artex admiringly cursed.

The greeting protocol was observed, so now only remained to wait for what the ambassador would say.

— Your Grace. The Sovereign of Concordia Runiel the Wise, daughter of Enissar the Conqueror and Dariel the Beautiful — sends you her best wishes. Besides this, the Sovereign sends you her Voice — the elf held out to the duke a small mahogany box. Mark immediately and unmistakably recognized the famous "voice mail." Although the duke had not previously received such parcels, being an extremely erudite man — he had no particular problems understanding the principle of use. He placed the casket on the throne's wide armrest and carefully opened the lid.

The hall filled with the soft and enchanting voice of the elven ruler, speaking (just like Quarandil with her mini-herald) in common-human, with a light, only-adding-charm accent: "I greet you, duke possessing Golden Dol. Knowing you as a learned and wise man — I address you directly, avoiding unnecessary equivocations: Concordia wishes to expand. Your domain is great and abundant, while being populated rather sparsely. As a wise man — you cannot fail to understand that collision with Concordia's might will end in great blood for your land. Therefore I propose you voluntarily transfer under my hand the western quarter of your duchy. This will allow us to preserve peace and good-neighborly relations between our countries for not one decade and allow many of your subjects to survive. In sign of great mercy — we will not only let the population from transferred territories go, but render them aid in moving east to lands remaining under your sovereignty. Not wishing to spill unnecessary human blood — I rely on your wisdom and prudence, duke. I give you one day for reflection. The answer — pass with my ambassador. Remember, Concordia always gets what she wants. By war or peace — the choice is yours."

An oppressive silence hung in the hall. The duke sat leaning back on the carved high throne-back and half-closing his eyes. The fingers of triangle-folded hands performed an elusive wavy dance. Understanding what he was thinking was extremely hard. While the king, literally from under the cloak covering his face, had visible working jaw-muscles, and his gaze was ready to incinerate the delegation including ambassador and casket, no worse than the recent ovoid. Nevertheless, so far Artex managed to restrain himself. Trust in his uncle on complex political matters and his own desire to become something more than nominal king under a ruling duke told.

After another couple of minutes the duke silently rose from the throne. Personal guards lowered palms to blade handles. The elven retinue likewise apparently prepared for the worst. Maintaining the maximum possible pause in conditions, the duke quite peaceably approached the ambassador closer and sympathetically looked at him.

— What did you do so badly to displease the Sovereign, Second Leaf? Fine, this question, as I suspect, you won't answer. Troops are already at our border, I understand correctly? Waiting signal? Again you can not answer, I know this perfectly well myself. Well now — a very serious question was put to me, so — I need to think about it. All night at minimum. In general, we'll do this: you — are my guests. Whoever offends you — offends me. Rest, visit our city, for example our unique menagerie, by the way much honored by our monarch... — the duke from the corner of his eye admired how on the right the king flinched and reddened under his cloak — My servants will escort you to your chambers and provide all you need to spend time in comfort. And I'll give an answer in exactly one day. Weigh all pros and cons, meditate over the map...

— "Prepare troops..." — developed his thought the ambassador, with light ironic smirk not hiding however the involuntary respect in the gaze turned on Ariette. — Your Grace, it's very gratifying to me to see that you could handle the first and quite expected impulse. The Sovereign was not wrong supposing you a wise man. I fear only that she underestimated your courage.

— We'll talk about this later, Quarandil — the duke sighed. — I have full hands of business, as you understand. I appoint the next audience for tomorrow. An hour before the deadline appointed by the Sovereign of Concordia in the ultimatum.

The ambassador ceremonially inclined his head and, having taken four steps back, mingled with his retinue. To the elves came the senior majordomo and invited them to follow him to the apartments allocated for the embassy. The duke made a barely-noticeable hand movement ordering the hall cleared, waited until the last of his people left, who turned out to be two mage-technicians finally deactivating and blocking the diplomatic transport seal, after which wearily lowered his head onto his hand and, glancing at the king, inquired:

— Well and what will you say, ruler?

— Don't be ironic — grumbled the monarch, finally throwing off the bothersome cloak behind his back, and with wide steps crossing that part of the hall where the ill-fated elves had recently loomed. — I hope you don't intend to give them a piece of the Dol? If anything — I won't allow.

— And what? Smaller territory — we haven't mastered all the nearby territories. The duchy is huge. Most of our continent's kingdoms are substantially smaller. We'll reduce territory — easier to manage. Financial losses — not too scary. We're hardly in want. But — we'll preserve calm, lots of lives... Fine-fine, calm down, I'm joking, joking! — the duke jerked from the throne and patted the stunned-into-stupor sovereign on the shoulder. — Of course I won't give these long-eared upstarts an inch. Other thing is we haven't had war in a long time. Grown fat. And these — have already managed to annoy all their neighbors. On the other hand — this situation was awaited by all my ancestors, beginning with Azor Ariette.

— The first duke was so prescient? — the king was intrigued.

— Well, that or not is hard to say, but the "stone necklace" was begun by him. Strange the squinty-eyes didn't take this into account. OK, my king — you, it seems, wanted to enter history? As far as I remember — you had excellent results in "tactics of modern war"? Well then... — unhurriedly walking a few more steps across the hall, the duke turned around, stepped to his young ruler and lowered himself before him on one knee.

— I, your faithful vassal, am in trouble, oh my king. I appeal to your nobility and the suzerain's duty and ask you for help. By this I appeal for help with your blade and your troops!

— Uh... — stumbled for a moment the king. — By this I accept your request, my faithful servant, and let my blade be your blade. And your enemy — my enemy! Stand behind my shield, for your oath is my duty!.. — Artex took a breath, and with some delay clarified — Uncle, isn't it nothing that you have about twice as many troops as I have with all my other vassals combined???

— Nothing... I don't need your troops so much — the duke stood up and brushed his knees — as your bright head. You — will lead both your and my regiments into battle. Well and strategy — leave to me.
