Chapter 5

— Arrogant bitch!!!!!! Creeping she-snake!!!!!!!! Wood-grown-grafted creature!!!!!!! "Whoooosh!.." — over the duke's head flew a small but mana-saturated fireball, a moment later turning one of the doorway gargoyles into an abstractionist sculpture. The short duke ironically raised an eyebrow. The much taller king — with an instinctive defensive gesture raised his palm above his luxuriant chestnut mane (subject of his secret pride) — feverishly calculating whether to immediately demand the head of the raging beauty — or to calm her by less cruel and far more pleasant means.

Lady-Archoness Antea was an outstanding personage in every sense. To begin with, the irrepressible fireball-thrower possessed such external data that admiring contemplation could not be distracted by any destruction — even ones threatening to spoil the king's own appearance. Short, slim and graceful, with huge almond-shaped violet eyes and longest lashes, with seductively lush breasts and very prominent bottom — Antea was, perhaps, the most beautiful of Herzstadt ladies and deservedly enjoyed heightened male attention. Further — not uninteresting may be the fact that Lady Antea was the strongest mage of her generation in Ksarpathia, and possibly in all human kingdoms. Not superfluous also to mention that the archoness was distinguished by inherited from mother (born Mazari Al Sharif) temper of wild tigress and explosiveness of fulminating mercury.

Besides — Antea was to the marrow of bones a patriot of Ksarpathia and wildest chauvinist of human race.

Well and one small "insignificant" detail.

She was half-elf.

Yes, biologically this was impossible. But when your mother is unquestionably best biomage of Az-Kataban caliphate, and in the dungeon sits a by-miracle-captured elven mage of one of higher ranks (the guy was "drained" by Greedy Liana — one of quite amusing creations of Mazari) — even the impossible becomes possible. In general, the arcana Mazari thought of nothing better than conducting such experiment on herself. No, in principle — nothing such new she didn't invent. About possibility of crossing elf and human via biomagic was known long. The know-how consisted in that for first time crossed two so mighty wizards.

Additional piquancy to the experiment was given by the fact that during all time of Mazari's pregnancy the caliphate continued heroic resistance. Yes, elves attacked, and the ardent arcana as before applied all forces to ensure that few troops of Az-Kataban held at whatever cost — but sober and impartial analysis suggested that defeat was already not far, and the delicate position of the resistance leader left no hopes for new extraordinary biomagic developments which as not once happened could turn the battle's course and give caliphate some respite. Be that as it may — accelerate her pregnancy Mazari didn't, fairly fearing the child even so could inherit elven metabolism. As result by moment of expected birth Az-Kataban fell under Concordia's onslaught — which was in general not surprising since said caliphate was as we remember quite small. Surprising rather is that it held more than a year, and at that inflicted on elves quite noticeable for its scale losses. Mazari Al Sharif herself — safely left the country before its fall, in composition of caravan evacuating values of caliph. Left in unconscious state, since in consciousness she strove to fight and arrange to elves bloody massacre.

The caliph, having quite reasonably supposed that in current conditions the battle one way or another was lost — gathered all surviving including most valuable subjects, and hastily left the city, ordering to surrender it to enemy upon fact of departure of caravan. Elves with captured people didn't particularly tyrannize, so decision was though far from glory but quite reasonable.

Coming to herself already in emigration, Mazari Al Sharif arranged wild scandal to caliph, sent him to desert ifrits and left retinue. Characteristically — to detain her no one ventured. No, waving fists after fight the far-from-stupid sorceress didn't. Not one biomage even mightiest, and not one arcana even angriest — is capable alone to oppose elven army. As one twists it, one in field isn't warrior. And Mazari, having figured out who exactly would become object of Concordia's interest in nearest perspective, set off to Golden Dol. There she settled, occupying honorable place among mages close to duke; there she birthed her daughter.

Actually — precisely her, charming and unique by inborn magic properties Antea, the furious and ardent Mazari decided to make instrument of her revenge.

And the result exceeded most bold expectations. The girl-half-elf couldn't stand even quite friendly to people dwarves, and from one mention of elves began hissing and spitting. Sometimes even with poison if corresponding spell was at hand. In word, excess tolerance was clearly not characteristic vice of Antea... Against this background quite amusing seemed the fact that close friend and afterwards nearest assistant of Antea was best graduate of Phoenix University, sturdy, squat and gingerish Helga. Half-dwarf. Being almost completely insusceptible (and therefore not capable) to magic, Helga was distinguished by magnificent mathematical apparatus and in general quite considerable intellect. Result was academic knowledge in theormag, which gave Helga-Antea alliance not-weak advantages over other students. Dwarven scientific thoroughness and elven strength and talent for magic — this, frankly speaking, is still some combination. And Antea was ready to use these wonderful abilities at first hint of first convenient case.

By way, for daughter's sake (and her plans regarding her) mother went for almost incredible for mage and especially strong mage step. Yes, dynastic-ness among mages was more than widespread. And nevertheless — Mazari directed daughter to study not her specialty but combat mage.

In general — Lady Antea was raised chauvinist, patriot and simply remarkable girl. By twenty years amazingly beautiful graduate of Phoenix University, lady-bachelor Antea Al Sharif, obsessed with ideas of human race superiority and filled with unreal reserve of mana and spells — was pride and decoration of higher circles of Herzstadt. Then she was presented at court. The young sorceress at first sight pleased the then still very young duke. In Antea captivated everything — her loyalty to convictions, both directness and not-small mind — and of course knockout beauty and sexuality.

History doesn't tolerate subjunctive moods but objectively speaking — at that moment Antea, perhaps, had every chance to become duchess of Dol. If not for one "but." To huge regret of all participants of this drama (and to Antea's greater hatred of elves) — Lady Al Sharif Junior could under no circumstances participate in continuation of duke's lineage. Magic permitted such unusual and unnatural union as elf and human to take place, but alas even it was powerless against iron law of nature: interspecies hybrids are sterile. And with dukes Ariette unlike with some other sovereigns — attitude to their duty before state and vassals was more than serious, sometimes painfully serious. Duke needed heir. And wife capable of providing duchy with heir.

As result — young people had nothing left but to remain friends. Friends very good and close.

Years passed; the duke as befits Ariette became competent and enlightened ruler, married, acquired heir after all. Lady Antea — by her talent, labor and in considerable measure inventiveness of Helga, achieved title of Archoness of duchy (and de facto of whole state). Luckily, from her mother Antea inherited not only magic talent and southern beauty, but also human longevity, and paradoxically and to even greater luck — from elves she got complete absence of aging. As result — at her thirty-with-small Lady Antea remained same ardent young beauty.

Not surprising that on her from first meeting fell also king. Antea also wasn't indifferent to crowned suitor (told both king's appearance and his refined manners, and equally general spirit of valor manifesting in race of Amriette for first time in long time). Therefore the duke quite reasonably suspected that the appearance of his "nephew" this morning in Herzstadt was connected far not only with "visiting menagerie." Though — bed and heart adventures of these two were on current moment perhaps last that interested Mark...

Having concluded negotiations with Hans and made several most necessary orders, the duke accompanied by king headed to remote wing of castle. There in small but very beautiful hall (drowning in bouquets from royal orangeries) was Lady Antea's personal laboratory. At first words about morning delegation and ultimatum — most flowers scattered along walls, air filled with aromas of sparking lightnings, and toward Runiel the Wise rushed such epithets that the highly-honored elven sovereign would certainly have appreciated by merit if she could hear them...

Lady threw a couple more fireballs into another wall, exhaled decent stream of smoke from right nostril and with flexible cat-like motion jumped into chair. In narrowed violet eyes flared silver sparks, graceful fingers with unwomanly strength squeezed armrests of chair, leaving on crimson velvet deep marks from sharp violet little nails.

King and duke, exchanging glances, unhurriedly moved to archoness. "And she's damn seductive when angry!" — involuntarily noted to himself Artex, with difficulty tearing gaze from often-and-highly heaving, fantastic-size and admirable-form firm chest of magess and from luxurious waves of her amazing, black-and-gold (another side effect of hybridization) hair, currently also stirred up by application of several electric spells. Near chair appeared as from underground drawn Helga, silently shoving friend a goblet with wine.

On stern wide-cheekboned face of faithful confidante read everything she thought regarding the unrestraint and disorder created here by Antea, and clear was that from dressing-down the magess at the moment was saved only by presence of two crowned persons simultaneously.

Antea slightly guiltily glanced at freckled nose of her ginger girlfriend, and pouting lips turned to sovereigns.

— And anyway! I'm very offended at you, Mark! You should have! Should have let me incinerate that accursed casket!!! — duke slightly smiled in response and pulled out from inside his clothes the "Voice."

— You see, my lady, if you'd incinerated this poor casket — we wouldn't be able to study it carefully. Who knows what we might extract from this trinket.

— We're on formal terms long, Mark? — peremptorily raised brow the charming brawler. Duke passed "Voice" to Artex (who, it seemed, partly shared Antea's attitude to casket, and took it with some squeamishness) — and casually leaned on one of hall columns miraculously surviving after the archoness's emotional outburst.

— No, my joy, just it seemed to me that fireball flying at my head — is symbol of some cooling of your attitude to me.

— As if few of them flew at you over years we know each other! — snorted and laughed the magic hooligan.

— Well, still can't manage to get used — brazenly lied the duke.

King looked at Mark with sincere admiration. So virtuosically and quickly bring willful beauty into normal state — he himself so far not once managed!

Silver laughter of Antea still sounded under hall vaults, but in magess's eyes was already visible that with good disposition of spirit returned to her also her usual ability to think, and think coolly, clearly and swiftly.

— So, fine, Your Majesties, since I finally more or less calmed — let's sort out in order — Antea discovered in her hand goblet with wine and sipped it. On face reflected obvious admiration for bouquet, and she gratefully turned to red-haired half-dwarfess standing as gloomy idol half-turned to magess — Hel, thank you.

— Later we'll talk — uncompromisingly cut off friend. Antea nodded with warmest smile aiming to at least partially soften if not prevent brewing storm (which Helga was master of) — and setting aside goblet again turned to men.

— So — elves finally deigned to get to our country — cat eyes of half-elven narrowed evilly and flared with fire of unconcealed excitement. — Well then... Ultimatum expires tomorrow. Time — short. So — urgently we convene whole Big Circle. We can try to hire legionnaires from neighbors. Mark, Artex — will you allocate me funds for purchase of mage-mercenaries?

— Of course. With financing no problems — duke nodded.

— Excellent — Antea easily slipped from chair, silent decisive step rushed to small bureau in distant, half-hidden by portieres part of hall and began extracting from its unexpectedly capacious bowels folios and scrolls. — Now need to think about artifacts, apparently will have to raise mass production...

Outside window still was peace. Greened trees in extensive well-kept parks of Herzstadt, laughed children and shouted their unpretentious cheers merchants... And here in personal laboratory of Lady Antea — already went war. So far — without blood. But to blood remained quite little time.
