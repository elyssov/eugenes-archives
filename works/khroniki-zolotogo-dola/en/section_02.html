Chapter 1

Year 2013 of the Kingdoms Era. (230 from the Duchy's founding). Ksarpathia, Duchy of Golden Dol. Herzstadt. The Duke of Zlatodol's castle.

The study was located under the very roof of the central tower. It was very sunny and spacious. The huge arched windows around the circumference contributed, as did the tower's overall diameter, clearly evidencing the builder's noticeable gigantomania. The builder, actually, was the current Duke's grandfather. The young man currently sitting in a high-backed chair of dark wood with decorative spikes, feet in heavy boots on the table and picking at some artifact with a dagger — was athletically built and very handsome. Lush chestnut hair fell to his shoulders, framing a manly and willful, slightly weathered but very attractive face. The attractiveness was even enhanced by light stubble. A short, neat chestnut beard emphasized the beauty of the chin line. The light summer shirt was unlaced at the chest, revealing ideally pumped chest muscles. Bottomless aquamarine-saturated eyes examined the dagger blade penetrating ever deeper into the crack that divided exactly in two the stone egg covered with mysterious runes.

— Artex! Put down the ovoid of Mirax immediately!

At the first sounds of this quiet authoritative voice the young man involuntarily straightened up... Perhaps a bit more sharply than he should, considering the unconstrained initial pose in a rather low, deep chair — and especially the leg position. With the crash of breaking wood the chair toppled. Legs flew up to the vaulted, sun-flooded ceiling of the study. Artex himself — judging by everything, that was indeed the name of the long-haired owner of the egg and dagger — tried to avoid meeting the floor, but all he managed was to group himself and at least not crack his head on the luxurious marble slabs. The ill-fated ovoid of Mirax described an arc through the air and with a quiet splat landed right in the offered palm of the new character.

The newcomer was of medium height, age "slightly over 30." A somewhat pale complexion betrayed in him a man spending most of his life indoors. Calling him a beauty would, perhaps, be hard. An ordinary man, of which there are many. The figure didn't particularly differ in athleticism, so even under a well-chosen loose suit the characteristic thickening at the waist was clearly visible, familiar to all without exception people who constantly while away time at sedentary work. The Duke approached the table and carefully placed the ovoid in its holder, after which with some sarcasm turned to his already-extracted-from-the-remains-of-the-perished-chair king.

— Alive, Your Majesty?

— Not thanks to your prayers, Mark! — snapped the slightly crumpled and still apparently experiencing certain moments of his flight sovereign.

— Well why so... Prayers have nothing to do with it — Grand Duke Mark do Ariette am Golden Dol again removed the ovoid from its stand — but I, perhaps, did save Your Majesty from death. You see, my sovereign, if you'd managed to pick open this extremely cunning device of the great mage-mechanic Mirax, I fear something like this could have happened... The Duke took the two parts of the egg, pronounced a short phrase and separated them from the end pointed at the wall. From the formed crack into the tower wall struck a blindingly bright golden beam. Mark snapped the ovoid shut. On the wall where the beam had struck, granite masonry was clearly melted.

— You see, my crowned nephew — the Duke addressed the slightly stunned king — undoubtedly this device is locked by a complex spell unknown to you. But I'm not inclined to underestimate your strength and persistence. Did the blade enter a quarter-nail?

— Half — admitted the monarch.

— Hmm, then I did underestimate you. In that case, you were amazingly close to that wall's fate — the duke set the artifact back on the table. — By the way, what exactly are you doing in my study? I mean — I'm always glad to see Your Majesty, and generally I'm your faithful servant and all that... But what Dark Lord brought you to MY study?!?

— Well, how... — the king pondered slightly. — Went hunting... Then dropped by Countess do Allier's ball. Then — the lads and I decided, why not visit Herzstadt and look at your famous menagerie... And then I decided to visit my dear uncle. The servants told me you were busy somewhere with something, so I decided you were in your tower as usual ex-cre-pre-me-re-rimenting. So I went looking for you...

— Clear. Saw I wasn't there, on the table — a new artifact, immediately forgot all my warnings and joyfully started picking at it. My favorite nephew never changes!.. — sighed the duke heavily. Pushing aside with his foot what remained of the chair, he came up close to Artex.

— OK, confess, you simply got bored, and decided to briefly distract from feasts and hunts and engage in politics, am I right?

— Mark, you understand... I don't want to be like father. He just relaxed until his death. I want to enter the kingdom's history as a great king, not like my father — a king who died of overscrewing!

— By the way, many would envy!.. — the duke couldn't resist a jab.

— Let them repeat his feat then! — snapped the young king. — And I don't want to be lost against the background of yet another duke of Zlatodol. Don't misunderstand me — you're in your place, and our state could hardly have been luckier than with your whole family... But I want my own place too — and preferably not as the third little fir tree on the right!..

— The sovereign's wish is law for the vassal — the duke ceremonially bowed. — I think you have every chance to leave your mark in history. The key moment in this — is your desire, and it's present. Well and meanwhile — we should hurry, the royal embassy from Danaan is about to arrive, and my sources report that the matter on which these accursed elves are coming — is extremely serious. I think the very king's presence in this matter will only benefit all Ksarpathia.
