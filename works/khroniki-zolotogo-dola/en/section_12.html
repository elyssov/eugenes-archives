Chapter 11

Should say, Pablio wasn't workaholic. Few of subordinates were aware, but practically any free minute at work — Pablio slept. Other matter is he possessed wonderful ability to fall asleep and wake in moments, and besides — really free minutes he got so few that affect his effectiveness this his small weakness chance had none. Actually, if in absence of problems Quarte fell asleep, then with presence of them — transformed, filling with boiling energy. Inquisitive mind of general-inquisitor demanded actions and movement. Well and since under Inquisition's jurisdiction were "enemies secret and obvious," i.e. crimes magical and state — getting bored didn't have to.

However — just today, on eve of war, Quarte would prefer to get good sleep. To help in preparation more than already did — he definitely couldn't.

And had to that black-fox, as her behind back (and some in face) called friends, do in damned Tarzemean. No, Antea, undoubtedly was in her right. After all Paitza is Paitza, and without it, especially in wartime conditions — Arcana's powers for pronouncing sentences are more than sufficient. But elven talers!!!!!

Quarte turned in hands yellow, as if glowing-from-within little circle. Like everything made by hands of long-eared — it was work of art. Even on "eagle," where with normal nations adorned-by-varying-degree-of-crookedness coat of arms or profile of ruler, on this coin — was carved whole battle scene. Fineness of work struck. As well as its meaninglessness.

Pablio sighed, tossed coin in air and catching squeezed in fist. Behind coughed one of interrogators and Great Inquisitor turned aquiline nose to subordinate.

— My lord. We've investigated remains. Alas — not a single trace of body find is simply impossible. Merchant, apparently, simply ceased to exist. Pass, please, at occasion, my congratulations to Great Arcana. She's clearly making progress. Last time — my group managed to gather at least kilogram of remains.

Pablio crookedly smirked.

— With slaves matters are somewhat more interesting. One — likewise, fully disintegrated. Concurrently received damage residence of Lady Sharif, fence of residence and panel above public water column on Winter street. In general all on direct directrix of strike. If you, my general, are interested in my modest opinion — then I think panel isn't worth even repairing. I assure you, cut-out by force of magic of our highly-respected silhouette of slave-bodyguard in combat stance — looks much fresher than old mosaic depicting pastoral shepherd scenes. Especially since pipe turned out located very.... piquantly.

Second — comparatively whole. No, he of course dead and easily can be stuffed in small chest.... But — at any rate we collected practically every his piece. Well, except for pair-three kilos which judging by everything were gobbled by new cadaver of our respected Mazari. I mean that horror, due to which we learned composition of lieutenant Aurelius's dinner. You know, by my view it still remembers it once was Rianna — mustached and constantly grinning physiognomy of major Vasco shuddered.

Suppose, won't be superfluous to note Vasco was known impudent, brawler, bretter (true tried not to kill opponents but only inflict noticeable wounds). Besides he practically didn't recognize authorities (respecting only real abilities of person and not his rank), joked even at reports with duke and could come rushing to meeting without washing off other's lipstick and reeking of reactive exhaust of sparkling wines. And long ago, probably, should have been sent into retirement, or even put for warning in zindan for couple months (exclusively for his own good)...

But with all his asocial qualities, this hero was incredibly good expert. So good that for ten years already, being rude and joking left and right — managed to advance in service and grow in ranks. So now too...

— In principle — we've already restored his exact appearance which he possessed BEFORE close acquaintance with Mazari's remarkable creation, and two of my boys are already making inquiries at coaching inns of Herzstadt whether anyone saw these lads, and at same time Tarzemean merchant. Ahead of your question — department of taxes and duties already reported that over last week to us arrived five Tarzemeans, each, of course, with assaya slaves. Oh this cute Tarzem tradition... — Excellent work, Vasco. What about talers?

— Fresh mintage. Judging by scenes on heraldic side — minted in honor of events of times of seizure of Az-Akabar. Alchemical analysis isn't yet completed, but I suppose, this is typical "elven gold." With blood signature of rulers. Naturally, traces of previous owner on it aren't. You know these elven jokes yourself.

— I know. Anonymity of payment is provided. Other thing puzzles me — why elven talers? Surely they don't have in treasury ours or currency of some of neighbors? They do. So why talers? And most importantly — why does merchant need them?

— Don't know, your honor — dummily stretched at attention Vasco, frankly snitching. However — in dashing eyes of this type lit furious excitement.

— Don't clown around. Not time for it.

— Yes, monsignor. Well, let's try suck out problem from all sides. My assumption — trade deal?

— Doesn't fit — I already inquired at transport ministorium. Over last week wasn't single official international transfer between us and elves. And into zone of Stone Belt — Tarzemeans never since birth peeked. — Logical, what for them to catch there besides garrison lice? Good. Your stupid move?

— Hmm... Stupid move... Gut. Payment of information services? — Inquisitors applied old-as-world method of version weeding. Such intellectual ping-pong actually was quite effective method of situation analysis. Here in given case — beginning with frank delirium and rejecting it, inquisitors began crystallizing truth.

— Doesn't fit. Sum too significant. There enough to buy royal Buyan. — Well, Buyan you exaggerated, of course, if you knew how much duke shelled out for him — you wouldn't sleep nights from horror. But overall yes, admit. Merchant sees not more than ordinary person. For information can pay, but not so much. Your turn to be stupid.

— As you say, excellence. Diversion? In sense — merchant was ordered diversion?

— Curious... And this is already close. Though... And what can merchant do? Political murder? Well on any of key peers of Dol — need not two and not ten assayas. Personal guard, and for some personal might — allow not to bother. To duke merchant in general would never sneak up. Poison water supplies? Well, on way to collector him will take object guard. Yes and we'll discover poisoning before water gets into water-pipe. Already silent that such diversion a priori has no meaning at this stage of war.

— Fine, boss. Stumped. Your move.

— Merchant isn't at affairs. He held himself too freely. Too calmly came for his order. Too peacefully. I think, merchant sold somebody something. Something very valuable. And here this NotKnowWho — is our corrupt friend....

— And with talers he settled absolutely calmly, because merchant is foreign, from Tarzem, and they don't disdain anything, and also in transit, most likely... Beats version, boss! Well-up... Vasco extracted from pocket of broad-brimmed cloak oval pebble of San-Sinha. Comparatively new communication artifact from east, purchased about five years ago by duke — surprisingly quickly entered circulation. Small river stone, with carved on it runic code from one side and block of runes from other, allowed easily call possessor of analogous pebble. Sufficed only to type in correct sequence of runes on block — and further stone transferred voice of interlocutor — to bearer of second stone and vice versa. Only drawback was need for large amount of "support columns," beautiful in eastern style carved structures, providing serviceable work of mobile artifacts in radius of pair of miles from each column. Duke didn't bother with trifles and provided all key services of state, and at same time all more-or-less large cities with network of columns. Of course — this cost pretty penny, but now operational services became truly operational. Besides — at present moment in Phoenix University by duke's order work went on expansion of "address capacity" of columns, which would allow start renting new artifacts to population. This project promised substantially spur economy of duchy (and of all kingdom of course), and at same time return to treasury all funds invested earlier. Besides — mages from Phoenix talked of new modifications of artifact pebble, with help of which possibly will turn out transmit not only sound but even image. However — this was already clearly matter of future.

So, Vasco extracted pebble and typed code of one of his lads.

Couple seconds little stone neutrally cooed, waiting until, feeling vibration, took it in hand from pocket subscriber. Then it shuddered and in Vasco's ear sounded sleepy voice of one of his subordinates.

— Boss?

— Well certainly not your mommy, oh my sleepy friend. Time to get up — calls trumpet and duty before duchy! Well-quickly-quickly-quickly!

— Boss, well what are you. As if I can sleep at such time?

— You, straight like our common boss can sleep any time and any place, at that unlike him — even regularly get caught on this. And don't puff so resentfully. Let's better work. Specifically — I'm interested: what kind of luxury items were registered over last week at customs by entering Tarzem merchants? By such items should understand works of art, jewelry products and other objects having value equivalent to no less than ten thousand Ksarpathian marks? Special sign — there should also be declared female slave.

— How many-how many thousand?????? Ten???? Not bad, must say! About two of my annual salaries.... This, by chance, isn't living icon they sold?

— Living icon? Hmm... Here you check. Very likely variant.

— Yes well! Since long-eared captured her — not variant at all.

— And you all the same check.

Vasco touched rune breaking connection and took pebble from ear. Pablio — questioningly looked at subordinate.

— Now we'll check customs, on slave — we'll filter out all extra. Accordingly — will become clear what to look for.

— Excellent. And I, perhaps, will go talk with Mazari.

In Mazari's laboratory — over passed hour nothing changed. Same way — Mazari's helpers, with mechanistic precision held in suspended state orc. In far corner, sucked into nipple of food-dispenser that which once was Rianna. Pablio instinctively turned head. To be honest with himself — he would with pleasure sentence Mazari to auto-da-fé. Matter wasn't even in crimes of said (from law's standpoint — Mazari was impeccable). Just her creativity, speaking frankly — was against very nature. However — this would inevitably mean completely spoiled relations with Black-fox. And friendship this — Pablio very very valued.

Noticing entered, relaxedly hung (and why strain if escape you all same — won't manage? Easier and indeed rest and gather strength) in biomats' arms orc — raised head and defiantly stared at inquisitor. Pablio questioningly raised brow.

Orc coughed couple times, then spoke:

— Inquisitor. I heard conversation of mother and daughter. I heard your words. War is on threshold. Elves — voice of orc turned out sonorous and rumbling. Orc didn't speak but proclaimed.

— Can congratulate you, son of Desert. You have remarkable hearing — drily and boringly responded Quarte.

— I'm Chiduk. Chief of Alma branch. I also very don't like elves. We with you, human, had disagreements. You won, I — didn't. Now disagreements aren't. There's common enemy. Common enemy — gives birth to our friendship.

— I begin to feel some curiosity. Continue. — noted count Quarte.

— I propose alliance. I — give you word of loyalty, until elves are repelled or until your country falls. You — are inquisitor. You know firmness of orc's word.

— I know. Fine. We'll discuss this at my place. — Inquisitor raised voice — Vasco!

— Yes your honor? — mustached muzzle was sketched after a second.

— Lady Mazari — Pablio addressed hostess of cabinet, all this time silently observing what happened — Inquisition confiscates from you this detained, in connection with matter of state importance. Of course — you'll receive corresponding compensation.

Mazari majestically nodded. If law needed this orc — then he already belongs to law. However — to say Sharif Senior was upset would mean serious understatement.

— Vasco — escort our red-skinned friend to residence. Detention regime — 3B.

— Yes sir. Lady Mazari? — sorceress slightly raised brow and biomats' arms unclenched. Orc gracefully landed on feet, restored balance and turned to Sharif. Pablio slightly noticeably tensed, and quietly threw back openwork lid from skilfully built-into-ring Sun Stone. Ring began slightly heating.

Orc demonstratively slowly and calmly made three steps to Mazari. During this time — horror once being Rianna tore away from food and, turning head sideways, like huge insect stared at Chiduk.

Orc stopped, then bowed head and lowered on one knee.

— I'm heard about you Mother of Monsters. I consider it honor that fell into precisely your hands. And though your labors turned out fruitless — I'm glad I met Great.

Chiduk rose from knee and turned to Vasco.

— Let's go, friend-human. I'm ready.

Having watched strange couple leave — inquisitor was about to return to Mazari's interrogation. However — circumstances again worked against. At very second he opened mouth — from corridor sounded noise and crash. Count cursed and turned to door. Next moment — in laboratory doorway appeared dusty and out-of-breath messenger of city guard:

— My lord! Fire at coaching inn! Arson!
