Chapter 10

...The vibration was so strong that to crawl out of the tent — took more than five minutes. That something very bad was happening at the Kazbegi base camp Ivan understood immediately.

The shaking stopped literally as soon as he tore himself out of the tent. The camp was in turmoil. People were screaming in horror, flashlight beams were darting...

This lasted not long, since a couple of minutes later — flashlights were no longer necessary. Over the people rose and illuminated everything for tens of kilometres around an enormous column of flame. The witnesses of this awe-inspiring and lethal beauty had fractions of a second to take it in. And then — came the shockwave.

In the Caucasus it became as bright as day.

Kazbek — had awakened.

*

— ...So, Mr. President, the damage from the eruption of Kazbek is substantially lower than we might have hoped. Unpleasant but not fatal. We are watching the Siberian Traps closely, but, frankly, all evidence indicates that precisely in Siberia everything is quiet.

— What of the refugees?

— We are taking in the Japanese as best we can. After the sea calmed — we were able to lead out the fleet to gather survivors. We'll save all whom we can.

— Save them. Spare no resources. We need a landing on their islands. Perhaps we can help there too. No more of the Kuriles?

— Well, not all. But yes, part of the chain went underwater or was destroyed by tectonic strikes. Kunashir, it seems, held out, but nothing living is left on it. Tyatya showed itself in all its splendour: the main crater blew. The volcano itself is now in the range of a couple of hundred metres in height. Maybe — half a kilometre. We'll be able to report more precisely a bit later.

— Europe?

— Italy is in ruins. Their supervolcano is of course far from Yellowstone, but the Mediterranean got it badly. Spain and Greece took heavy losses. Some of the islands, according to the data we are receiving — were simply washed away.

— Andrei Viktorovich, how long, in your view, will this nightmare continue? — the President turned to the volcanologist.

— Mr. President, only the Lord can give you guarantees — we don't even have anything empirically comparable to draw upon. However, purely theoretically — I would no longer expect new upheavals. The geological, tectonic tension — has been released. I suppose minor aftershocks are possible, compared to events already past. But — no more than that.

— Thank you. Any refugees from there?

— Not yet — none. The EU is trying to save people by its own means.

— All right, prepare EMERCOM transport for dispatch. We'll have to help them.

The President leaned back and grew lost in thought...
