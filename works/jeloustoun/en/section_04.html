Chapter 4

Julie got lucky. At any rate, that's what it seemed to her at the moment when the US Bank Tower collapsed behind her back. The girl herself didn't keep her feet, landing not at all elegantly on her bottom. The freshly minted actress (even if in the role of a friend of the main heroine, who was killed in the first 20 minutes of the film) had been celebrating the wrap of shooting with friends and had only just gotten out of a taxi by her building when it began. The apartment in the centre, which the producer had rented for her (this, like the role, was part of a deal that suited Julie perfectly well), ceased to exist literally before her eyes. That she wasn't covered by debris from the collapsed building seemed to the girl a miracle. Not stupid, this generally pretty thing remembered that buildings in LA were generally built to be seismic-resistant; so the very fact of their collapse — already qualified as something supernatural... ...The next shock, or, more accurately, series of shocks, simply rolled her across the asphalt like a doll thrown into a concrete mixer. She twisted her ankle, scraped her elbows and knees, and painfully struck her head against a parked car, but on the whole was intact. The pause lasted half a minute, during which Julie managed to take stock of the situation and grab onto the nearest lamp post with all her might, in the hope that it at least would serve as an anchor... ...The new shock was not a shock. Rather, it resembled a falling sensation. The girl's vestibular apparatus made her feel as if on a giant amusement ride with seats falling from height. The fires that had broken out here and there and were already blazing (gas was burning, petrol stations, fuel storage) made it possible to assess the degree of destruction. The city, in fact, no longer existed. Only now did Julie realise that she couldn't hear anything. More precisely, not quite anything. She heard with her entire body the low-frequency, gradually rising rumble. Apparently the blow had damaged her hearing, and she perceived the vibrations with her whole body — and these vibrations were intensifying. And also her vestibular system gave a renewed signal, prompting her that the girl was moving with an ever-increasing acceleration. Still not understanding what was happening — the girl forced herself to stand by an act of will, and protecting her ankle, stepped onto the street... To see a torrent of water tearing toward her, flooding the streets. It wasn't even a tsunami. The city was simply being plunged into the abyss, carried there by the oceanic plate to which it belonged... Julie didn't even try to save herself. She let go of the post, stepped to meet the flow, raised her head and laughed. If at this moment she had been caught on even a single camera — the world would have learned what it had lost, perhaps, one of the greatest actresses in history. This laugh and this welcoming of death were so sincere that in the last second of her life Julia even felt a certain relief...
