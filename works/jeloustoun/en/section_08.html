Chapter 8

Japan was both burning and drowning simultaneously. The Pacific Ring of Fire, awakened in the wake of Yellowstone, was raging, adding nightmare to the already galloping end of the world. In fact, the entire Pacific plate had been set in motion, having ridden over Juan de Fuca and collided with the North American plate. On geological scales, the eruption of Yellowstone was, of course, almost negligible. But it became the catalyst for a tectonic "discharge." And this discharge made itself known. First to reach the Islands was an earthquake. Magnitude 8 on the Japanese 7-point scale. Earthquake-resistant skyscrapers folded, screwing into themselves (their seismic-damping mechanisms had an effect); ordinary buildings — simply ceased to exist. People were smeared against ruins and stones. Those who survived were overtaken by a tsunami wave. However, here it would be more apt to write Tsunami, since waves equal to this had never crashed upon the land of Yamato... As a result, the centre of Honshu was being engulfed, ever widening, by raging fires. The Pacific coast of the country, meanwhile, was covered with boiling water, stubbornly pressing further, as if only to extinguish the fire. From the eastern coast of the country, boats and ships were casting off into the stormy Sea of Japan. Strangely enough, they were not headed for Busan, or Gangneung. Most of them — were heading for Vladivostok. The Japanese refugees understood that the enormous continental expanses of Russia might prove their only salvation.

The Far Eastern Border Service of Russia — was raised by full alarm. Not to catch people. To save them. Today — it was not a matter of state borders. Only of lives — as many as could be wrested from the elements that had gone mad.

Those remaining in the country fought desperately for lives — their own and their loved ones'. Alas, for now the scales were not tipping in their favour...

What was happening in the Philippines, Indonesia, and Malaysia was practically impossible to describe. A powerful tsunami had struck them too. The Philippines were simply washed away — the group of scattered islands had proved, unfortunately, an insufficient barrier, especially considering that literally in the centre of the archipelago a new underground tremor of off-the-charts magnitude had struck. In Indonesia — all the volcanoes had awakened at once...

...The world was ablaze...
