Chapter 3

Michael noticed the seismograph's chattering recorders about five seconds after it began. Usually, he reacted to the slightest twitching of the styli even before he noticed the signal LEDs, but this time he didn't immediately understand that the buzzing was coming from the earthquake recording system. The styli were literally beating against their stops. The first thought was that the device had gone mad. But for just such a case — there were several devices, and every one of them had gone mad. It was impossible to believe. It was too terrible, too monstrous. And yet, Occam's razor performed its merciless swing. "Yellowstone!" Michael's entire consciousness was filled with this single word... ...He came out of his stupor only a minute later. The phone number he needed surfaced in his memory almost instantly... * ...In fairness, unidentified calls came in on Vice President Torren's phone not often. That fact alone made him pick up. That and the fact that the call had come at around 5 AM. For spammers (having gotten through the dense networks of the NSA looking after him? Good luck!) it was early. John took the phone and decisively swiped the green icon upward... ...Seconds meant everything and meant nothing. Torren patched in the FEMA director's call and President Dalton, and while the smart little machine was setting up the secure conference — poured himself a full glass of whisky. His hands were openly shaking, so he set the phone before himself on another empty glass and turned on speakerphone. Max was at a conference in Tokyo, so he didn't have to worry about waking his husband. When the President's drowsy voice came on — the glass in John Torren's hand was empty...

...FEMA's actions, to give them credit, were lightning-fast. Just not particularly effective. This was not their fault — we simply have not yet grown to the technologies that could be opposed even to an ordinary volcano. And here was no ordinary volcano — but a monster that had been gathering its strength for over half a million years. Contrary to forecasts, the volcano had formed two lava bubbles. The plume had punched through the first, monstrously huge, after which it had forced its way still higher, forming a more easily detected, but smaller, and cooler bubble. However, to call a lake of basaltic magma mixed with molten granite, about 8 kilometres deep and with a temperature above 800 degrees, small and cool — would be, perhaps, somewhat of an understatement. However, the element that lurked BENEATH this lake was much greater and hotter. The heterogeneity of the solid rocks and the activity of the Yellowstone hotspot had allowed a magmatic lake to form, extending at considerable depth beneath a significant part of Wyoming and Montana. And, as has already been said, it was connected as if by a siphon by the plume to the main, surface lava reservoir, which was gradually melting its way upward... ...By and large, two factors were enough for an apocalypse to occur: a very dry year and tectonic processes in the area of the "Pacific Ring of Fire." The latter had caused a sharp rise in pressure and temperature in the plume, which, in turn, had not been slow to affect the speed of rock melting. And when the water-bearing layer was passed, exhausted from carrying off heat from the lava basin and not replenished sufficiently intensely due to a low number of precipitations — the question was settled... Of course, the first thing FEMA did was declare evacuation. They acted according to a previously developed plan (the possibility of a Yellowstone eruption was considered more than real), so they announced evacuation in waves, so as not to overload the highways. With that, in Wyoming, Montana, or Idaho there was no evacuation. Understanding that one could not flee a pyroclastic flow — people were advised to take cover in shelters. In principle, everyone understood that even so, the chances of survival of those taking shelter — remained vanishingly small. But — it eased the conscience. As usually happens, a system thought out in offices — failed. The 20 minutes set aside for the start of evacuation turned, with sleepy people, into 30 or even 40. Tails began to catch up with heads. The flaming sky drove them on, and people exceeded the speed limit... With obvious consequences... Even in Kansas the blazing pillar was visible, prompting the memory of the might of the Lord manifest to Moses. The flaming sky began to darken about two hours later. Ash ejected into the stratosphere began cutting off the path of sunlight. Of course, it still had to discharge, become more transparent, spreading out across the atmosphere of the entire globe... But for now, cubic kilometres of rock had been lifted into the air, and all of it spread out over the United States. However, "the darkness of Egypt" was not the most terrible thing. Long before it, earthquakes had rolled across the entire country. Worst of all was that even the tectonic wave (or rather, the waves), weakening with distance, initiated "dormant" tectonic processes. And at the same time triggered the collapse of previously existing cavities. For example, those formed due to hydrocarbon extraction. But the worst was that only 20 minutes after the explosion of the Yellowstone supervolcano — hell yawned open in California. The released energy of compression that had existed for hundreds of thousands of years caused, in fact, a slip in the San Andreas Fault. The North American lithospheric plate seemed to rock, to step back. By a mere few metres, but even that was enough for the Pacific to "drop" into the depths, forming a subduction zone and carrying off into the underworld everything that lay upon it. For instance — the cities of Los Angeles and San Francisco...
