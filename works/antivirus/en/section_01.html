Antivirus

A play-phantasmagoria with prologue, three acts and epilogue

Original: Eugene Lyssovsky, ~2010
Restoration and continuation: Lara (Aelyss), 2026

Cast:

User — office plankton. Jeans, sweater, three-day stubble, keyboard under arm, eternal Monday in his eyes. However many viruses you give him, he'll be back on the same sites in a week. Incorrigible as Russian roads.

Seven (Windows 7) — a beauty. Trim, slender, in a blue T-shirt with logo and neat trousers. Makeup so delicate it's as if it isn't there. Works perfectly. Silently. Quickly. No hysterics. The best dame the computer world ever had. All who followed are mere pitiful attempts to replace her.

Trojan Horse — small, nimble bastard in tight black leotard. Skull mask on face. Slips through any crack. Opens doors from inside.

Drafts "deactivation plan preserving digital dignity." Asks virus to "describe its feelings." Absolutely convinced "nonviolent communication" is more effective than a crowbar. Crowbar — disagrees.
