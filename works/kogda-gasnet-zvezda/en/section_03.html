Chapter 3

Nearly all summer, the weather in Moscow held at something more like autumn-spring. The thermometer never once rose above 20 degrees. By the end of August, things got truly bizarre: at noon, the sun shone as if through a dense haze. In the mornings, though it rose at the astronomically correct time, it didn't get properly light until about an hour and a half later. On the other hand, for Margo, with her nocturnal lifestyle, this was rather convenient. At least it let her sleep in after a night session at the club without the bright sun glaring straight into her eyes. In any case, such changes in the weather couldn't go entirely unnoticed. Rumors circulated among the people about climate change, about another American trick ruining the weather either with HAARP or an unmanned shuttle. Margo laughed at the fools. Before becoming a dancer at the 'Naked Passion' club, she'd managed two years at the Russian State Hydrometeorological University, so she had her own opinion about what was happening. Though weather matters barely figured in her range of life interests. The main thing was that the Maldives, where Artur had promised to take her for 'a breath of fresh air,' would be warm and cozy....

An address to the nation by the President of Russia, Vladimir Evgenievich Sheremetiev!! My friends! Fellow citizens! Brothers and sisters. Fate has placed our country in my hands during a time of severe trials. We have liberated it from comprador elements, but alas, our trials are not over. Today, a threat hangs over the entire planet. We do not yet know what is happening. Our scientists are working on it. Nor do we know when the process will reverse. We know one thing for certain - our star is still very young, so this process cannot last forever. Nevertheless, we are all entering a period of severe trials. We should expect an extremely harsh winter, and possibly a very cold summer. You have been listening to the presidential address of the President of Russia. Beep!'...

...Liao Kan, General Secretary of the CPC, perhaps the youngest leader in the country's entire communist history, was fingering jade prayer beads. The ancient imperial relic, one of the symbols of the Mandate of Heaven, slid pleasantly between his fingers in an endless flow. The symbol was in his hands. But the mandate, it seemed, heaven had revoked. And apparently for good. The Russians and the Yankees had fed their people a line, but the advanced science of the Middle Kingdom had already given its answer - the 'cloud' around the photosphere was growing denser by the day, as if drawing strength from the sun, and judging by the dynamics, it had no intention of dissipating. Moreover, it was already quite observable through instrumental methods. Liao sighed heavily. The barbarians' reasoning was entirely understandable. Panic now would bring an end to civilization in days, not months, and it was no longer possible to simply not answer the question 'what's wrong with the sun.' The beads completed another circuit. Liao looked again at the papers on his desk. The wisdom of his predecessors, and above all Chairman Mao, had once again manifested in China's success: the underground cities prepared in the Tien Shan and Himalayan mountains, built in case of nuclear war, were now to become the foundation for the survival of the best of the Chinese nation. At the very start of the crisis, millions of workers had been thrown into tunnel-boring operations. Losses and costs were deemed irrelevant. Especially since sources in the US and Russia confirmed that the barbarians were engaged in exactly the same thing. Biological, transgenic experiments, conducted covertly for over 40 years (and occasionally leaking to the surface in the form of 'cat-dogs' or 'Hong Kong mermaids' in the early century), had reached a level where they could easily adapt people to underground living for an unlimited time. In the red folder on the Secretary's desk lay a top-secret report listing the minor modifications planned for the evacuees: enhanced photosensitivity of the retina for life in semi-darkness, increased subcutaneous fat norms to reduce heating requirements, integration of certain bovine genes allowing the female population to be used, among other things, for producing nutritious milk (the modifications primarily concerned optimizing the composition of the product and output norms and overall lactation duration - now a woman could produce up to five liters of milk per day, and lactation ceased only with severe malnutrition. Naturally, this led to accelerated depletion of the woman's body and guaranteed gigantomastia (in fact, one experimental leak had even made it into the Guinness Book of Records, though fortunately without exposing details of the transgenic experiments), but on the other hand, this eliminated the need to spend resources on keeping cows or goats, and the problem of plant-based animal feed was set to become especially acute. Moreover, supplying millions of people with milk could only be solved by maintaining correspondingly large herds. And including milk in the diet was an obvious step that would largely standardize and simplify nutrition without sacrificing its value. Relatedly, a parallel biomodification involved adapting the population to lactose consumption (which was, generally speaking, a problem for the Chinese, though among the evacuees, lactose intolerance was significantly less common - this was because the evacuees, for the most part, belonged to the more affluent social strata whose diets had included lactose from early childhood). The red folder also proposed implanting luminophore genes so that future underground dwellers wouldn't face significant difficulties finding each other in yet-unlit tunnels... In total, no fewer than 20 different modifications to ordinary human DNA were planned. All, according to the research specialists, had been tested, refined and were absolutely safe for recipients. A grim and ironic thought came to Liao - that the Middle Kingdom was, at this very moment, by his own hand, being transformed into the Under-Mountain Kingdom. He gazed thoughtfully at the red folder for another couple of minutes, then shifted his gaze to the imperial prayer beads. A shadow crossed his face. Heaven revoked the mandate? Very well, then we'll take it ourselves. Liao Kan hurled the precious jade prayer beads against the stone wall of the study, and while the greenish, seemingly luminous spheres bounced across the floor, he pulled the red folder toward him with a sharp motion and signed with a sweeping hand, authorizing mandatory gene therapy in the selected areas....

Her son ran out into the street, and Olga let out a sob and slid down the wall. Yesterday she had accidentally overheard her neighbor, the state security colonel, talking with his wife. The colonel had told his wife that he'd given away their places to a family with children - they still had life ahead of them, while he and his wife were practically old. Rumors that the government was obviously hiding something had been circulating for some time - there were stories about enormous shelters made from mines, equipped with nuclear reactors for autonomy, about people disappearing every day (the latter was actually true - for instance, at Olga's workplace, a young and very promising manager had suddenly not shown up for work one day. No phone answered, and the department head who stopped by after work (they lived on neighboring streets) found a completely empty apartment, from which, according to neighbors, everyone had moved out during the night. Adding to the intrigue was the fact that this employee's husband was some kind of incredibly secret and brilliant defense physicist or something like that). In short, the colonel's conversation was the last straw. It seemed the sun had no intention of warming up again. At least not in the foreseeable future. Combined with the first snowfall, which came in mid-September, the forecasts looked rather bleak. Olga pictured Mishka with his eyes frozen to icicles and choked on her tears... ...Alisa cried quietly the entire ride from the airport. Svetlana took her onto her lap, pressed the girl's head to her shoulder, stroked her hair and whispered something comforting... And the train rolled on, unhurried, toward Zheleznogorsk, the new capital of the country, leaving behind millions of doomed inhabitants of the planet... Sergei smoked cigarette after cigarette, knowing that the moment the blast doors closed behind him, the bad habit would have to be urgently abandoned. The Seleznyovs had decided everything in a single day. The next day, Sergei and Svetlana and the pale Igor and Elena, using Igor's connections, had finalized Alisa's adoption by mutual consent. Thus, on the day of departure, a silent and uncharacteristically serious Alisa with a small backpack and a laptop joined Sergei and Svetlana. The girl held it together all the way to the airport, and only when they transferred to the special train that would take them deep into the underground complex did the child break down and burst into tears....
