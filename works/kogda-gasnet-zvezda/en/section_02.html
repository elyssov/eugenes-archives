Chapter 2

  • So, as I understand it, the situation is critical. Against all reason, our star is dying. - President Sheremetiev drummed the fingers of his left hand on the tabletop. His right hand, ever since the coup, hung in a sling across his chest. He could use it somewhat by now, but full recovery was still years away. Sergei Krasin nodded and flicked another file from his tablet to the president's workstation.

Sheremetiev scanned the graph, took another sip of coffee from his famously plain tin mug - known to the entire country - and turned to his aide-de-camp:

  • Volkov, assemble the full cabinet, everyone you can reach. The rest I want on the secure video link within the hour. Tell them the classification is absolute. Furthermore - I don't exist for anyone today. Disturb me only if nuclear war breaks out. All meetings, negotiations, et cetera - scrub them. The Patriarch, the Mufti and the Rabbi - show them the door. I was planning to put them in their place anyway, and now I have all the more reason... Tell them the president has sunk into profound misanthropy upon grasping the full weight and neglect of the burden he has shouldered. - the aide and Sergei couldn't help but snicker. Judging by the smirk, the president had expected that reaction:
  • When you're done, have some light wine sent to the oak room. You understand, the subject calls for it...

I'll be there in about ten minutes.. Oh, and have all the electronics set up in there. When Major Volkov left, the president wearily rubbed his forehead and looked at Sergei.

  • Tell me, Sergei Pavlovich, why does everything in our lives always turn out like this? The moment we set about putting the house in order, the whole village goes up in flames... And we had such plans! Such projects getting off the ground! We'd only just finished the purge... God... Is there any chance the sun might reignite?
  • Judging by the dynamics, Vladimir Evgenievich, no chance at all. Plus, we still don't understand what processes are at play. It looks as though something is siphoning off the solar radiation. And doing so more aggressively with each passing day....
  • Well then, it's fate. At least our predecessors in this situation would have definitely fumbled everything, then ensured the rescue of a dozen or so oligarchs and their inner circle, leaving the entire nation to fend for itself. Say what you will, but we seized the reins just in time...

...The government's gone completely mad! Meggy, since when do they send brand-new subs to the scrapyard???? - Ray was clearly out of sorts, already guzzling his sixth bottle of beer with no intention of stopping. Though given what he was telling her, the reasons were sound enough, so Meggy, as a faithful and loving wife should, went to the kitchen and brought the men another pack of Bud.

  • Guys, this is classified, you understand? But NSDF needs to know! Today, with these very hands, I was cutting up the 'John Warner'!!!! They launched it just a couple of years ago!!!! I personally remember the launch!!!! - Raymond Walter Baskett, Wilmington shipyard worker, waved his hand in despair and latched onto his bottle. His old buddy Michael, a trucker and fellow active member of the nationalist-patriotic group 'National Self Defense Force,' shook his head in disbelief and tried to focus his glazed eyes on the beer pack. The third drinking companion was Ray's new neighbor, who had ended up in the group owing to his extremely nationalistic views and love of all manner of weaponry. Unlike his drinking companions, he was more pretending to be drunk than actually being so. And the news he'd just heard required urgent relay to his cell leader. As Prophet Connor rightly said - the corrupt communists, led by a woman, had finally decided to openly surrender the country to the resurrected Bolsheviks!

...Thus, the decision is made, comrades. To summarize: we urgently begin tunneling operations in Zheleznogorsk, proceed with the conversion of major coal and other mines with enclosed extraction methods. State Security together with the Ministry of Science compile lists of all personnel critical to us. Full secrecy. State Reserve - distribute everything you have to the underground residential complexes as they become ready. Further - communications must be maintained under any conditions. This also needs to be worked out and organized. As Sergei has already warned us - we'll no longer have an ionosphere and consequently no ozone layer. So, apparently, we'll need to use relay stations for communication between individual shelters. The Ministry of Heavy Industry must prepare crawler vehicle designs as quickly as possible for maintaining links between shelters. So, comrades, if we try hard enough, we can save 5% of the population. At least 5! - The President surveyed everyone present in the room. Both in person and virtually. The entire ministerial apparatus, by old habit, sported military insignia and bearing. And now they simply faced a new enemy. A serious and terrifying one. But the president had ordered them to defeat it. And orders must be carried out. So, the moment Sheremetiev nodded, releasing his associates and greenlighting the plans just worked out, the wheels of the state machine spun to full power. In keeping with the tradition already established by the new government, the ministers began acting before they'd even left the conference room. Phones chirped, short, precise orders rained down. The president turned to Sergei:

  • Sergei Pavlovich, naturally, you get a place in the shelter. Along with your family. And simultaneously you're going on garrison duty. In short, you have one week to settle all your affairs. After that, you'll be contacted and everything will be arranged.

...Intelligence reports a sharp increase in Russian activity. Their government freight transport activity has increased more than tenfold in three days. Virtually the entire private transport market is currently chartered by the Kremlin.

  • That's what we expected. The Russians couldn't possibly miss what's happening. How are our preparations going?
  • Hmm... we crashed the Tokyo and Beijing stock exchanges. Triggered a sharp spike in meat and grain prices. The Europeans are getting nervous too. However, if we can avoid leaks for at least another month, the food we've purchased will already have been shipped, and then - let the chips fall where they may.
  • It looks like we'll need to talk to the bears soon. Otherwise we risk a leak after all.
  • Madam President, the Kremlin on the secure line!
  • Speak of the devil. Patch it through to the conference room. - Sara Kennedy leaned forward toward the screen, which was just displaying the image of the Kremlin's oak study, once belonging to Stalin and now occupied by the new president.
  • Good afternoon, colleague - Vladimir Sheremetiev greeted her somewhat outside protocol (damn those unpronounceable Russian names!).
  • Hi. - Sara reciprocated.
  • I believe you know perfectly well why I've called? It's rather obvious from your economic policy on the Asian markets.

President Kennedy buried her nose in a paper handed to her from behind by an aide, then raised her eyebrows in surprise:

  • You know, judging by your market activity, it's even more obvious! Tell me - what do you need three gold mines in South Africa for??? And at such outlandish prices????
  • Oh, that's simple. Geothermal heat, plus hundreds of kilometers of tunnels. In total, we can relocate up to a million people there.
  • Interesting, and the locals won't object?
  • Well, under current conditions, the locals' objections interest me very little.
  • And what does interest you a great deal?
  • Coordinating efforts and actions with you. Panic would serve neither Moscow nor Washington.
  • Agreed. And what do you propose?
  • Create a coordination schedule....

...Unfortunately, I can't decide who gets saved. But! I have a chance to take your Alisa to the shelter. - Sergei looked guiltily into the eyes of Professor Seleznyov and his son. The old man chewed his lips, lost in thought... Igor Seleznyov blinked once, twice... Then cast a glance toward the kitchen, where his wife and daughter were cooking something for the men, not even suspecting that right now their fate was being decided. Igor closed his eyes, swallowed, then slowly opened them.

  • Adoption. Right? Our family has no chance. Who needs lawyers who don't set the world on fire, and housewives? Father is too old to be selected. And astronomers aren't exactly in demand under these conditions either.
  • I don't know, Igor. I don't know what the selection criteria will be. I got a family spot probably just by inertia.
  • I understand, Sergei Pavlovich. And I'm very grateful. Yes... We agree, of course.... I need to talk to my wife...
  • I trust I don't need to remind you of the utmost secrecy?
  • Yes, that's in our interest too.... Give me a minute..

Igor stood up. His tall frame was hunched, his head bowed. After a short while he gathered his courage, lifted his head and walked with firm steps into the kitchen. A couple of minutes later, Alisa came bounding out. A cheerful ball of energy, in her signature red overalls, golden-haired and blue-eyed - the image of her mother. First thing, she jumped into the old mid-twentieth-century armchair, spun around in it, then gave her grandfather a mischievous look and, putting on the most serious face she could muster, inquired:

  • Adults, what's new? - even given the tension of the moment, Sergei couldn't help but laugh. The professor held back too, though his eyes glistened treacherously...

...The 'Komsomolskaya' mine looked like a disturbed beehive. Just a week ago, several train loads packed with every kind of equipment had arrived, and along with them, some operatives from State Security. They immediately began some kind of total screening of the miners. In practice, at most two out of three kept their jobs. Those who were let go, however, weren't abandoned. From day one they began evacuating them to central Russia. It should be noted that those who remained, to the surprise of the dismissed, after their conversation with Security not only showed no joy at keeping their positions (and rumor had it they'd received some unheard-of bonuses), but on the contrary grew so grim-faced it was as if someone had died, and virtually ceased communicating with their dismissed colleagues. Though, after their meetings with Security, they barely showed up at home either, every single one suddenly becoming a Stakhanovite. They lived in the mines day and night... One odd thing, though - they were now bringing up to the surface not quality anthracite, but mostly waste rock, albeit in absolutely staggering quantities. In fact, by all accounts, something similar was happening at most other major mines. The final straw in the Vorkuta conspiracy theories was an armored train bearing radiation symbols, escorted by a powerful military detachment including combat helicopters.... And a month later, the answer to all questions arrived. A terrible and hopeless answer...
