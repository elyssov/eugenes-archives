Chapter 5

Consciousness returned slowly and painfully. Her head buzzed, her whole body was battered. There were also some new, strange sensations. They were not unpleasant, rather the opposite... But they evoked memories. Arda nearly cried out in horror when for a fraction of a second she felt the smell of the Mon'keigh and saw, as if in real life, the hideous face... However, the horror retreated, leaving emptiness filled with a pleasure unknown to her before. The sensation was so all-consuming... And then the universe exploded, and from non-existence appeared the face of another... Mon'keigh? This time the vision was not fleeting — the face of this... alien... was... different? From it grew none of those ugly, hard hairs as on the previous ones, and it smelled very pleasantly. He did not resemble her countrymen-Eldar either, but, unlike the first Mon'keigh, was not perceived as ugly. Perhaps... Perhaps even the opposite. The face was harder than the Eldar's, the chin noticeably heavier and not as sharply pointed... But this evoked rather a subconscious attraction. Arda finally figured out which word was on the tip of her tongue: "masculinity." Amusingly, it had come from the lexicon of the Exodites and was practically unknown among the inhabitants of the Craftworlds. Her head had finally cleared enough to open her eyes. At the same time she focused herself, preparing to fight, but not to allow a repetition of what had happened: yes, it had been... Incredibly beautiful... But at the same time it had been vile, shameful, dirty, and, what was much worse — too compelling. As if some kind of crack had arisen in her. To allow it to widen was unthinkable. Meditations, self-discipline... Spiritual wounds — could be healed. One needed only time. Throwing open her eyes, she discovered she was in some kind of chamber with a wooden-looking ceiling, richly decorated with carvings. The chamber was bathed in soft, warm, fortunately not cutting light. There was no one nearby. Passing her hands around herself and listening to her sensations, she understood that she was lying without clothing, beneath some soft fabric, on a soft, perhaps even too soft, couch. It was warm. Not hot, rather very comfortable. With one motion Arda flowed into a sitting position with her legs dangling. The couch turned out to be a little low for a tall Eldar, so she had to spread her legs slightly. The room was small, with walls covered in carved panels. The room was illuminated by an artfully made chandelier. One of the walls was for the most part a huge porthole, beyond which spread open space. Especially enchanting and gaze-arresting was a swirling nebula nearby. Beside the couch — stood a wooden table. Someone had set it while she slept. Copper dishes with unknown plant fruits. Three copper goblets stood there, with various liquids: one was filled, it seemed, with water, the second with some weak alcohol, the third, evidently, contained the juice of some fruits. Arda slaked her thirst with water, thought for a bit, and decided after all to nibble at the fruits. When she had emptied the dish (the fruits at the Mon'keigh's turned out to be quite edible) — the door to her room opened. On the threshold stood the Mon'keigh from her last memory. Suppressing the first reaction (to spring at the opponent, take him out of action, and flee), the girl boldly looked her guest in the eye. — I welcome you aboard my ship, Eldar. I regret that circumstances took such a sorrowful turn. However, since you have survived our first meeting, I think I can offer you my hospitality... For some time — the Mon'keigh spoke her dialect not too well, but quite understandably. — And then what? You'll kill me? — Rather, when the occasion presents itself, I shall return you — to your people. But until then — I would find it interesting to know you better. — Arda blinked. The word used by the Mon'keigh had a double meaning: to know better — as a friend. Or — to study as an object. The defining article was terribly lacking. — Why would you study an Eldar? Does the Imperium have a shortage of the dead bodies of my countrymen? — I am not your enemy, young woman. Not a friend, but not an enemy either. However, I shall not for now traumatise you with excessive communication with a species alien to you. For the time being, this cabin will be your home; further, if we find a common language with each other, your zone of freedom will significantly expand. Clothing fitted to your size has been found — it is in the drawer above your bed. Simply press on the wall panel. Falling water — is behind the wall panel on the opposite side. If you require food or water — pull this cord. With this, I shall, perhaps, leave you.
