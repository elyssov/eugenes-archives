Chapter 4

...Alexander had long since wiped and returned his sword to its scabbard. By and large, today he had not had much fighting to do. In fact, he had led the IG fighters forward, cut down two and shot one Guardian. The rest the guardsmen, inspired by his example, finished. Having shed his cloak and presented himself in all the glory of his master-craft power armour, he had, perhaps, produced on the soldiers an effect comparable to the manifestation of one of the Primarchs. The Inquisitor inwardly smiled at the "heretical" nature of his own thoughts. He was, of course, no radical, but he had grown accustomed to looking at many things soberly and from the height of his knowledge as an Inquisitor. Anyway, though he did not consider the Primarchs gods, before the majesty of these true sons of the God-Emperor he bowed, particularly singling out Guilliman. A light tingling let him know of Rosa's approach. As always after a battle and his praise, expressed even in a kiss, Rosa was radiant with a sincere smile (and the desire to finish off another two or three enemies of the Emperor). According to Isaac's calculations, no more than an hour and a half remained to wait for the relic's full synchronisation, after which they would have about an hour to extract it. Vyshinsky clicked a flat flask of excellent amasec out of one of the hidden compartments of his armour. He glanced sidelong at Rosa and extended the alcohol to her. The girl, with visible pleasure, took a good swallow and returned the flask. The Inquisitor tried to follow her example... But he was interrupted by a strange cry. The cry was female, resembling a cry for help... But, at the same time, full of some strange, unnatural passion. The thought of the manifestation of Slaaneshi daemonettes was, perhaps, the first to enter the head of the servant of the Ordo Malleus... ...It took Alexander about ten minutes to reach the source of the cry — first, he gathered his whole detachment before venturing into a place where the Ruinous Powers might have broken through; second, the foul mud underfoot had its effect. To Vyshinsky's surprise, the other Savlarites had not particularly reacted to the cry (which, incidentally, did not repeat itself). On the spot, the Inquisitor discovered a picture that frankly threw him into a brief stupor for a couple of seconds:

Behind the last "Chimera" in the row, a group of four scum had "laid out" the captured Eldar (his augmented memory helpfully supplied the picture of the madwoman who had led the rest of the Guardians in a suicidal attack). Judging by the fact that two of the four had a very satisfied look and were pulling on lho-sticks — they had already had their turn. Another was vigorously moving his pelvis on the xenos girl held by the last by her arms. The girl's mouth was stopped with a gag of rags of some fabric, judging by her limply lolling head, the xenos was unconscious... However, what surprised Vyshinsky more than the events themselves (already extraordinary) was that even on the unconscious Eldar, her face was frozen in a grimace of pleasure... — What the... WHAT'S GOING ON HERE!?! — A second's pause let Alexander recover and begin to act. The smokers fell out of their trance (it seemed the lho was of unsanctioned composition) and gawped, dumbfounded, at the two-metre man in golden power armour. Then one of them sank to his knees, clasped his hands on his chest in the sign of the aquila, and began muttering prayers. The second, evidently thinking faster, but somewhere off the wrong track, decided to lunge towards the lasgun thrown some distance away. His fate was cut short by Rosa's shot. The fighter absorbed in his occupation froze only after the shot. Then he slowly turned to the sound. His comrade had long since (relative to the start of events) let go of the alien's arms and was now trying to flatten himself against the Chimera's armour. — I always knew Savlarites were scum. But today you have managed to surpass even my notions of your baseness — Vyshinsky did not so much pronounce the words as spit them out. The Inquisitor's eyes blazed with righteous fire. — You have undertaken to copulate with a captured woman. A xenos. I'm not even certain which is worse: that you are rapists, or that you took it upon yourselves to do this with an ALIEN! — I think, my lord, worst of all is that with an alien. For it is said — "kill the xenos," not "love thy" — observed Isaac, raising to the sky his trembling old man's hand with admonishing finger. — In any case, the verdict here can only be one. In the name of the God-Emperor, for crimes committed before His countenance and Humanity's — I sentence you... to death. The condemned did not have to wait long for the execution — neither Bromstein nor Strauss nor Rosa had ever had the habit of dragging out the implementation of orders. As for the targets, they had silently divided them up through their auspexes during the indictment speech — the verdict had been obvious one way or another. — My lord, what shall we do with the xenos? — Strauss raised his bolt-pistol and aimed it at the head of the still unconscious girl. Alexander thought. By rights, eliminating the xenos would be the correct decision... However, her face... Something in what was happening was not right, and the Inquisitor's inquisitive and observant mind demanded he sort it out. — To my ship with her. Arrange her delivery to the shuttle and her placement in a stasis pod. And — I do not trust this to Savlarites, as you understand. Bromstein, see to it. The rest — follow me, the relic is about to appear... — Oooh... — it seemed consciousness was returning to the girl. Evidently due to the servomotors that allowed him to walk in "seven-league strides," Vyshinsky was the first beside her. Lifting the weightless body of the girl, even without armour, out of the mud (and being astonished once again at the Savlarites' uncleanliness), he tilted his head to hear what the xenos was trying to say. Some basics of the Eldar language he had at one point picked up for one of his missions. Now this might prove useful. The girl raised her head and focused her still-clouded gaze on the Inquisitor's face. For a fraction of a second her gaze cleared; she even leaned forward, extending her hand to Vyshinsky's cheek... And went limp like a broken doll. From the characteristic sensation, Alexander understood that Rosa had approached them at close range, finishing off, evidently with her antiaura, the poor alien. The Inquisitor handed the barely living body to Bromstein and, with a wave of his hand, led his squad on toward the relic...
