Chapter 2

...Arduriel! You — again?????!!! — her mother's voice was full of righteous wrath. The girl clamped her hand over her mouth, trying not to give her presence away by giggling. After all, dear brother, festooned with wet, dirty laundry tipped over him by the trap that had been sprung — had earned his fate himself.

— Calm down, dear. I think we ought less to scold Arduriel than to ask Eltan — how he intends to be a hunter, if he can't notice even the simplest snare? — Father appeared on the scene, as usual, soundlessly, unnoticed, and, somehow, directly behind the hiding troublemaker.

— But... Faaaather! I'm at home! I'm not in the forest! — protested the soaked brother.

— And is that a reason to relax? Do you think few dangers await you in the village? Or do you suppose that the status of the elder's son can shield you from all troubles? Eltan, grow up at last!

— Zauris, you spoil Arda! You indulge her insufferable behaviour! — mother, as always, was obsessed with "propriety." — Do you know what the neighbours say about her? — My dear, I'd guess about the same as they said about me, in my childhood... In this world, one cannot be too fanatical. One cannot follow rules unconditionally and be rigid... Only those who are capable of thinking beyond common bounds — may, one day, become chieftain!...

A jolt awoke her. The shuttle, which had seemed to land perfectly level, suddenly tilted, sinking its support strut into the mud. Arduriel sighed heavily and began to rub her eyes. Completely childishly, with her little fists. The young woman had only recently joined the Guardians, and had not yet learned, like her warlike brothers and sisters, to come to herself instantly. Among her people, this would, of course, have been grounds for endless ribbing; however, among the cold and reserved inhabitants of the Craftworlds, to notice such a small thing was, evidently, considered beneath one's dignity. Arda herself perfectly understood that one day this slowness might become a problem both for her and for the whole unit; nevertheless, for now, she could do nothing about it. There was no need to hurry, according to the plan of the Farseer who had sent them here. By and large, their mission was of a backup nature. The main engagement was supposed to take place in space — where a small Mon'keigh frigate, lurking in elegantly woven holofields, was waiting for the elegant but deadly Eldar cruiser. Arduriel had never yet seen these Mon'keigh, but she felt sorry for them in advance. The artefact they had been ordered to defend — went back to the time of her race's absolute might. To those ancient ages when the Eldar were still one people. Wise, strong, omniscient... Who had buried the Sphere of Causality on the most distant planet of their empire. They had buried it not without reason, evidently. Now, as the Harlequins had managed to discover, the Mon'keigh had resolved to lay their paws on this sacred and lethally dangerous relic. The girl tightened her grip on the forestock of her shuriken catapult. Even if these savages broke through to this place — she would show them the true steadfastness of her race! ...— So, an Eldar "pirate"... Well then, it seems I shall have to conduct a serious investigation upon return. Our mission was of strictly secret character, and very few people had information about it, brother-captain. I hope you taught the xenos a worthy lesson? — Have no doubt, my lord. They did not expect the appearance of our cruiser and were too taken with pursuing your frigate. They seem to have missed the fact that you had landed shuttles from the maximum distance. — I believe I know whom I ought to thank for this... Besides the God-Emperor, of course — the Inquisitor glanced sidelong at his "untouchable" companion and felt a wave of tenderness. Once again her cursed "talent" had saved the entire mission. — I believe you are correct in your assumptions, Inquisitor. The xenos were not ready for our appearance, so we were able to drop a landing party of our brother-Terminators aboard, who decided the outcome of the engagement. — Your service to Him is, as always, immaculate. I trust losses were minimal? — Two of our warriors shall forever take their places in the Halls of Heroes. Another, I think, will soon join us as a Venerable Dreadnought. Nothing more serious. But I would warn you, my lord. I think there may be an ambush on the planet. — I would be surprised if there weren't. — We can drop a landing party within a couple of hours. — I don't think there's any need for that, brother-captain. You yourself say it was a light cruiser. I don't think they could have had a large landing party on board. And as for the rest — the guardsmen assigned to me will handle it. At the same time, I'll see what the Savlarites are capable of. — The God-Emperor preserve you! — The Emperor protects us! End of transmission. Alexander raised his clenched fist. A couple of seconds later, the vehicles halted, and another vox call appeared before the Inquisitor's eyes. — My lord? Has something happened? — the commissar's voice was alarmed, while Colonel Burns remained silent in the conference for now. The Inquisitor noted to himself who was actually in charge. — My sources have reported that there is an ambush of xenos on this planet. Eldar. Our frigate was attacked, but the Eldar ship was destroyed. — Destroyed... by the frigate? — and there was the colonel chiming in, Vyshinsky smiled to himself. — Just destroyed. That's all you need to know. — I understand — the commissar Schwartz answered softly. — Warn the soldiers and prepare to encounter the ambush. They will most likely be guarding the artefact. And — vacate your "Chimera" of your protégés. I confess, I'm not thrilled at the proximity to them, but in conditions of possible ambush I consider going on foot unwise; so we'll have ourselves a command vehicle. — We'll pick you up presently. If I may be permitted to say, you could have asked for this earlier — grumbled the colonel. — I don't like inconveniencing people for nothing. Besides, I am in power armour, and your soldiers are not.

In the next ten minutes, the formation changed completely. Notified through their sergeants, the Imperial Guard troopers in part left their "Chimeras" and stretched out in a line, while others gripped the stationary lasguns integrated into the armour, and now all the APCs jerkily moved the short barrels of their rifles, like beetles their legs. The Inquisitor took his place in the "command" Chimera, where, following him, both "scientific officers" relocated. Lady Rosa, in her power armour borrowed from the Sisters of Battle, in a Celestian helm, experiencing no problems with the rain, preferred to remain outside, though she climbed onto the armour and arranged herself comfortably there, laying out a sniper bolter. Their common mentor with Vyshinsky always said that, whatever gift one had, one ought to develop at least several skills not connected with one's talents. Sniper shooting was one such skill for Rosa. Two more Chimeras of the four available — surged ahead, forming a line with fifty-metre intervals between the sides. Alexander felt the hairs on his arms stand on end — a sure sign that the manifestation of the relic was nearing... ...When they heard with their souls the cry of the fallen Farseer — they felt emptiness and despair. However, the children of Isha always discharge their duty to the end. The Mon'keigh were approaching, and the Guardians had nothing left to do but to destroy as many of them as possible. There was, on the whole, a chance — against the tanks they had three jetbike platforms with bright lances and missile launchers. As a matter of fact, three squads of Guardians — were a serious force. Arda crouched behind a rock, like a coiled spring. The beating of her own heart pounded in her temples like hammer blows: "Tuk-tuk... Tuk-tuk-tuk... Tuk-tuk-tuk-tuk-tuk!" Fear, the girl was surprised to find, she felt little of. Rather, this was some peculiar, unfamiliar excitement, coming from somewhere in the depths of her nature. This excitement was to play a cruel trick on her... The first "Chimera" exploded when literally a couple of hundred metres remained to the cliff in which, presumably, the artefact was located. Following it, a second vehicle burst into flame. Several infantrymen fell to the ground, instantly turning into bloody stumps. The rest of the infantry hit the dirt, opening disordered fire in the direction of the cliff. From the roof of the command "Chimera," loud, booming claps rang out — Rosa had opened fire from her monstrous bolter. Knowing his partner, Alexander subtracted from the total Eldar count at least four fighters...
