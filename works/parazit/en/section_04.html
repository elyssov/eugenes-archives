Chapter 4

The European part of Russia, major US cities — held. The main problem of praetorians was "operational space." In conditions of plains theater of war — they had nothing to counter heavy equipment. So a peculiar parity arose. The main task was — to not allow penetration of monster swarms into the territory of any major cities. On the basis of this paradigm the defense strategy was built:

Around cities — forests were burned for tens of kilometers. Posts of armored vehicles and heavy trucks reinforced with steel sheets were set up. Zombies appearing at outskirts were ground both by weapon fire and by tracks and wheels.

States had to unseal their "stockpiles." Rosrezerv — provided city population with rations. Yes, it was relatively scant food, but better to eat tinned meat every day than for someone to eat you.

On the structure of Z-Committee biolabs special hopes were placed. Our employees didn't sleep for days both in Russia and in US.

The discoveries made by Moscow biolab cluster — were fully confirmed. Praetorians were a chimera consisting of several species. The initial parasite itself — remained the central nervous system of the praetorian even after its complete rebirth (though, traces of host DNA could still be found in bone structures, eyes, teeth and spinal fluid of the praetorian). The body of the praetorian itself, for the most part represented a new and absolutely precisely — alien to our biology structure. At that — inside each praetorian matured eggs of a new species. Thus — the praetorians themselves reproduced through human corpses:

a human infected with initial parasite after death became a classic zombie. A zombie, given time and food — gradually mutated into a "runner." A "runner" — given the same factors, in approximately a month accumulated enough material, after which "pupated." We managed to reproduce the process in lab and studied it: As they ate — the runner "gained weight." Swelled. The accumulated material was not fat but some form of "stem cells." Or more precisely — undifferentiated biomass, resembling consequences of autolysis of larvae in pupa stage.

Actually — what followed really resembled most of all earthly insects with complete metamorphosis: Having gained the necessary mass — the "runner" sharply lost mobility. Its body was covered with hard solid armor of chitin. Inside began the process of histolysis. Looks like, absolutely all organs underwent decay, except perhaps eyes and bones (and actually the parasite's brain). When the whole pupa represented some hyperegg, resembling in form a human statue, with skeleton inside — came the time of histogenesis. From the primary "broth" — new organs began forming.

Approximately then, right before the start of complete histolysis — one of the main miracles happened. The thing is, Zhanna's research — confirmed my guess: yes, in a cell at one time was only one DNA. However, other forms — were stored as mRNA chains and during metamorphosis were simply transcribed into the nucleus, depending on need. Effectively, the basic spore of the parasite — already contained everything necessary for resynthesis of all three forms of DNA we encountered. One DNA was already in the spore's nucleus. Two others — of praetorian and egg — were stored as mRNA in reserve cell organelles. As the original sample developed — the current parasite DNA aged, organelles were absorbed by the nucleus and on the basis of contained in them iRNA threads occurred synthesis of new DNA spiral. Already of next generation of organisms.

Another purpose of Hippocratic glands was revealed: besides regenerative function aimed at preventing death of host-organism, occurred also slow saturation of tissues with pseudo-viral structures. Contagiousness of this virus was nearly zero, but it effectively introduced new iRNA into host cells, managing to complete replacement just at start of metamorphosis histolysis.

Further — everything happened by scenario well-known to biologists: somewhere within three to five days, depending on external factors, the zombie internally was reborn. Under thin chitinous film — matured a completely new organism, taxonomically relating already not to humans, but, rather, to insect kingdom. Though — from humans it inherited warm-bloodedness and absence of rigid dependence on ambient temperature. Though — praetorians wore no clothes, lit no fires, so remaining alive humanity placed great hopes specifically on General Frost.

Be that as it may — the undead army had no military equipment and methods of opposing it. More precisely — they were learning, their wit developed very quickly. By some estimates — their intellect already within a month after Mumbai's fall — was at the level of well-developed chimpanzee. Though — life showed we strongly underestimated them. More precisely — underestimated that during all metamorphoses, the parasite's brain — didn't change. Well — this happened much later. For now — we needed to understand — what was in those eggs.

Unfortunately, the samples we got didn't contain larvae inside. In this sense they were closer to warm-blooded animal eggs — whatever was there, it was to develop outside maternal individual. By the way — maternal individuals were all praetorians without exception. Looks like, eggs arose outside sexual development and were fertilized from moment of metamorphosis completion. This caused special wariness. Especially since by this moment we'd already taken Hinstein's theory as axiom.

We — were dealing with some form of alien invasion. Looks like, this whole story with parasite — was a variety of biological weapon. With such a layout arose the thought: if praetorian is already an ideal soldier-infantryman (fast, fearless, strong, tenacious, able to fly) then what was supposed to come from eggs? Development graph frankly frightened with prospects.

In general it was decided, despite fierce battles — to send an expedition to India. Headed by McManus. Along military line — I insisted on sending Captain Vasiliev with his people to combat. This guy had once shown his abilities... Moreover he had cut my phone all up when he learned of our plans.

The expedition was to be international. To the military airfield near Kiev, from where the start was planned — flew boards from around the world. The Yanks specifically for this brought a detachment of fighters armed with combat-testing-stage Raytheon armored exoskeletons. China couldn't allocate fighters — they had a hardest situation in the south anyway (in the mountains they broke it through massive nuclear bombardments making Tibet's passes simply impassable even for praetorians, whose locust wings, in conditions of rarefied mountain air, couldn't pull heavy carcasses). But — sent a whole brigade of scientists ready if needed to enter combat with weapons in hands. In general the degree of Chinese enthusiasm and inspiration never ceased to amaze me. Japan, which closed itself completely from first days of crisis — wrote that gaijin problems didn't concern her. Honestly — there was no time for them. Though — one of my good acquaintances from intelligence whispered, that Japanese were studying their zombies for killswitch and at same time for using them as own bioweapon.

Europeans had no time for expeditions. Suffice to say that there now fought, besides their own forces — additionally sent by Committee troops from US and Russia. Hardest had French. Germans held better, but joy was little too. South — was already eaten by crowds of monsters matured in Balkans.

By plan — the expedition was to proceed as follows: on three dozen An-124 we planned to drop an army research group in savanna of southeast Africa in Tanzania. The group landed on two dozen BMP-3, the rest of the vehicles — were dropped directly on Julius Nyerere international airport, on outskirts of Dar es Salaam and established perimeter. Then — already by landing method, two reinforced companies of paratroopers were unloaded along with armored vehicles. Effectively — we created a bridgehead.

The earlier-landed group — was to within two weeks find egg clutches or already-hatched creatures, gather samples and reach the airport. If anything — backup could be delivered at any moment, since the landing zone was within an hour's flight of the bridgehead.

The world — froze in anticipation. We were sure they'd find something. But we didn't expect what was actually discovered...
