Chapter 5

If you look into the abyss long enough — the abyss may look into you. Looks like, they were universal — and this explained the rather eclectic look of the creature. I looked at it, and it at me — with its huge saucer-eyes. Periodically it (or it?) blinked them in turn. Its head, resembling distantly a horse's head (such, a little cartoonish — what came to mind right away were "ponies" from the well-known series.... Or a Chinese crested dog...) like a dog's was turned now to one side then the other.

Between us there was no defense barrier. It simply wasn't needed.

The rounded body of the creature — was covered with thick, silky curly fur of cheerful coloring. On the head and ears — it turned into guard hair forming a mane resembling overgrown horse mane.

Two huge eyes evoked frankly anime associations.

In general, the creature could be even pretty. If not for being in its structure a sort of parody on a spider. To the main body, resembling a pebble covered with fur — was attached a slightly forward-protruding chest section. If from the first grew four sturdy spider legs ending in semblance of hooves with retractable claws, then from the upper section — grew four thin, mobile but strong "hands" distantly resembling limbs of a spider crab. Unlike the latter, true, there were more degrees of freedom: as many as four joints. And they ended not in claws but in almost full hands, true with four not five fingers. The "little finger" was absent.

The creature seemed completely pacified and calm. The ears gave it away. Huge, resembling cat's, borrowed from Cornish Rexes (the fur, by the way, also resembled Cornish) — twitched nervously, awaiting something. Periodically the creature melodiously called to me. Apparently requesting instructions.

In general — the expedition succeeded. Effectively, Africa was empty — all praetorians with very rare exception had "gone to the front." McManus's army dashingly raced through savanna, quickly reached Serengeti — where it discovered clutches. Hatched clutches.

And crowds of new creatures.

The first reaction was shooting. They put down several dozen before realizing the creatures didn't respond to fire, only gathered in flocks and plaintively sang something.

McManus decided to risk it: got out and went to them. Those cautiously let him approach right up, after which began to fawn like real cats. To say folks were dumbfounded — wouldn't be saying anything.

Imagine a spider on four legs, with body having tier structure and head from a cartoonish pony? Now add it fur, saucer-eyes and huge cat ears? And habits of a good house animal. Outfit the resulting nightmare — with the voice of a girl-singer about 12 and long pole-arms, with child hands on the ends? More precisely — one pair ended in hands, and the other thicker and stronger — with powerful "grabbers" of three "fingers."

The expedition gathered several dozen specimens and was already preparing to push off when "nannies" appeared. These turned out to be the same praetorians, only in their paws they held... WEAPONS.

There weren't many praetors, the guys had already managed to hide under armor of BMP-3, so on the whole it went off without particular problems. More precisely — a thermal cannon destroyed one BMP and one of the fighters got wounded by spike-worm. After which before comrades' eyes very quickly became a zombie. For the rest — the battle went smoothly, all praetorians were destroyed. Weapons — were gathered.

And all this good was delivered to us, in Moscow.

...Actually — at first we were sure these were the masters of the praetorians. The race for whose sake the planet capture was going. The creatures we caught, despite their passivity and absence of aggression — seemed quite reasonable: communicated with each other, interacted. At that — constantly something tried to craft from everything that came to hand.

Though, further observations and studies — showed they were too passive for masters.

The situation was rather paradoxical: our drones showed that in Africa, by thousands of creatures were being erected some resembling termite mounds buildings of organic polymer produced by their own organism. Inside these buildings something was clearly happening. At any rate there was constantly some quantity of creatures there crafting something. We knocked down several "termite mounds" with nuclear strikes. The creatures drew conclusions and moved production into deep jungle. And our UAVs began to be shot down by praetorians. Yes, as observation of a control, specifically left for this, "termite mound" showed — they were making weapons there.

It's hard to say what processes happened inside, but at the output came one of four possible weapon types. We managed to study all, although this took time and one more sortie.

The first weapon was the "poisonspitter." Presumably, the tube was a chimeric construction from praetorian parts and biopolymer produced by glands of the "engineer" as we called the new species.

From biopolymer was formed a tube with cellular, honeycomb-structured outer wall. From the predator — poison glands, lung sac, heart and some incomprehensible organ not encountered by us before.

The essence was simple "as three kopecks" — lungs took lots of air, glands injected poison into a shell created by that same new organ, after which with mighty exhale the spit was sent to distance of up to 100 meters. Not many in general, but the accuracy of the roaches was beyond, plus reflexes and speed they surpassed us by a head. The presence of such weapons — sharply nullified our fire superiority. After all, an untrained volunteer from a Kalashnikov couldn't hit a target even from a hundred meters.

The second weapon — was made on some other basis. Where they got organs for it — was a riddle. However it shot with a chitin capsule containing two compartments. In each was a special viscous liquid substance. After hitting the target the capsule burst, the substances mixed. Ignition occurred.

Flame temperature reached 1500-2000 degrees. Everything burned. It was exactly such a shell that destroyed the BMP.

The third was the wormcaster. The nastiest gun. The charge was a small, pinky-finger-sized worm, on whose head was a durable calcareous shell. It was launched the same way, pneumatically, true the organ there again was unknown. Where they got it — a riddle.

By itself the worm couldn't inflict any serious damage (though, hitting a body it began to gnaw into it, causing strongest pain). But the worm's task was not to cause damage, but to inject into victim's blood a cocktail of enzymes and hormones sharply suppressing immunity and activating the parasite sitting inside each human. The one attacked by worm — fell, lost consciousness and in ten-fifteen minutes woke up already as a primary zombie. But not slow and sad, but fast and brisk, practically like "runner." Of course! A living organism was reborn, not a dead one! Another nasty feature of the wormcaster was its self-homing. The worm went on the source of human pheromones. Yes, course it could correct only in small limits. But within these limits it didn't miss. And besides — desperately waving its tail like a rudder, it almost always managed to home in on unarmored part of body.

The last gun was anti-equipment. I'd have said anti-tank, but they shot down planes and UAVs with this thing too. Effectively, by structure it was the same wormcaster, only significantly larger in dimensions. And the worm was different.

First it had a very developed and flat tail. Second — it homed on thermal radiation, and somehow well cut off thermal traps.

And third — behind the armor-piercing head were located sacs with thermal mixture. Having broken armor or at least fixed on it — it exploded, igniting and melting through everything around. Range of this wonder-weapon was about 500 meters.

And praetorians, as turned out, knew perfectly how to use all this stuff.

Realizing the nightmare of what was happening — we began worldwide hunt for "engineers" and their hives. Alas — effect was minimal. After demolishing two-three termite mounds in a region, they disappeared. Weapons however continued to come. "Spice must flow," is that it?

Establishing contact with the "engineers" themselves we so far failed. They had hearing, but to our attempts to establish contact they didn't react at all. More precisely — at sight of human they fawned on him. If human began to behave aggressively — scattered and in future tried not to approach him. Though, lasted them not long and a week later approximately — they forgave him. Though from the third time roughly — human became persona non grata finally: the creatures fled from such always, after which nastily hissed at him from dark corners. Didn't try to attack, even being alone with the "killer." Their kin — didn't defend. In general, questions were noticeably more than answers.

The appearance of weapons in praetorians — became the straw that broke the camel's back. If before this we somehow held safe zone perimeters (largely thanks to automatic defense systems: robot-machineguns and robot-grenadelaunchers greatly eased life), now praetorians acquired possibility to oppose us on equal terms in firepower. And quantitatively — they long since exceeded us anyway.

The front shuddered and crept north. In Russia containing the offensive was easier — on our side played cold. But in the US...

Washington fell in early November. Nevertheless people by this moment had been evacuated to hastily-turned-into-fortress New York.

Praetorian waves came in from western front, eating Kansas, Wyoming, but getting stuck in Idaho mountains. Though — the main mass crashed onto the main human concentration, trying not to distract on agricultural states. US together with Canada — hastily erected the Wall. This project was decided to build along bank of Susquehanna, cutting off from hordes Philadelphia, Massachusetts, Connecticut, Rhode Island, New Jersey and partially Pennsylvania. All civilians who hadn't yet entered National Guard parts — were hastily evacuated to this "corner." Second defense line was prepared at Long Island. Lined banks with anti-aircraft, rebuilt all buildings as dormitories. In general, Yanks showed incredible level of solidarity — New Yorkers with incredible enthusiasm slogged at these free constructions, preparing to receive refugees. Tens of thousands of ordinary families — signed up at municipality in lists of "foster families," ready to shelter refugees at their homes.

Army men, seeing this — dug deeper into ground, slogging at fortification regions until they fell from feet.

China — crumbled like card house. Collapsed the bonds holding tens of nations together and the colossus fell. Now, each province saved itself however it could. To put simply — we expected another wave of about a billion praetorians from east.

In Russia there was no American hospitality. There were no refugees in their understanding of this word. Russians simply took weapons in hands.

All.

From child about ten and to old woman. Everyone who could hold weapon.

Refugees — there were none. There was militia. Nobody offered to anyone to settle in own apartments — in apartments no owners remained either. Everyone who could fight — prepared to fight.

Children (both orphans and those having living parents) — were gathered in specially organized children's homes. They were our future, so these homes were guarded worse than Fort Knox. Suffice to say that exactly on guard of children's homes were placed the first "zombie-tanks" — the latest research by Professor Li showed that praetorians very disliked hard neutron radiation, so were hastily created broadband self-propelled neutron emitters. Weapon of last chance, in general.

In January — the Wall fell. The wave of zombies in Russia reached Kuban and stopped for winter. We struck several nuclear blows on our own territory trying to reduce numbers of advancers, but effect turned out noticeably weaker than expected, so we didn't repeat this, counting on saving last means for defense of major cities.

In March in US came turning point. Praetorians were still stopped at New York border. By this moment — all surroundings of city represented radioactive desert created by strikes of our missiles — Yanks asked for help in critical minute, so we dropped on American continent all might we once prepared. Irony of fate — we thought we'd have to kill Americans with this weapon. Now — we saved them with these strikes.

In these conditions practically all research stopped — there was no time. And only at our lab — it continued.

April 10 — Zhanna died. She developed a method of radiation surgery destroying the parasite in brain, but slightly miscalculated. April 11 Ivan Konovalov, her deputy in department — performed radiation operation on himself, having clarified all data and sat over calculations all night. He became the first human freed from parasite and remaining alive.

By April 20 — all lab personnel got rid of parasite, and at the same time the President and government members. All necessary data was transferred to all human enclaves still remaining alive.

April 22 — praetorian army in one rush swept the outer defense line of Moscow and came out under city walls.

Judging by tactics — these were already intelligent beings. Actually, by this moment we knew this for sure: shortly before this, we conducted research on several individuals captured for us by military.

Conclusions astonished — looks like, the parasite didn't just take body. It also copied host's mind. No, traces of self-consciousness we didn't find — and praetorians didn't climb to embrace with shouts "Hi! I'm Vasya!". Nevertheless — looks like, the parasite managed to assimilate very much from host's brain and over passed time managed to digest this, making praetorians finally super-soldiers.

Now they conducted battle commandingly, using tactics and strategy. Commanders stood out, evidently from former military. Nevertheless, outer wall held. Until May 1.

And at our place at this time was already underway synthesis. We — were close to breakthrough. Only time lacked... Time...

Militia, their lives — were the price time was worth.

I couldn't not add my life to this scale. It would be wrong, and my brains at this stage, labs no longer needed. A week before — McManus went into militia.

Today — for first time I went on duty.
