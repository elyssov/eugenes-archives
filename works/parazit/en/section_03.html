Chapter 3

Studies of the captured "praetorian" (the name I borrowed from "Aliens" — caught on and became international) — continued. The new parasite species — shocked the scientific community. The Chinese, in fulfillment of committee decisions — conducted a large-scale military operation and managed to capture several hundred samples.

The first one, after completion of all studies, I personally dissected. Slovak and Pavel assisted. Making a classic incision along the white line proved difficult — I immediately remembered that Mark before the start of the procedure had dragged me lobster tongs. Nevertheless, the trephination saw managed. About ten minutes later and having splashed the suit fully — I did manage (not without help of a rib spreader) to separate the praetorian's chest plates. The first thing that caught my eye — was that the creature lying before me — wasn't human.

The pulmonary system was atrophied, turning into rather primitive but effective lung sacs. The heart, on the contrary — was duplicated and became a paired organ. The excretory system devolved into one common cloaca, the place of kidneys — was taken by complex structures, judging by everything related to the reproductive system. Judging by everything, before us was a female — at any rate at the bottom of the pelvis were discovered oviducts and an ovipositor. In the peritoneum, in some semblance of a uterus or what had been a uterus — were located several dozen not-too-large (roughly chicken-size) mature eggs with soft, leathery shells.

The color of internal tissues was mostly dark, close to burgundy and brown. The smell was hard to describe. It smelled of rotten herring, sweetish notes of an opened human brain, excrement. The creature's esophagus was full — regarding diet this became another discovery. The thing is, the captured creature — fed on the most varied food. More precisely — it was omnivorous. At that, human flesh it didn't eat under any circumstances. So: killed, but didn't eat. As a principle.

Cytological analysis revealed a strange cell structure. Actually, that the parasite was initially foreign to us — we figured out in the first days of the pandemic: the DNA spiral consisted of four threads, of complex structure. Actually, calling things by their names — this wasn't even DNA. At any rate — not the DNA we were used to. So how this crap managed to integrate into our organisms — became a very significant question. Especially since Professor Hinstein from Berkeley — put forward the assumption that we were dealing with an artificial organism.

Now — the question became even more complex. What was before us, from the energy point of view pulled at the top of evolution, at any rate as we know it. The creature's cells were sharpened for maximally rational use and obtaining of energy.

Thus, the number of mitochondria (or structures replacing them) was greater than in humans by many times. The ATP concentration in cellular cytosol — likewise differed from human by many times.

What replaced blood in the monster — apparently had not a hint of erythrocytes, but at that — transferred oxygen with its entire mass.

In general this was truly an ideal predator. And — judging by the presence of eggs — it intended to breed.

One of the eggs — was immediately sent to Dr. Smerdyakova's lab. We dug into the corpse further.

Despite the presence of chitinous carapace, inside, under muscle tissue, was discovered a developed skeleton characteristic of a higher vertebrate. Unlike human — the spine of this creature was significantly less mobile. Thus, it had not 33 vertebrae, but only 16. Vertebrae of thoracic and sacral departments — fused, forming a solid formation. Lumbar department — likewise fused pairwise. At that, the joints that preserved mobility — acquired additional degrees of freedom. So, the thoracic department relative to lumbar — I managed to turn 160 degrees, without any significant resistance from praetorian anatomy.

The legs, seriously changed and resembling taxonomically rather mantis limbs — ended in three-fingered pincers, with extended "fingers," the inner surface of which was covered with serrated horny growth. In the tips of the growth — strange shiny inclusions were seen. Slovak set about picking them out and broke the tweezers, after which managed also to cut off a piece of scalpel blade. Surgical steel — was easily torn by praetorian pincers.

One of the monster's weaknesses turned out to be eyes — they remained human, but due to anatomical changes in skull structure — protruded strongly from the cranial box, as well as — almost doubled in diameter. One eye I dissected and examined under microscope — judging by everything the monster's retina consisted predominantly of rods. Cones were encountered, but extremely rarely.

Looks like our little monster didn't distinguish colors very well, but — saw magnificently in the dark. In principle, observation of it in captivity quite confirmed this.

Legs — more resembled those of an animal. Knees bent back allowed making more powerful jumps and parrying falls from great height. Muscle mass of lower limbs was distributed in such a way as to allow the praetorian to develop huge speed and make jumps over incredible distances. A grasshopper-overgrown of sorts. I imagined with horror what will be if the next step of their evolution becomes wings.

The oral cavity was strongly changed and represented something between insect and human mouth. Teeth — there were and more resembled still human teeth. At that — there were no cheeks. Instead were mandibles opening together with the lower jaw like a "snapdragon" flower, as if turning the mouth inside out and thus biting off large pieces of food. An extremely unpleasant discovery for me became a pair of bone tubes, not yet developed but already quite clearly going along the upper palate. On closer examination — they ended in the nasopharynx with two muscle sacs of volume by eye about 100 ml each. Due to size — the nasopharynx at the place of attachment significantly expanded, forming two characteristic "goiters" on sides of the neck. With special caution I cut out these organs and dissected one of them in a sealed manipulator chamber. As a result spoiled an expensive specimen table. From the sac flowed thick-as-snot slime, with activity of nitric acid. Inside the sac, under a muscle layer — hid a tangle of glandular tissue. Looks like, Mumbai was taken by storm by underdeveloped praetorians. Well or only such fell to us. At any rate — spitting acid we, thank god, during our retreat never encountered. Otherwise, I suppose, we wouldn't be working here.

Both organs were transferred to neighboring labs. Toxicologists rushed to find out what kind of poison this was, and biologists at my request — to stimulate muscle contractions of the organ to understand at what distance the poison can be spat. We with my team continued gutting the little animal.

The brain — already nothing in any way resembled a human one. Rather it resembled a tangle of crappy Soviet macaroni, forgotten on fire by a careless hostess. Although death had occurred no less than an hour ago — the tangle still trembled like a jellyfish pulled into sunlight.

Individual "macaronis" stirred, as if trying to feel out the path to the stopped muscles. I rejoiced that for euthanasia — we prudently cut the very spinal column of the praetorian with a steel plate, leaving it in the end in the wound. in the area of the first cervical vertebra. Thank god, the head separated from the body — likewise couldn't live independently. But the brain — lived.

Though — was it the brain of this creature? Giving in to intuition I picked up the intercom and called cytology, ordering them another test.

All internal organs of the praetorian — were supplemented by small outgrowths of Hippocratic gland. Effectively, if not for the trauma we caused the monster — it, most likely, was simply immortal. If not for the plate — it would have long since fused its spinal column. At any rate the body still lived, as far as possible in semi-dismembered state. The first heart laid on scales — still beat. The second, disconnected and soaking in saline — already stopped, but, judging by everything — was ready to turn on anew.

The organ into which the liver turned (we still hadn't understood its purpose) — likewise periodically pulsed, sending an unpleasant-looking wave across black surface with bright-scarlet veins.

Effectively, if not for literally a couple of details in skeletal structure and our "pre-knowledge" about its origin — I would never have said this creature was anyhow related to human. Moreover — show me it someone ten years ago — and I'd unconditionally accept the hypothesis about its extraterrestrial origin. In current conditions — I knew this creature was once human... But god — I couldn't accept this.

Truth be told — I was already frankly nauseous from the little freak. So I handed over leadership to Slovak and went to wash up.

I confess — I've seen a lot in my life: a doctor's work doesn't imply clean hands and conditioned air. At any rate — an infection doctor. But — so disgusting I felt for the first time in my life. I wanted to wash from head to toe and not once. And the smell of herring — seemed to have eaten into my nostrils forever.

In general Lyuda Smerdyakova found me in the shower. Looks like, what knocked our best cell researcher off track was something major. Moreover — not only her. Together with her into the men's shower stormed my old flame — Zhanna Cube, our leading virologist-geneticist.

— Chief! They're different! They're different!

— Andrew! Analysis is precise! It can't be, but it is!!!

— OH!!! DRESS!!!! — (the last was performed in two voices).

About five minutes later of fierce wiping and crawling into a brand-new disposable set of lab clothes — we sat together at a table in the "wardroom." I warmed my hands on a liter cup with three "Earl Grey" bags and listened to the report:

— Andrey, I rechecked results several times. DNA sequencing went correctly all three times.

— Andrew. These are DIFFERENT DNAs. More precisely it's not DNA at all, it's some devilry. But as far as I can judge — this molecule is responsible in the parasite for hereditary information. So. This — is DNA of three absolutely different species. I'm not ready yet to tell you what the key differences are and which gene is responsible for what. But on the whole...

— Chief, Zhanna is trying to say that the parasite in the cranial box, the praetorian itself and its egg — are three different species. All three have only the non-human, four-thread DNA in common. All the rest there — is different.

— Stop-stop-stop. Don't fuss. Let me chew. The "brain," the "body" and the "fetus" — have three different DNA sets? Each individual?

— Yes. — Zhanna pulled herself together and I admired the witch. French women are still a special grade of women.

— Chimerism?

— You seem not to have understood, Andrew. There are three different DNAs. Not just different individuals. There — DNA of DIFFERENT SPECIES — the last Zhanna emphasized vocally.

— Wow. No. Wow. Wow-wow. Wow-wow-wow-wow.

— Aha! Got through! — the girls exchanged glances.

— And at that — the egg somehow manages to develop in a body foreign to it, under control of a brain foreign to all this? Something doesn't add up here... — I gathered myself a bit. The idea hovered somewhere nearby. It just needed to be caught by its squirming tail. Well — that I always was good at doing.

— Lyuda, you now take all your team and however you want find out for me what or who should be born from this egg. You can prepare an expedition to dead territory. I'll sign and request help from the Committee. Zhanna — you with your lab must give me an answer: how in a closed organism occurred the rebirth of basic, human DNA and where did three different ones come from. Request help from the St. Louis Washington Institute. They have great experience. Within a week — I want to know about this beast's genetics everything possible and a bit more on top. I — will try to push through with the Committee a green light for all this — after all our group seems to have stumbled upon vital information.

Hot tea with bergamot rolled like fiery lava into the stomach. Felt good. The thought fluttered caught by my mind.

At that moment I still didn't know that on all Z-zone perimeters at this moment had begun heaviest battles. I didn't know that right now, a black wave on rustling mica wings (and I had feared this) was falling on Cairo. I didn't know that hundreds of thousands of Chinese military were dying right now in Tibet being devoured by zombies and on the approaches to Nanjing. I didn't know that the Great American Wall — had been swept by a many-thousand crowd of refugees from Mexico, "on whose back" — millions of praetorians rushed to the continent's north.

Once again some trigger fired. Zombies ended the "truce."

Now they attacked — and weren't going to stop until the bitter end.
