Chapter 7

...We waited for search team only by morning. First to hear it was Masha. Woke up, slipped out from under my arm, looked out window and shook me. To attract attention of APC driver had to be by shooting into air. In stilled, peaceful air — shots became good landmark....

...I managed to lash burst at praetorians falling on me. Girl — also put down two or three, but for rest of them us should have been with abundance. The more surprising was that already almost reaching us with claws, praetors fell like broken dolls. Moreover — emitting strange, guttural sound resembling whining, praetors before eyes began to melt, turning into transparent slime flowing from all carapace cracks. I needed a minute to figure — my lab succeeded....

...On remnants of strength — we hobbled to door, went down. Shooting in city quieted. Peaceful evening descended. First peaceful evening of new world. Over roofs of houses — at full speed passed Mi-8. I estimated course — vehicle was going to Kozelsk. Judging by everything it carried gift to neighboring continent..

To try to find way to our own we had no strength. They sufficed only to find empty apartment with sufficiently weak door. In two-room apartment — turned out excellent double bed, on which we collapsed.... And then we both very and very sharply realized we're still alive... Sleep had to be postponed.

We didn't know each other's names, we didn't know how many years each of us was, we didn't know if each of us had someone... All this wasn't important. Important was that here and now met two people who ten minutes ago were supposed to die, but survived. And won.

And we loved each other. In this young girl — there was passion like in cat gone wild in spring. And my 45 years — as if flew off shoulders. Her hot kisses — covered my face. I caressed her body and felt as she trembled from each my touch and arched from desire overflowing her. Her small, firm chest, with provocatively sticking nipples — time and again touched my chest, her arms were in my arms... And together — we were a single whole.

Alive!

...APC delivered us to checkpoint on TTK, in Leninsky Prospect area. Further our paths diverged. I — had to urgently get to lab. At checkpoint already waited for me helicopter called by radio from APC. Masha (the only thing I knew about her was her name) — had to urgently go to hospital. We both understood that we'd never see each other again. In new world there were no cell phones, emails... More precisely — yet no. I knew we could restore everything...

...In lab reigned animation. In synthesis hall — was President, who attentively delved into technical process of producing pheromone. Yes, we'd groped killswitch. Pre-programmed by creators of this biological weapon self-destruction mechanism. Judging by everything — it was narrowly specific to praetorians. At any rate all previously infected by parasites — likewise remained infected, and our available "engineers" — were cheerful and merry even with maximum dose of pheromone.

While we were at checkpoint — shooting started again in city. Were shooting down zombies obtained from earlier killed army and militia fighters. Parasite still functioned diligently raising bodies. Though, after praetorians zombies weren't problem.

As I supposed, as soon as it became clear that rocket launched from right backyard of institute with pheromone sprayer aboard worked — we sent gift overseas. Fastest way — ballistic missile. Only thing — head part was repurposed. In principle — everything was ready long ago, waited only completion of synthesis, so didn't waste time. As here — praetors in US perished almost instantly. Pheromone caused in praetorians shutdown at once of all organs, relaxation of all muscles, after which launched process of total autolysis.

Praetorian simply dissolved, exuding extremely useful as fertilizer mineral-rich slime.

Over next month, we sprayed in planet's atmosphere several hundred tons of pheromones. Sooner or later — they had to get everywhere. Funny side effect was mass death of cockroaches. Looks like they really were relatives. Be that as it may — life began to return.

Month later was conducted population census. In Russia survived 25 million people. In US — 30 million. China — by miracle gathered 10 million — central authority's fall told.

In India — no survivors were found. More precisely — there wasn't one in any major populated points. What was happening in Indian jungles and villages — no one set out to find out.

Europe preserved about 70 million people for all. By battle under Moscow — they all sat down in Denmark, fenced off by defensive line Heide-Kiel. Most survived were Scandinavians and Germans.

Very surprised Belarus — survived 5 million people. In streets of Minsk — never stepped foot of foreigner. In general — Belarus turned out example of what means effective and extremely centralized state administration in combination with strong, motivated and heroic army. And Ukraine, unfortunately, was eaten almost entirely. Survived about seven million people, and predominantly in Donetsk and Zaporozhye. Local residents — could decently organize in conditions of state chaos. Practically didn't suffer Crimea: With start of praetorian invasion — sailors of Russian and Ukrainian Navies — stood wall on Perekop. Weapon depots of Russian Navy — were opened. Weapons — distributed to Crimean militia. By joint efforts Perekop was held. And consequently held Crimea.

Africa, like India — remained Terra Incognita. In nearest several years humanity didn't plan to go there.

On basis of Committee — was created Unified Earth Federation. In prevailing conditions there was no longer speech of borders, rivalry, wars...

Priority project became development of automated parasitoectomy system — we weren't satisfied with necessity to behead our own dead...

In general half a year after Battle under Moscow — we could restore agriculture level necessary for feeding survivors, set up production of first necessity items (luckily, on territories reclaimed from praetorians no one destroyed production capacities).

Small part of population (first of all risk groups — children, elderly, pregnant women) were freed from parasitic infection. Alas, assembly of parasito-ectomizing stands was associated with enormous difficulties, and in maintenance required neurosurgeons of highest level. Though — we with team worked on significantly simplified, effectively automatic variant.

By efforts of OSCD — managed to prevent epidemics that could arise in post-war conditions.

Already in first month — all utility services were set up. As already said — praetorians, luckily, didn't damage technical structures on occupied territories...

We — were reviving. We reclaimed our planet.

We — earned our right to life.

And then, into earth's atmosphere entered the Master.

In him was about 16 kilometers in length and three in width.

He resembled gigantic blue whale, sharpened for flights in space.

He entered atmosphere over Moscow. The place where we decided our fate.

And everything again cardinally changed....
