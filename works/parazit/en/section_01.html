Title: Parasite

Author: Eugene Lyssovsky

Chapter 1

At first we decided it was a form of spongiform encephalopathy. Unfortunately, the first outbreaks happened in Afghanistan and remote Indian provinces, so the diagnosis was made from brain preparations of victims. From the visual appearance of preparations, I mean. Yes, several samples reached labs, but — a negative result on prion infection isn't a final result. After all — it's one of the hardest pathogens to detect.

Given the contagiousness of prion infections and obvious lesions — no one bothered to dig into the first samples. Especially as WHO's attention was drawn to the epidemiological situation in Syria, so they just noted that in several Middle East villages located comparatively close together — an outbreak of prion disease characterized by quite rapid course had been recorded.

Maybe — we could have avoided this if we'd been more attentive. If we'd immediately dug into the victims' brains... Alas.

Of course, those weren't prions. They were just studying us. Preparing. Learning. The lesson was absorbed. And five years later — the lesson was taught to us.

Actually — we were ready. Our whole culture at that moment had been fanboying this phenomenon. More precisely, finishing fanboying it. Just at the moment of first manifestations — we'd begun fearing aliens from space again. Funny how one enemy managed to combine in itself features of both our two cult fears at once. Pop culture had prepared us for both, but how ironic it turned out that the closest in his foresight turned out to be the author of the film recognized as the worst of all time.

At any rate, when in our cities' streets, almost simultaneously, in dozens of places worldwide there appeared strange people, foul-smelling, decomposing and lunging at the normal — no one asked what this was. The term "zombie" was self-evident.

However — our readiness played a cruel joke on us. Actually — we missed several hours from the start of first zombies' appearance. More like a day, really. The thing is we took them for a spontaneous zombie flashmob. Letting them, thereby, gain strength. And only when calls began coming to police in all world cities about caught people being skinned for real by zombies — did at least some action begin. On the other hand — the thought of existing living dead caught on somehow quickly.

Strictly speaking, zombies acted by canonical rules: They wanted to eat.

A freshly-awakened zombie — immediately sought the nearest pack (doing so by orienting on the trail of spores that older zombies expelled through their parietal sphincter).

A zombie evolved. A young "ten-minuter" — barely dragged his feet. An hour-two later — already walked quite decently. A day later — ran like a seasoned Olympian. Further evolution slowed substantially, due to parasite characteristics.

Yes, that's right — the matter was in a parasite. Not in a virus, not in a bacterium and not in black magic. Actually, anyone with even a smattering of biology — should understand that no virus is capable of raising a body after biological death and even if it somehow managed to isolate individual organs and prolong their existence — it could never meaningfully control a body. A virus simply has no consciousness for this.

The parasite turned out to be quite a different matter. The parasite matured from a tiny, microscopic spore. Getting into the organism through the nose — it grew into the posterior wall of the nasopharynx, forming a miniature cyst masked by mucosal layers from visual examination.

Several days later — the cyst began sending "roots." Long and microscopically thin white threads, resembling nervous tissue — penetrated through the back wall of the nasopharynx further, to the spinal column... and into the brain. And there, not too far from the cerebellum, they began forming the first proper organs of the parasite. Its nerve node and Hippocratic gland. With this — the primary phase of infection ended.

The parasite was extremely cautious. The parasite was in no hurry. In the form in which it existed — it existed in practically every Earth resident at the moment of manifestation. Further — the process went only in case of substantial weakening of the organism or its death.

The whole matter was in immunity. Well, and in a biological timer.

It was precisely the parasite that was the "zombie." And it really ate brains. In the most direct sense. Eating out the host's brain and replacing it with itself. Intercepting control of all organs.

As soon as the body weakened, from the parasite's instincts' point of view, to a sufficient level — it began growing into the brain. The process was lightning fast. Not seconds, but to raise a fresh corpse the parasite needed about ten minutes.

First — the nerve node threw out axons in all directions, grabbing nerve endings of the spinal cord. Into the blood from the Hippocratic gland was injected something like biological micro-machines. Single-celled creatures ideally suited for urgent repair of vital organs. At that, everything not needed — was sent for disassembly. First of all this was skin, hair, nails. Cytolysis proceeded with formation of pus-like substance, on one hand possessing disgusting appearance and smell, and on the other — containing a huge number of crazed antibodies, not allowing thereby infection to penetrate through forming trophic ulcers into the body hijacked by the parasite.

The first several hours — zombies represented exactly classic zombies from horror films: rotting, decomposing, dumb. Obsessed exclusively with desire to eat. Considering that most zombies before reanimation were themselves eating — the spectacle was most colorful.

On the other hand, in several hours, the whole zombie's cranial chamber — was occupied by an adult parasite. Having brain volume — no less than human. Our luck was — that there was no reason as such there: bare instincts. But backed by lightning reflexes, ideal hearing, vision and smell.

Just, approximately in two hours — the parasite formed an additional, sexual organ. Parasites were hermaphrodites, of sequential type. First into the air were thrown male gametes, then, in half an hour, sex changed. The parasite drew in through the carrier's nostrils foreign male gametes, which inside the changed paired sexual organ fertilized the parasite's own female gametes. Another hour later, through a sphincter formed on the zombie's skull crown — billions upon billions of spores were thrown into the air. Inhaling even one — you ensured yourself a fun afterlife.

Actually, the five years passed from the moment of the first outbreak of that very encephalopathy — were needed by the parasite not only to master our bodies but also to saturate the planet's air with sufficient quantity of spores. Later studies showed that the first generation didn't make zombies from carriers. It formed polyps in the nasopharynx, which were hypertrophied sexual organs of the parasite. A person breathed — and spread spores. Spores settled on leaves, on other people's clothes. In roadside dust. Wind carried them in all directions. Rain knocked them down. Heat — along with steam carried them up. Spores were almost invulnerable and sooner or later, at least one in a million — settled on a carrier's mucosa and began to grow. Nevertheless, the carrier's death — didn't launch the body capture mechanism. In the spores a term was clearly programmed. When the clock ran out — all spores which could came alive. All who at that moment were near death, in coma, just dead — all became first-wave zombies. It may seem to you this isn't much. But by that moment on earth almost everyone was infected. Women, children, men...

First manifestation caught me just when I was together with Mark McManus from the CDC drinking coffee in "Shokoladnitsa" in the high-rise on Barrikadnaya. A warm June evening stood, it was end of the week — and, looks like, we were waiting for an excellent weekend. The joint project of newly-arisen Russian FSCD and American CDC — a unified operational group of quarantine infection control was acquiring ever more finished features.

The WHO representative, Jacques Lafer promised to crawl over to us a bit later, after which our whole team intended to go to the central FSCD office, where the rest of the team was already waiting and my brand-new "Chevy Van 30" loaded with marinated meat.

So, we sat, drank coffee and discussed fresh news spinning on the TV hung in the corner:

— I never understood this zombie infatuation — Mark nodded at the screen, where at that moment a crowd of dressed-up zombies was pushing on a camera operator somewhere in New York. — What's good about the living dead? The stench alone!

— Well, these guys never went on assignment to Haiti regarding cholera, for example. Or didn't sort out a died-out village in equatorial Africa. They should talk to Jacques...

— Oh yes! Jacques has seen more than both of us! — Mark shrugged his shoulders — by the way, as a child I myself dreamed of a zombie apocalypse.

— That's probably so that world justice be restored and you got the bicycle gifted to your brother for his birthday? — I sipped coffee frappé and closed my eyes.

— How did you guess?! Only I was thinking not about a bicycle but about a car, but on the whole... — Mark couldn't restrain himself and guffawed.

— What else could a well-off American teenager think about in pre-crisis times? By the way, I never asked you — did your parents push you to become a doctor — from the TV came chaotic firing. I opened my eyes and turned to the screen. On it — several police chaotically fired on a flashmob. Zombies regardless of losses — rushed at them. Filming was from a helicopter.

The chopper chaotically tried to take the most advantageous position, but due to the height of buildings this didn't work. Nevertheless — how the shooters were crushed I managed to see clearly.

— In my view, even after the second wave of crisis not everyone here has their brains back in place, Mark.

— Andrew, I don't know what's happening there, but this doesn't look like a flashmob... Maybe some cult?

"Breaking news! From all over the world come reports of strange events related to dead bodies. Hundreds of victims in major US and European cities. Moscow police confirm there are alarming signals from Moscow, St. Petersburg, Stalingrad morgues too. Now with a special address to citizens — the President of the Russian Federation:

Compatriots! Citizens of the Russian Federation!

The world stands on the brink of global catastrophe! As a result of a man-made catastrophe, worldwide living people face threat of attack from mutated creatures externally having human appearance. The epidemic spreads with unprecedented speed. Danger hangs over the world and our Motherland!

In accordance with Art. 88 of the Constitution of the Russian Federation, I have signed a Decree on introduction of state of emergency throughout the territory of the Russian Federation! The Federation Council and State Duma are notified of the situation.

The Armed Forces of the Russian Federation, internal affairs bodies, FSB and EMERCOM divisions are placed on combat readiness.

Curfew is introduced throughout Russia.

Please maintain calm and don't leave your homes without necessity. Comply with all requirements of authorized state bodies.

I have appealed to the United Nations, to the World Community with a request for help in eliminating the threat to the population of the Russian Federation.

We will do everything in our power to ensure protection of human and citizen rights and freedoms, protection of the constitutional system of the Russian Federation.

Only together can we overcome the impending threat. I count on your understanding and support, I count on your reason and citizenship. Thank you."

The end of the speech we no longer heard — both of us synchronously got mobile calls. Though normal talking didn't work either. We only managed to hear the order to urgently come to the office, when over Moscow floated dense, alarm-bell ringing of bells. Looks like the church had drawn its conclusions from what was happening and decided that the end of the world had come.

The first zombie we saw near "Chistye Prudy" metro. While we stood at the light on Bolshaya Myasnitskaya — we saw that from the overground vestibule a foam of crowd was pouring with loud shrieks. After another couple of minutes — from the doors fell a staggering and trembling old lady making inarticulate sounds. Mark and I understood each other without words, so he instantly parked the car in a freed pocket before a Big-Mac place, after which we jumped out and dashed to intercept the zombie. There was already hurrying a patrol of two cops crazed by what was happening. The younger one, fumbling with his holster was trying to extract a "Strizh." The older, at the sight of us — turned chest to us and tried to detain:

— So, citizens, no need to go here. Drive to your business. Police controls the situation. We'll sort it out. — During the lecture, the old lady slowly, on buckling legs, howling began to close distance.

— Sergeant, step out of the way and establish a perimeter. Call support. This is FSCD business — I'm senior agent Pirogov. — The sergeant stared at the ID, trying to understand who we even were. At this time, his partner tried to establish contact with the old lady. Luckily for the young policeman, McManus turned up behind his shoulder in time. The red-haired Irishman was distinguished by considerable speed and strength, so he simply yanked our cop out from under the claws of the suddenly accelerated old lady. The four of us sharply increased the distance to the zombie. The old lady, after the jerk, looks like, drained her internal energy reserves a lot, so she began to move twice as slowly.

— Th-thanks! — The policeman was shaking.

— Welcome! — Mark smiled radiantly.

— So, zombies are quite slow. And in New York — on the contrary. Fast. What aren't we accounting for?

— Well, I have a couple of hypotheses, but can I borrow your pistol first? — Mark politely inquired. The policemen exchanged glances, not understanding a word. I took out my "Strizh" and held it out to my colleague.

— Gentlemen — this is a CDC US employee. My colleague. I take the situation under my control and responsibility. Relax. By the way, sergeant, have you called for backup already?

Mark aimed for a second, after which he made two precise shots. The "Strizh" has highest accuracy as is, and considering the distance and Mark's skills... In general, with two shots he disabled the zombie's legs. Now the dead old lady aimlessly waved her arms around. Mark returned the gun to me and headed to the body for samples...

To the office we had to drive through a police cordon. Looks like everyone was called to service and now folks tried to establish checkpoints in the megacity to avoid clumping of the dead. That they were drawn to swarm — we already knew by this point. We just didn't understand why.

— Doctors? — inquired a police captain with a nervously twitching eye. Looks like what was happening had knocked him off track. Over the city, moreover, the alarm bell hum continued to float, increasing nervousness.

— Doctors. Disease control. Just about our natural disaster. Did dead appear here? — I took back my ID and badge.

— Negative. Relatively quiet. They were saying horror's happening in all city hospitals. And you don't know — isn't this contagious?

— Don't know yet. Need to catch and dissect a couple of zombies, see how dead can walk at all...

— Maybe some virus?

— No, cap. While driving — we discussed with my colleague. It can't be a virus. Viruses don't work like that. Excuse us — we have to go, you understand.

— Good luck!

First to find the parasite were the Chinese. Dr. Li Chan — paid attention to a strange tomogram of a zombie brain. Yes, you heard right. These guys figured out to shoot off all the zombie's limbs, after which to stuff it in a tomograph. Of course — a nerve node of a new type was immediately noticed. They stuffed in a few more — same effect. Plus — new nerve fibers going aside from native ones in the spine to important organs. Plus new glands (which we by that time were studying in Moscow and just had called Hippocratic). At this place we made an error deciding it was a mutation of the original brain. But Dr. Li didn't stop at this.

By her order — a death-sentenced was dragged in. Who was stuffed into the tomograph and quietly and accurately strangled. The world community first gasped at the inhumanity of Dr. Li's actions, and then — at the results of her research. Half an hour later — all research centers in the WHO system received a video recording of the transformation of a human corpse into a zombie. And at the same time confirmation that the process can start in a still-living human. If not for the foresight of Dr. Li who ordered strangling the condemned in the scanner chamber — we would never have learned this.

However, despite the scientific recognition of her results, from the dubious honor of christening the parasite "Chan's Parasite" — the doctor refused, citing her innate modesty. So the parasite — remained simply a parasite.

The European bureau — needed two days to understand how the parasite reproduces.

We with the Yanks — another hour to understand everyone was infected and a day to prove it. By this moment in Syria, in Egypt and in Iran — crowds of thousands of zombies were roaming simultaneously. War regularly supplied the parasite with new bodies. In Iran a new fatwa was issued: now, the deceased was to have head immediately separated from body. To come alive as a zombie — was haram. To let your relative come alive — also. Police in megacities — armed themselves with sabers and katanas. Losing consciousness on the street was now extremely not recommended.

And another week later — appeared the first second-generation zombies. They — evolved. And this meant the beginning of the end of that humanity we'd all gotten used to.
