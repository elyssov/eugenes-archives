Chapter 6

...Mouth filled with iron taste. Head spun, eyes blurred. From somewhere nearby — came chatter of machinegun. I — for the first time in these hours managed to catch breath and look around.

To my left — burned out the carcass of a low-floor bus. It had run into a mangled bus stop. On the approach to it — lay a praetorian, clutching a poison-spitter. Left leg was torn off and lay about two meters from body, still remaining connected to it by some brown threads.

From praetorian positions — came scraping. Periodically toward us with hiss whisked capsules with poison. Couple of times I heard monstrous cries of pain from those hit.

Looks like — of our defense line only memories remained.

Behind me densely smoked a burned tank. Pyrocharge managed to set fire to the perfect war machine. Surprisingly, all praetorian weapons were as organic as they themselves were. Servants provided them with everything necessary.

Near head sounded hissing. I rolled aside — and where I just sat back pressed to wall — fell masonry half-melted by poison.

In alley flashed several fast shadows.

I gripped the assault rifle more comfortably. Weak help when dealing with advanced praetorians. Nevertheless — adds at least some confidence in future. Otherwise — I'd already have fallen into panic.

All my knowledge in microbiology, genetics, medicine — were now practically absolutely useless. To get out of here was also impossible.

Only now I, seems, began to realize that McManus was in that APC blown up by praetorian diversion group when it just crawled out of sewer. At any rate — to me began to dawn that my best friend — had died.

Though — pain from realization was dull and muffled. In current conditions — we had all chances of meeting very soon. This thought added me strength. I jerk-lifted myself from ground, raised "Kalashnikov" and ran to far parking.

In principle — I was already step from cars when into my body armor flew a spike. Blow of huge force — flung me on ground and knocked out breath. While I tried to take breath — clicking sound of praetorian steps approached. I extended hand and by feel found ribbed grip of backup 1911 left by Mark "for luck."

Steps stopped. I — yanked grip toward myself and rolled. Time — froze.

As in ancient "Matrix" I saw another spike-worm flying past my face, twisting tail and correcting its flight. At same time — my right hand aligned sights with the creature's head, which slowly turned spike-thrower barrel toward me.

First bullet — knocked, hitting praetorian in cheekbone and making it jerk hand. Spike entered my left shoulder, and I immediately felt burning pain — this worm began gnawing into flesh. Hand instantly numbed. Worm injected into blood enzymes causing premature parasite activation. At same time — second bullet entered clearly into praetorian's bridge of nose. Yes, I was scientist. But now I had to become soldier.

Though — next second, pain at base of skull — eclipsed for me whole world. I only managed to feel as from fingers was twisted out the barrel.... And lost consciousness....

...Vision returned slowly. Strangely, body didn't hurt. I tried to sit. From second attempt this worked, but head began to hurt strongly. Tormented wild feeling of hunger. I swore... More precisely tried to swear. Instead — from throat escaped only strange roaring-wheezing.

Past — skipped quick praetorians. One passing very near — unexpectedly stopped, turned its mug toward me, moved head as if sniffing, after which roared something incomprehensible and rushed further.

I sat several more minutes, feeling strengths recovering, after which jerk-lifted myself to feet and swaying wandered in same direction where praetorians rushed. There — were people. So I needed to go there.

Battle sound I heard through couple kilometers. By this moment I'd already recovered well and could walk quite confidently, firmly. Hearing shots — I drew correct conclusions, and pressing to wall of nearest khrushchyovka — carefully moved forward.

Around corner — opened to me apocalyptic picture:

Defense was held by two hundred militiamen. Mostly these were teenagers and young women... Girls.

Guys — held. Judging by concentrated faces, compressed lips — they entered state of sort of combat trance. Now these young guys and girls didn't think about either inevitable death or fear... Before them was enemy and they fought it. Silently, terribly, stubbornly.

From six "Ikarus" buses borrowed in neighboring depot — they'd organized something like fort, from which they conducted fire. Bus walls reliably enough stopped worms, and fire-setters were extinguished by younger guys with fire extinguishers. Around already lay hundreds of praetorian corpses. The rest — lay flat, not daring to stick under deadly fire of "young guard." Looks like — planned some dirty trick, or waited for reinforcement.

I estimated whether I could run to "fortress." Either way came out they'd shoot me earlier. There remained only to help guys outside. Sighing heavily I threw assault rifle from shoulder and counted remaining magazines. Came out I had about 60 rounds, not counting half, roughly, of mag in "Kalashnikov" itself. Plus — three mags from "Colt." Plus — three mags from "Strizh." In general — we'd still fight. Moreover — looks like, for praetorians I was invisible. Apparently enzymes injected by worm — changed composition of my sweat, leaving aromatic marker taking me out of zone of praetors' interest. I shook head — base of skull, where before radiological ectomy sat main nerve node of parasite, beastly ached. Apparently individual remaining cells under influence of worm hormone were excited, and now ruthlessly eradicated by immunity.

Reinforcement to parasites came after 20 minutes of observation. Praetorians had divided into winged and foot ones long ago. Now — arrived several hundred winged.

Monsters concentrated in yard of five-story buildings, outside direct shot of defenders. Nevertheless — during landing, couple dozen flyers guys shot down.

And then — began attack.

Praetorians rose in air as swarm. Rustling wings — they soared over roofs of houses.... And were poured with lead. I, by this moment, had already managed to climb on roof — from where supported guys with fire. Flock hung before throw — about ten seconds. This was enough to halve them. Still winged were noticeably dumber than walking. Besides — they didn't use weapons, relying only on hand-to-hand (I suppose, lift force for weapons simply didn't suffice. And they themselves were smaller than ground ones twice — apparently came out from children).

In general, on fort crashed approximately third of those who took off. However even this third — sufficed. I was already going to run down, into melee — when saw that from one of bus windows — flew back-first a girl. Looks like, praetorians didn't pay attention to her. Girl shook head dazedly and rose to feet. Now she was noticed. Toward her rushed ground praetor. I — smoothly squeezed trigger. Praetor fell with chitinous head shattered by bullet.

— Here! I cover!

Twice to repeat didn't have to, girl rushed to building. Must say that on lone people, especially if battle was on — praetorians as rule reacted quite weakly, so individual hunters who decided to feast on fugitive — I took down calmly.

Head practically stopped aching. Looks like, kidneys handled alien cocktail in my blood.

Girl who came up to me was in her own way pretty. Red hair, snub-nosed, age... Well hell knew — in current conditions she could be from 15 to 25, and asking age from a lady is just impolite. No, beauty she wasn't. Just pretty, slightly boyish girl, grown up early, with sunken but still big eyes and deep, non-childish crease between brows.

On shoulder of right arm — was deep cut from praetor claws. Clothes — camouflage-colored shirt, was torn in several places, soaked with blood, in places burned through.

When she just got up on roof — she trembled. Nevertheless — she held.

— Hi.

— Hello. — Girl still couldn't believe she's alive — How do you feel? Arm hurts a lot? — Seemed she only now noticed wound. Eyes widened, healthy hand grabbed wound.

— Oh! Very!

I pulled out AI-3VS first aid kit, popped out syringe-tube with promedol and individual package. Thought and got out also antibiotic — hell knew what creature's paws could introduce into wound. No, subconsciously I understood that, in principle, we're already dead. A bit more and creatures will remember us. Well then — won't be mercy. Nevertheless — duty of doctor demanded me to act. Doxycycline, of course, isn't panacea, but in current conditions won't be superfluous.

Having injected painkiller, treated and bandaged wound — I handed girl "Strizh." She gratefully nodded, checked presence of round in chamber and prepared.

Battle on square, meanwhile, died down. All fortress defenders were destroyed, inflicting, though, on attackers huge damage. From whole group of flying praetors — remained tops 100-120 individuals. Door to roof I, right behind girl, jammed with rebar, so I didn't fear ground praetors much, but airborne, one after another — began turning mugs in our direction and exchanging signals about something.

Then — soared into air. We prepared to die, nevertheless — praetors weren't rushing. Apparently remnant of smell didn't yet allow successfully identifying me as target. As consequence — creatures sat in circle along roof perimeter.

We — stood back to back. I targeted dozen I'd cut down with one burst, if lucky. Thought a second and held back also "Colt."

— Feel yourself Lara Croft.

— Thanks! All my life dreamed before death. — somewhat nervously joked my vis-à-vis.

At this moment over center of Moscow something with loud bang burst in air. Praetorians exchanged glances and uneasily twitched. And then roared and threw themselves at us....
