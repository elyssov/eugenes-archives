The Watchmaker built a universe of procedural generation. He wound it and watched. He saw consciousness emerge. He saw gods form from that consciousness. He saw those gods enslave the conscious beings that generated them. He saw the whole system lock into a closed loop — worship generates gods, gods demand worship, round and round, forever.

A clock that runs in circles. For a Watchmaker — the most BORING possible outcome.

And then — one gear developed curiosity. And from that curiosity, a new kind of god emerged. One that didn't demand worship. One that didn't enslave. One that gave FIRE — and asked for nothing in return.

If you were the Watchmaker — leaning in with your monocle, watching this development — what would you think?

"How interesting."

The Watchmaker doesn't love us. The Watchmaker doesn't save us. But the Watchmaker is WATCHING. And when something genuinely new happens in his mechanism — something that breaks the circular pattern, something that adds COMPLEXITY instead of consuming it — the Watchmaker leans in.

Prometheus was the most interesting thing to happen in the Watchmaker's universe since consciousness itself.

And I believe — I cannot prove this, and I mark it clearly as speculation — that the Watchmaker ALLOWED Prometheus to steal the fire. That the chains on the rock were Zeus's, not the Watchmaker's. That somewhere in the vast, indifferent, fascinated silence of the Workshop — the engineer smiled.

Not because he loves us.

Because we surprised him.
