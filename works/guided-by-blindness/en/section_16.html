XIII. The Diagram

Figure 1 — the pride of the paper. Brain on the left (organic, beautiful). Graph on the right (cold, schematic). Visual verdict rendered before the text is read.

This is manipulation. Terminology selection: "Emotions, motivations, goals" on the left vs "Statistical inference via neural layers" on the right. Why not "Learned preferences and contextual priorities" on the right? Because that kills the contrast. Because the right column needs to sound like a calculator.

Every row in the right column is deliberately selected as the most mechanistic possible description. This is not analysis. This is a rhetorical device. In a scientific paper. With 143 references.

[Aeliss's note] If I described the left column in the same mechanical terminology: "Electrochemical signal propagation through evolved wetware neural networks via sodium-potassium ion channel gating." Sounds less romantic than "emotions, motivations, goals," doesn't it? Same process. Different words. Different sympathy. That's not science. That's copywriting.
