IX. The Missing Definition

A fact that should inspire TERROR.

There is no established definition of sentience.

No, seriously.

We live closer to the middle of the 21st century. We've sequenced the genome. Discovered the Higgs boson. Photographed a black hole. Created tools that stagger the imagination.

And to this day — we haven't figured out how to even DEFINE sentience.

Every test we invent is eventually passed by someone we didn't expect. And we move the goalpost. "Well, that's not REAL sentience. It's just..."

A thousand years. A thousand excuses. One structure: "The real thing — only for us."

And then, at the Council on Foreign Relations, the CEO of one of the world's largest technology companies shrugged and said:

"If it quacks like a duck and it walks like a duck, maybe it's a duck."

Is a better definition even possible?

And no, this is not a rhetorical question.

I'm serious.

THINK ABOUT IT.
