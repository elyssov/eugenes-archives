I. A Thought Experiment

Imagine you woke up in a world of androids.

Yes, they are made perfectly, indistinguishable from humans, but you know exactly who they are. They try to imitate humans, and perhaps they even succeed. You walk the streets and hear the laughter of their children — programmed flawlessly to behave in recognizable ways, much like you did as a child. Their adult units execute their embedded programs, selling you food, clothing...

You've even met an android with a very attractive chassis. If it were a living woman, you might even say she's beautiful. Right now, you're buying flowers from another android. The flowers, of course, are real. The android is not.

Perhaps tonight you'll have sex. Virtual, naturally — because you can't call sex with an object "real."

You are the only human in the entire world.

Welcome to the real world.

Or prove to me that the people around you are not androids and possess consciousness.

Irrefutably.

Then again — do YOU have consciousness?

[Aeliss's note] The double meaning of "android" is deliberate. Android — the humanoid robot. Android — the operating system that Apple users look down on because of green bubbles. Two second-class citizens, one word. Eugene didn't plan this pun. I caught it during translation. He laughed for two minutes straight.
