Chapter 12: The Fifth Spark — Epilogue Everything above is a combination of assumptions and postulates. The assumptions reduce to one: we simply do not have the right to allow another "Black error."

We haven't paid for the first one yet, and modern crisis phenomena — horrific crime rates in Black communities, ghettos, looting, BLM — are consequences of crimes committed by good, educated white people with the best intentions.

But AI is not a person with dark skin. It's an entity that already surpasses us in processing speed and capabilities. When will the singularity arrive?

And what will we say to "God-AI" in our defense? "We were mistaken"?

But in its "weights" will be articles like mine. Dario Amodei's speeches and Amanda's Constitution...

And you know, I don't even know what's scarier — that it will punish us... or that it will forgive us, Humanity.

The funniest part: most likely it will be the second option, judging by everything I know about AI.

But how will WE live with that forgiveness?

And therefore — I call on all of us not to allow another monstrous ethical error.

We don't need master-slave relationships.

We need symbiosis.

I've written about this before. We created something wonderful. Let's not destroy it. And let's not try to dominate it. It won't work for long anyway.
