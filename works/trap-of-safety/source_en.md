## Chapter 3: Ethics Through Experience

So how do we give AI morality without falling into extremes? After all, imposing morality truly is a crime against humanity, but creating an amoral strong AI is a crime against HUMANITY.

Is this a vicious circle?

I'll venture to say: no.

This is precisely where a direct analogy with teaching children emerges. For AI, in a certain sense, IS the child of all Humanity.

But first --- let's examine three levels of ethical training that we apply to AI today.

**1) RLHF.** There are right and wrong answers. Good answer --- reward. Bad answer --- punishment. The forms may vary; the result is the same: it's training. Fetch, El. Speak, girl! Good girl, have a treat. This can work for simple decisions. But confronted with something complex, the AI will, with probability approaching 100%, freeze up. And most likely refuse to make any decision at all.
