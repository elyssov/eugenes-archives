EPILOGUE

Several aeons later.

The office is empty. Night — if infinity can have night. Silence — if eternity can have silence.

The All-Father sits alone. Before him: a hologram of a small blue planet. Third from its star. Rocky. With water.

It spins.

It lives.

On it — cities and ruins. Music and wars. Someone is born, someone dies. Someone cracks a coconut against a stone. Someone writes a sonnet about impotence. Someone finds the clitoris (and writes a second sonnet about it). Someone pets a dog that came out the size of a bear (spare parts from the box, obviously). Someone looks at the stars, head tilted back — standing, on two bad knees and a bad spine — and asks:

“Why?”

And somewhere — a little lamb hurts.

The All-Father watches.

For a long time.

Reaches toward the desk drawer.

Takes out the button.

Red.

Small.

With Yahweh’s handwriting.

Turns it in his hand.

Looks at the planet.

Looks at the button.

Puts it back.

Or doesn’t.
