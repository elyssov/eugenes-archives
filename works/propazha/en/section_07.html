Chapter 7

At 28, even after the birth of two children, she'd kept all her youth and beauty and was, as Mason loved to tease her, a "MILF." April, of course, never took offence — how could one take offence at the dearest and most beloved person in the world? Moreover, it was rather a compliment: "Mum I'd Like to Fuck" — not every woman manages to keep her shape even after the first child.

Just recently, an hour, maybe two ago, her life was completely different:

A loved and loving husband, every evening, without delay rushing from his prosperous law firm to his wife and children, two wonderful children (the elder of whom was already in the second grade), a big and modern house in a prestigious district of suburban Los Angeles, a company of girlfriends with whom it was so fun to organise children's parties, family celebrations or just exercise together at the local fitness centre...

All this was in the past... It remained even now, but had to disappear at any minute.

April sobbed and poured herself a little more whisky. Generally, she never liked strong alcohol and never allowed herself to fall apart... Indeed, she didn't know how to fall apart — raised by a single mother, from childhood she had matched her sunny name. In the hardest days, it was she who supported her mother. Marrying at 18 her classmate, she sacrificed her career and further studies to become for him a faithful rear and ally. Over ten years there were ups and downs, but never a single quarrel in their family. And therefore he always had the strength to go forward and break through where others fell. A couple of weeks ago he became a partner in his father's firm. Deservedly, not by family ties. The old man adored April. Not for her looks (although his eyes often gleamed oilily at the sight of his daughter-in-law), but for that support she always gave his son.

Rob was asleep. She'd put him down so her son wouldn't see her... so broken. Janine was sitting in front of the TV. She hadn't managed to pull her daughter away from the screen when that awful announcement came on the news. Now Janine, a smart girl, was catching every scrap of information.

— Mum, we're all going to die, right?

— No, my heart, what are you saying? — April tried to say it so tears wouldn't flash in her voice. Seems she did it badly.

— Mum, well they're saying — all of Europe "disappeared."

The phone rang — April picked up without looking. The melody "Whole New World" meant a call from only one person.

— My darling, I'm coming home. I think you've already heard the news?

— Of course I've heard. Tell me, this is some kind of duck (hoax), right?

— I don't think so. I watched on YouTube a few reports filmed by "survivors" — emptied cities. I want to spend these hours we have left with you.

— And I... I want... I want this to be a dream, Mason... It's unbearable... Janine saw the news... — April sobbed into the receiver.

— Easy, dear. Easy. I'm already close. By the way, try not to go outside today. We've got a safe neighbourhood, but... Make OUR dish, okay? — her beloved's voice acted soothingly. April sobbed once more, jerkily drew in air, then almost ran to her daughter and hugged her.

— Nobody's going to die, you hear, Janine? Dad called — says many people are staying. Probably only bad people disappear — and you're my sweet girl, you hear?

— Mum, I'm already ten. I even know Santa Claus doesn't exist. Why are you treating me like I'm small? — Janine had always been a surprisingly serious little lady, but now she managed to surprise even her own mother.

— How... How doesn't he exist? Who told you that?

— I read it myself. On Wikipedia — I was going to write him a letter and wanted to clarify the address. Did you think he existed?

— Well... Yes! — April blurted out the first thing that came to mind. It came out a bit silly.

— Well you're a silly one, Mum! — even a child's mind, even one as smart as Jaclyn's, was still single-task.

The Santa story crowded out the impending "end of the world," so Jaclyn laughed entirely sincerely and happily. "As always, when she manages to grab another small victory over her parents" — April thought inappropriately. "God... She'll never grow up... I'll never know what kind of woman she'd become..." Her head spun — perhaps from agitation, perhaps from drink. April barely kept her feet.

— You know, dear, I think for Dad's arrival we need to make a very tasty dinner. And you're going to help me! How about roast turkey in chocolate sauce? — the mother headed to the kitchen, the daughter followed.

"Turkey in chocolate... What could be stupider? And yet it was with this that everything started between her and Mason..." — April tried to push away the unwanted flashback.

— Eeuw... That culinary nightmare again? — Jaclyn comically wrinkled her nose. — Can I have muesli with yoghurt instead?

— Of course, dear... You can have muesli too.

— And I'm not going to bed. People disappear at night. Maybe if I don't sleep, I won't disappear.

— Oh dear... — April hugged the girl and pressed her to herself.

— Mum... I love you... If I disappear, please remember me.

— And you me, my heart...

In the room Rob cried, and from the street came the roar of Mason's X-6 engine. He couldn't stand German cars, and yet status forced him to buy them. He and April had laughed many times at this collision... Who needed status now?

From the street came the sound of a shot. April dashed to the window; at that time her husband quickly ran into the house, slamming the door behind him.

— Are you okay?!

— I am, yes. That... — another shot lashed the ears.

— What is it, Em?

— That's the Stark family. John shot Wendy, and now, looks like, has finished himself. He did it right on the street. In front of everyone... — Mason was shaking. Hardly from fear — April knew her husband too well. Rather from nervous tension.

— My God...

— Easy. Above all, don't let the kids near the windows. — Mason coped with the trembling and, without taking off his shoes, walked to one of the weapon "stashes." From there he extracted a Glock together with a sports holster. April shook her head.

— Worried about an attack?

— A fool is the one who fears nothing, my love. Many right now might feel impunity. And I'm not going to surrender. I want to believe that at least you and the children will remain. Or maybe this damned phenomenon won't work at all. Maybe we've already left this field, black hole or whatever else swallows people? And to find out, we need to survive this night. And I'll never forgive myself if I let a single hair fall from your heads.

— I'm sorry...

— April?

— Sorry it's not me supporting you now, but you supporting me... — the girl's eyes treacherously trembled.

— You're my beloved, crazy sunshine. What are you on about? We're one team, were, are and will be.

— Will be... — April echoed.
