Chapter 14

...This happened approximately two weeks after April lost everything and everyone. Perhaps two. She herself wasn't sure — time passed by her. The first week she, it seems, didn't go outside at all. Tears ended quickly. There remained infinite anguish. April lived only by hope that her beloved ones would return. That at some point she'd hear children's laughter and her husband's voice again...

From suicide she was stopped by this hope, as well as fear. Fear that if this really were the Rapture, then suicide would separate her from her family eternally.

When water disappeared from the taps, she understood that, apparently, no miracle would occur. Strangely, the realisation acted sobering. April finally got to the mirror. What she saw horrified her to the very depth of her soul. The indifferent glass reflected a young old woman, with grey strands, a sunken face, with hollowed eyes. Of the former beauty nothing remained. The thought struck like current that Mason wouldn't recognise her when he returned. For the first time in the past time, she set about putting herself in order. Water, thank God, was in cooler bottles, so she could wash and more-or-less give herself a human appearance. With the movement of the body, the liveliness of mind also returned. And along with it, the understanding that staying at home no longer made sense. One had to seek living people.

So she left the house. Carefully tidied up, turned off the gas supply (at that moment, the pressure in the main was still there), closed the shutters and door and throwing a small bag into the car, set course for Disneyland. Why? Hard to say. Perhaps she couldn't have rationally explained this choice. Nevertheless, for some reason, it seemed self-evident to her...

In the next week... A week? Perhaps... Much fit. Yes, she found people. Unfortunately, not the best ones... From these thoughts, her wrists, chafed by plastic ties, started itching again... Doesn't matter — it was all behind... Like the three corpses — her husband hadn't taught her to shoot all types of weapons available to a civilian in vain... And not in vain had he gifted her the four-barrelled derringer...

In general, all this shook her. She needed release, needed a certain crisis. A certain... test. And — even what happened during... Doesn't matter. What was important was that she finally awoke. Came alive. And understood she had to get used to life in this world. The new world. A world where the former April had no place.

And as if confirming this decision — the Heavens parted. More precisely, first they were marked by absolutely straight, diverging at different angles whimsical fiery lines... And then everything was illuminated by bright flashes. April was sufficiently far from possible targets, but even so she saw how, blossoming and swelling, fireballs bloomed — where Los Angeles once was, and where the Stockpoll air force base was. How above the mountain range in the north grew a huge, black mushroom of a nuclear explosion...

And then April laughed.... And laughed until she died.

Through the eyes of the one who once was April, the fragments of the former world were surveyed by a completely different woman... She had no name yet... And for the new world — that was good.
