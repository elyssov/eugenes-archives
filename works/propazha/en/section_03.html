Chapter 3

...The trip to Auchan (and then a walk with an axe borrowed at Auchan through the shopping centre) brought a decent haul. In the hypermarket itself, the newly-minted survivor stocked up on canned goods, bottled water, tools. In the gun shop (he had to fiddle and Auchan's angle grinder came in very handy) he borrowed a Vepr 12 gauge and a supply of cartridges for it (not that he feared a zombie invasion or considered he could repel an American invasion single-handed, but the apocalypse action template included armament. Besides, in the absence of people, one had to fear animals, and judging by the surviving Okorok, those were doing just fine). The pharmacy he emptied: Vlad was a future GP and understood the price of medicines in apocalypse conditions.

He wheeled his trophies out to the street on six trolleys, one after another. Having driven the cavalcade of trolleys out to the parking lot, he pondered. Supplies he now had. But there was a big problem: what to do with them next?

It must be said that Vlad had grasped and fully accepted the situation. He'd refused too long to allow himself to believe the obvious, so the finally triumphant belief killed hope in the most brutal way.

So, he was the last living person... within the broadest geographical bounds. At any rate, in Moscow and central Russia at least. Otherwise, on at least one forum, chat, blog platform or social network he'd have been answered (already in the shop, the lad couldn't take it and arranged from his still-working smartphone a brutal spam of all available networking points). There were a couple of dozen messages from the USA — panicked, of the "what the hell is going on with you guys, we're all going to die, save yourselves who can" variety, but even Europe didn't answer, never mind Russia or China. In general, the blogs and social pages belonging to Vlad enjoyed no particular popularity, so the sample couldn't be considered representative, nevertheless it showed the overall picture.

In thinking about further actions, an idea was born too: Vlad without hesitation went to Tema Lebedev's page, Pilyulkin's, ran through the top ten of the RuNet "Facebook" rating, leaving everywhere the same message: "I'm alive and I'm in Moscow — if anyone's there, respond!"

The lad didn't risk leaving his phone number though. You never know — they'd compute it, aim a missile right at the handset...

Returning to planning further actions, the budding survivor came to three conclusions:

1. Need to find a place where one could provide oneself with autonomous water, electricity and sewerage (the last is optional).

2. Need to find transport — to somehow drag everything looted to the "lair."

3. Need to understand what the hell was going on at all. The last was also a more-or-less optional task.

Answers to the first and second points were found quickly. As always, in a critical situation, the lad's pot boiled well. In particular, he safely remembered the existence at the upstairs neighbour's of a huge Toyota Tundra pickup with a fashionable body kit. Moreover, memory helpfully suggested that this pickup, when Vlad was leaving for the Auchan raid, had been parked by the building (obstructing the passage and walkway of everything that could drive and walk — buying such auto-monsters in the city, in Vlad's view, was definitely connected with something Freudian).

The second point flowed from the first: the thought of pickups being inapplicable in cities automatically reminded him of the existence of suburban cottage settlements. And what is a modern cottage but a small, autonomous nest? Well, provided the owner had bothered with a well and an autonomous septic? A generator could be obtained at the nearest construction market...

Said and done. Twenty minutes later, the panting Vlad (in the prevailing conditions, he didn't risk using the lift, and to his neighbour on the 10th floor he climbed on foot) was already studying the lock of the neighbour's door. It didn't, it must be said, look monumental like the safe doors of the nineties. Without thinking long, recalling the experience of American action films, Vladislav loaded the gun with a slug, took aim and...

Actually, that his story might have ended somewhere here, Vlad realised, tracing the distinct ricochet marks of the lump of soft lead that the slug had turned into. Luckily for the student, he'd chosen the firing angle well, so the bullet bounced first into the tiled floor, then into the ceiling, and at the end of its flight went into the wooden cladding of the door of the neighbour opposite. Passing about a centimetre from the ill-fated "Rambo's" ear.

The neighbour's door (Vlad with difficulty recalled he was called Viktor Sergeevich) the shot effectively didn't damage. But the student, who'd jerked aside from fright, butt-first threw open the unlocked door of the neighbouring flat to Viktor's.

Through the characteristic gunpowder smell came the smell of a typical old person's dwelling. It took Vlad ten seconds to figure out the balconies were connected. With his legs still trembling from the miraculous miss of involuntary suicide, the unfortunate marksman entered the open flat...
