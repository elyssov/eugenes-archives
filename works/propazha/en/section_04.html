Chapter 4

...The flat met Vlad with dim light through cobweb-covered tulle on the windows. The unpleasant old-person smell intensified, with notes of unwashed body and dust added.

In a one-room flat the balcony was in the living room, so the "survivor" headed there, bypassing the kitchen... and froze in the doorway.

Someone was lying in the bed.

From surprise, Vlad jerked back, painfully struck his shoulder against the doorframe, stumbled, nearly fell, dropped the rifle with a clatter...

The person in the bed didn't even move.

The lad cautiously rose to his feet, set the Vepr aside, just in case, and approached the bed closer, flicking the light switch along the way.

The dim light of the old chandelier illuminated yellowed skin, a sharpened nose and the swollen, wax-like cheeks of the corpse of an old woman. On her head a worn, washed-out kerchief was tied under the chin. Perhaps that's why her mouth, in death, hadn't fully gaped but only half-opened, marking her teeth with dry lips. One eye was half-closed, the other open — both stared at the ceiling. One hand, lying on the blanket, was slightly bent, with twisted, contorted fingers like a bird's claw, taking on some crumpled appearance. The second was hidden under the blanket. The legs were slightly bent. The medical student estimated the signs of rigor mortis and decided the mistress of the flat had died at least 12 hours ago.

Despite all the morgue practice, touching the corpse was unpleasant. Nevertheless, the lad made an effort of will and forced himself to close the granny's eyes and pull the blanket over the face. The hand, jostled by this movement, straightened and unpleasantly caught on his jeans leg.

The situation with the flat's owner somewhat delayed Vlad. At any rate, he had to spend a couple of minutes washing his hands well and drying them. Logic, meanwhile, was working at full power. Looks like the granny had died before people started disappearing. Looks like only the living had disappeared. This version needed checking, and for that, one way or another, he still needed transport.

Taking a deep breath, the lad headed for the balcony...

...It took Vlad about ten minutes to climb over to the neighbour's flat. First he honestly tried to do it in the best traditions of cinema. Nearly fell. Then he got tired of it, grabbed the Vepr and with a few shots of buckshot simply demolished the drywall partition and plastic seal between balconies, after which he just "walked" through the formed hole.

As he'd supposed, the neighbour lived richly. Actually, the huge fuck-bed in the centre of the large room and opposite the huge, wall-sized television was clearly in use. On the bed itself lay a condom, three glasses of champagne, and, for some reason, exactly one pair of women's panties. Another pair lay in random spots around the room. The men's lay quite neatly on the armchair. There were also the jeans, in whose pocket the student discovered a fob with car keys. The room was "decorated" with tasteless gilded stucco, paintings of pastoral shepherdesses and other "status," in the owner's view, rubbish.

Trying not to touch anything, Vlad quickly left the premises — luckily the door turned out to be locked from inside not with a lock but with a bolt...
