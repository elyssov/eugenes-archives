Chapter 6

...From a practically shock state, Vika ("Little One," as Vlad christened her, his dreams of a "princess" replaced with care for, in a way, a "little sister") was brought out by Okorok. The cat was released from the carrier and instantly cuddled to bits. Luckily, feline nobility didn't allow the furry fussbudget to put the impudent young lady on the menu. On the contrary, the cat climbed onto her chest, hugged her neck with his paws, buried his forehead in her chin and loudly rumbled.

On the way to Krylatskoye, Vika briefly told her story: she woke up in the morning, none of the adults were home. What's interesting — Verny had also disappeared — her father's pet, a three-year-old German shepherd. At first the little one didn't attach significance to what had happened and tried to call her "folks." But when both phones turned up in the parental bedroom, and under the blanket she found mum's nightgown and dad's underwear — separately from their owners — all sorts of bad thoughts crept into her head. In general, Vika was a "geek" girl: a lover of sci-fi and fantasy, an inveterate gamer (the family, as Vlad understood, wasn't the richest, but the girl had managed to wrangle a couple of consoles), and overall a somewhat life-detached but well-read and very smart being.

Vika chattered without stopping the whole way, evidently releasing stress. Surprisingly, this didn't irritate Vlad. On one hand, after a whole day of solid silence, the chatter was a joy. On the other, the little one had a surprisingly pleasant voice. Had she been older, Vlad would perhaps have used the adjective "sex-appealing" in his head, but the redhead's age made its corrections: to perceive as a sexual object a completely flat, tiny, red-haired girl — even Humbert couldn't have managed it.

On site, all the student's suspicions were confirmed. Nobody opened the door of the flat at the ring, so he opened it with his own key (a month ago, Dasha had exchanged spare keys with him as a symbol of the transition of their relationship to "boyfriend-girlfriend" status). A weekday knickers and a lace negligee he found where he'd expected — in Dasha's room. A somewhat less expected find turned out to be men's family-style boxers lying nearby. The trailing redhead, whom he considered somewhat inhumane to leave alone in the car, muttered a meaningful "Oy-oy" and skedaddled. Vlad spent another couple of minutes dumbly looking at those very boxers and then guffawed. Strangely, he felt rather relief. It wasn't even important whether adultery had actually taken place here or he'd "got it all wrong." Simply, this still-life provoked release in him.

From the window of Dasha's flat opened a most picturesque view of a plane that had landed on the "comb" of buildings of Northern Krylatskoye. Evidently, at the moment of people's disappearance it had been approaching Vnukovo, but lost its pilots and made it as far as the west of Moscow. Three buildings of the "comb" were on fire. There was no one to put them out and no point. Such a picture of the meaninglessness of all that exists was depressing.

On the still-working internet, Vlad found the addresses of several middle-tier cottage settlements located comparatively nearby. The fancy ones were certainly supplied with water centrally, and at the small ones wells would have been insufficiently deep to deliver really clean, potable water.

As the nearest target he chose "Knyazheskiy" on Novaya Riga. It attracted both by architecture and by proximity to the megacity (supplies needed replenishing somewhere) and by location on a lake shore. The little one, on getting acquainted with the idea, expressed sluggish enthusiasm. It wasn't that she didn't like it, but the girl was being knocked out: among other things, by the hearty food — she'd emptied Dasha's fridge, lo and behold, in it was discovered a rich Ukrainian borscht cooked by Olesya — Dasha's flatmate. The stress, of course, was telling too.

In general, while the "big brother" was printing himself routes from Yandex (not hoping the internet would last long enough, he also launched offline navigator synchronisation), Vika dozed off. As a result, she descended the stairs cosily settled in Vlad's arms, snuffling with her nose buried in his neck. The lad carefully placed the girl on the back seat of his "Taiga," after which he set off toward "Kuntsevskaya" — there was located one of the Lukoil filling stations which he intended to visit: in the huge pickup bed, tied with sturdy rubber holders, a dozen tin jerry cans for petrol slightly rattled. To fill them all to the brim Vlad planned: while electricity was still in the city and the pumps were working. Then, by his calculations, their path should lead to the construction market in the Pyatnitskoye Highway area — there one should stock up on the most powerful petrol generator that the lad could in principle load into the bed. And only after that could one go searching for a new home.
