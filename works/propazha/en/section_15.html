Chapter 15

The news that the USA had been hit by a nuclear strike a couple of months ago, Vlad took surprisingly calmly. At minimum, the absence of a "counter-strike" was pleasing — in two months, if anyone could have done anything, they'd already have done it.

Vika took the news somewhat more nervously, perhaps simply feeling the pain in Vasilyev's voice. The professor, having received the data, somehow immediately became an old man. Hunched, his gaze faded...

Kuzmin, head of the Kremlin commune, heavily rose.

— Vanya, there's definitely no fault of yours here. People are pity, but you were preparing to defend all of us. And it worked — there was no war all these years, however difficult relations were, right? And that almost all the planet's inhabitants would suddenly vanish — that certainly wasn't in your plans. In general — wrap up with the blues, I ask you as a friend. Pyotr, escort Ivan Semyonovich to his flat. And, perhaps, stay with him. Have a drink there, why not...

Vlad shook his head. Perhaps he'd already accustomed himself to the thought that almost no people were left, and perceived the USA as something even more deserted than his own country, but to understand such heavy experiences of the old people about the strike that befell the depopulated States was hard for him.

— So, now you two. What, raiders, have decided to fall back to civilisation? — Alexander Kuzmin turned to the sweet pair, ironically arching a brow.

— It still has to be sorted out — who's the raiders here, and who was just looking for fireworks! — Vika didn't stick out her tongue but was clearly close to it.

— Will you state your version of events?

— We won't state! — Vlad outstripped his red wonder — if a certain someone starts laying out her vision of the situation now, we'll be spending the night here.

— Mean one! — this time, the tongue was stuck out. Apparently age and general representativeness shielded Kuzmin, but her own, kindred and beloved Vlad the hooligan didn't shy from at all.

— Guys, if anything — we've got more than enough premises for you here. Plus there's electricity, water, food. In general, move over. The threat, as I understand, is no longer any in the city, but outside the city, animals are starting little by little to settle in. Besides — together it's simply more convenient to survive. And yes, are you prepared for winter?

— Relatively. In our cottage there's a fireplace, there's a gas boiler. We've already hauled in considerable gas — in cylinders. Water — artesian.

— Not bad! Looks like the former owner didn't suffer.

— Looks like it. In general, we'll think about your offer, just for now it's more habitual there. Some, already settled place. Lived-in.

— Well, alright. As you say. If you need any help — turn to us. We'll help with the whole world. And my offer regarding your relocation is always open. Just say — we'll allocate people, help load and come... Well and of course, the most important, perhaps: guys, when there are children, bring them here: we have a couple of teachers, doctors, and in general everyone knows at least something. In general we'll teach everything we can. Since humanity has so dumped... Well, we need somehow to restore the population and civilisation.

— Children? — Vika for the first time in a long time looked frankly embarrassed. Vlad, of course, couldn't restrain himself.

— Of course, sunshine. You know that from what we do, children appear? — the "troll" whispered in her ear.

Vika blushed so that for a second she approached her own hair in face colour. Nevertheless, no return tirade followed. Apparently, the girl simply couldn't promptly find a suitable barb.

...The whole way back, Vika rode deeply lost in some thought, and already on the bedroom threshold stopped.

— You know, Vlad... And Kuzmichev, right now, is the most important person in Moscow, right?

— Well yes, and...

— Wait. I read that at sea, the captain can marry. On land, it turns out, the president or whoever's in his place, right?

Vika turned to the petrified and goggle-eyed-with-surprise lad:

— Vlad, I don't want our child, when it comes, to be born to two strangers. I want you to take me as your wife.
