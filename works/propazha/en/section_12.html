Chapter 12

...Mason looked with horror at where, a second ago, his wife had been. Beautiful, beloved April, who'd only had time to pour him a glass of water... And vanished.

A second later he screamed in horror. To the cry came Janine. The girl immediately assessed the situation. Trying not to cry, she rushed to her father, hugged him...

The morning the family met in front of the television. They needed at least some explanation for what had happened, while the television, instead of answers, threw out additional riddles.

"Connection restored with Japan and China, Moscow, Berlin, Paris, London... Put simply — the whole world is as if it hadn't gone anywhere... A traceless disappearance of a certain number of people has been recorded... In all major cities, memorials are being created. People are bringing flowers and photographs of the disappeared..."

Mason didn't go to work. About April's disappearance, he informed his father practically immediately. On the other end of the line one could hear how the old man's voice immediately drooped. The father, as if he'd suddenly felt all the weight of his years at once. Of course, of Mason's presence at the office there was no question. Mason's and April's mothers, having interrupted their joint holiday in Hawaii, were flying home on the very first flight to support the family that had suddenly lost its sun...

...Dasha, swearing, dialled Vlad's number again. The mobile gave another series of long beeps. Nobody picked up.

— Oh come on! He'll call himself. — her brother consoled her. Pasha had arrived yesterday, late in the evening, without warning, so Dasha, old-style, put him "head-to-foot" in her bed. Now he'd slept off and was bustling with vigorous activity — fucking his sister in the brain.

— Pasha! He didn't call yesterday! And usually every day! Something's happened to him! Have you heard these terrible news about disappearing people? Look, all of America disappeared!

— Dashka, units have disappeared. Your Vlad will be found. By the way, you'll introduce us at the same time. Let's go — visit your handsome.

— Aha. He lives far.

— So I'm on a motorcycle. The helmet I gave you for New Year — is it still kept?

— Kept... On a motorcycle?! Hooray! Let's go!

...A gloomy man, standing on the observation platform on the slope of Fuji, snapped shut an antique pocket watch and surveyed the space opening before him. Something had clearly gone wrong. He again surveyed the places where his comrades, vanished in the blink of an eye, had stood. Heavily sighing, he approached the Object, slightly tensed lifting the metallic body, and carefully laid it in the cumbersome protective case. Slammed the lid, with a quiet hiss the air, pumped out by internal mechanisms — harmful for the Object — escaped. Having dragged the case aside, the man took a phone from the pocket of his dark coat and called someone. After all, it wasn't in his rank to carry weights. Even very, very valuable and secret weights. On the other end of the line they listened to his short command, just as shortly answered...

The man put down the receiver, sat on a neat plastic bench and prepared to wait...
