Chapter 5

...The mobile's bleep caught Vlad on the MKAD. He'd just turned in from Besedinsky Highway. From surprise, he nearly drove into the guardrail. He braked, parked, then took out the communicator and with hands trembling from excitement opened his mail:

The reply had landed on a message left in Pilyulkin's thread (the "doctor" was at length reasoning on the topic of the need to introduce sanctions against the Ukrainian government, for gas siphoned without payment over the past winter — in general, as usual), and contained text consisting mostly of exclamation marks:

"My name is Vika! I'm alive! I'm in Moscow! Please call me! +7-926-345-28-17!!!!"

Barely hitting the buttons, Vlad typed in the number — to copy and paste didn't even occur to him. His brains, from excitement, were almost not working.

— Vika?

— You're alive?! You're in Moscow?

— Well yes. On the MKAD right now.

— You're Korvalol96?

— In person, well better, still, Vlad. — for the first time, the joke nickname that had stuck back from his grandmother somewhat burdened the lad. One thing when a granny calls a prankster a "walking corvalol" and quite another when a barely-known woman does.

— Good! Vlad! Can we meet up? I'm completely alone here! Everyone's gone! I'm very, very scared!

— And where, exactly, are you?

— Chertanovo! Kirovogradskaya 17!

— Right, Vika — I'll drive to you now. Please open the window, or better go out to the street — it's warm, you won't catch cold. Come down by the stairs, even if you live on a high floor. And yes — I don't know whether mobiles will work by that point, so just in case I'll fire several shots into the air from a rifle. If there's no call but you hear three shots, go toward the sound. Okay?

— Okay! There's a ravine in front of our building. Stop between the building and the ravine — I'll look for you there!

— Agreed! Don't be scared — see, there are already two of us!

In the conversation partner's voice, Vlad distinguished distinct notes of panic and sobs; looks like the girl was on the edge. His running imagination, oriented by her sweet, ringing yet tender little voice, had already painted a languid princess awaiting rescue. The "knight," of course, reined in his imagination, but not very strictly. The potential picture made him "floor it," all the more so as fear of fines and police clearly wasn't necessary. Okorok, indignant at his owner's driving style, gave an angry roulade in the back seat but was ignored.

In general, what was happening at the moment in Vlad's soul was hard to describe by ordinary measures. For example — he didn't for a second forget about, albeit dim-witted but sweet Dashka, and was actually driving to her in Krylatskoye when Vika reached him. Subconsciously, at the same time, he understood perfectly that Dasha's silent phone could mean exactly one thing. That's why some part of his being really treated Vika not just as a "lady in distress" but as a "lady in distress with prospects." Besides, her quite sexy voice tuned him.

In general, he pulled up to Vika's building twenty minutes after the conversation. He didn't find any princesses around, so he climbed onto the pickup's running board, raised his rifle, prepared to give a salute... And nearly fell off the running board from surprise on hearing someone fearfully scratching at the passenger door.

— Vika? It's open — climb in — the lad ducked inside, just in time to see the door fly open and reveal to his gaze...

A red and dishevelled mop of hair adorned a small, it must be admitted, elegant, little head on a thin little neck. Green, enormous eyes hid behind the lenses of clearly mismatched-to-the-face glasses. Freckles surrounded a shy smile sparkling with braces. The "princess" herself was a metre and a half tall, was thin as a reed, and her age was, God willing, 13 or 14.

Vlad carefully returned his dropped jaw to its place, while the girl... the girl, having made out the living and seemingly not-scary student, suddenly impulsively dove inside the car, hugged the lad and broke into bitter sobs.
