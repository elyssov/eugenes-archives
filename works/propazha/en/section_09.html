Chapter 9

...They found the cottage practically on the second try. Built of some thick, porous-on-break bricks (Vlad wasn't strong on construction), two-storey, small, standing slightly aside, about two hundred metres from the lake. The cottage was fenced by a not-too-high but quite capital little fence and equipped with an electrified door. The nimble and tiny Redhead managed to leap onto the fence cat-like, using the bed and then the roof of the Tundra as a springboard. Balanced a couple of seconds on the gatepost, then with a dashing pirouette went down. Vlad only whistled.

The girl needed about ten minutes to find a way to open the gates. The stationary remote, as it turned out, was hidden on the inside wall of the utility shed. Vlad drove the faithful "Taiga" into the yard (the yard immediately shrank in size) and looked around. Above all, he was pleased by the presence of a freestanding utility building from which came a steady humming. The lad immediately twigged what that was. The second pleasant surprise was the unlocked front door. Vlad had already been calculating ways of least-traumatic penetration into a house locked from inside, and the fact that these ways turned out unnecessary delighted him beyond words. From the kitchen already came chomping — the Redhead together with Okorok had started emptying the fridge. Vlad shook his head — the gluttony of the newly-acquired "back-bone-gnawer" was simply frightening.

— Little One, not that I begrudge it, but didn't they feed you at home? — the lad tried to put all his irony into his voice so as not to offend his partner.

— Nope! Dad thought if I ate too much, I'd grow tits and ass and lose all athleticism.

— I don't get it. How does this very "athleticism" combine in you with glasses and nerdiness?

— You're rude, young man! — the girl demonstrated her well-read-ness. — Should I make you a sandwich with red caviar? Oh! There's black here too! Never tried it!

— Not rude, just curious. Gobble the black, share the red!

— Mrrrrrrrrrrrr!!!!

— And give Okorok another sausage. He hasn't eaten since morning.

— As for your question — don't look at the glasses. You know how sporty I am? Dad ran me ragged practically every day. Look! — The girl with a sharp movement lifted her T-shirt to about her chest. Vlad, from surprise, didn't manage to look away and was forced to note quite well-developed abs.

The lads snacked well, after which they set about searching the new dwelling. The mistress, or rather her clothes, they found on the second floor. The master — on the first, in a small study, fairly decently equipped, as far as Vlad could judge, with computer equipment: on the floor stood a sizeable case ("SLI rig! Not bad! 980s! And the seventh Core! Wow! If the net hasn't died — must urgently put up torrents with games for download! Beanpole, move over, will you?!"), eight large monitors. The Little One was already in full swing rummaging in the system, installing and launching something — given that the owner, it seemed, did without a password.

In the utility annex Vlad discovered a working diesel generator at 15 kilowatts — apparently switched on automatically, having lost the substation. Next to the generator towered a metal tank of titanic dimensions. Tapping it, the lad discovered it was filled to the brim. A minute later he convinced himself of this, having found a hatch beneath which was hidden a dial with the figure "1000 l." The next pleasant discovery was the presence of a battery cellar: a small hatch in the far corner of the boiler room led to a basement where, in even rows, stood crates with batteries. Vlad whistled in amazement, imagining how much this project had cost the owner.

Next to the boiler room was also discovered a concreted cap of a well. It's hard to say how deep it was, but the water in the tap was definitely fresh and fit for drinking.

At the kitchen exit he collided with the Redhead, again trying to make her way to the fridge.

— Oh! And I was just looking for you! — the Little One, seeing her partner, instantly replayed her plans. — I know what the master of the house did!

— And what was that?

— Professional trader. There are three different providers here, on each a hundred-channel. Plus there's also Yota dangling for insurance. And on the computer he's got very specific software — Bloomberg, Alpari and other crap. Will need to be wiped, by the way.

Outside the window the diesel fell silent. The lads exchanged wary glances, after which Vlad slapped his forehead.

— Right. Batteries are charged. It formally worked the program and fell asleep. Saves engine hours.

— There are also batteries?

— Yep. Like a submarine. By the way, it occurred to me — the less we'll eat electricity, the longer the engine will last.

Vika sceptically glanced at the old commander's watch, looking frankly grotesque on her fragile wrist.

— I have a business proposal! Let's start saving electricity right now — let's go to bed!

...The next week passed for the lads extremely tumultuously: the next morning, in the cottage's integrated garage, they discovered two cars at once: a small lemon-yellow Citroen DS3 and a deep black Range Rover. They'd found the car keys in advance, so the Redhead, even without asking permission, instantly jumped behind the wheel of the "citramon" (Russian play on Citroen/citramone medicine), started the engine and dashingly flew out into the street. Miraculously not knocking down the Tundra's bed, the small spontaneous disaster, almost putting her yellow car on two wheels, flew out onto the street... Half an hour later, Vlad had to drink a sedative when he learned that the hooligan had learned to drive in some computer racing games.

This whole day was decided to be dedicated to "shopping": electricity in the city had started to "run out," one substation after another failing. Consequently, perishables had to be moved to their "base" as soon as possible. And simultaneously to ensure worthy storage.

This time the raid target was the "Europark" shopping complex on Rublyovka. Along the way the lads, grunting and cursing, loaded into the pickup bed three freezing units borrowed from MediaMart. In Auchan's back room they found several blocks of ice apparently stored for the fish department. Smashing them, they poured them into the new freezers, after which loaded the not-yet-thawed meat and fish into them, and again filled the bed to capacity with canned goods, grains and other pasta. The Tundra even slightly sagged.

The forays continued all week. Besides the obligatory visit to Auchan, Vika started hanging around in Zara and smaller boutiques. However, due to her smallness and flat-chestedness, she had no chance of finding anything fitting in the adult sections. And in the children's, she herself was not eager to stock up. Vlad still got lost trying to understand how to treat her: either as a grown-up girl, or as a little young woman. At the present moment, Vika represented a kind of fusion of these two variants: not serious enough for an adult, but already far from childish innocence and carefreeness. Looking once in the evening, over her shoulder, at how she chopped monsters with a chainsaw in the new Doom, Vlad settled on "little young woman."

In general, Vika tried to seem carefree and with great willingness grabbed any tasks and work... But Vlad heard her crying at night. He himself was also visited at night by the ghosts of loved ones from memory. Perhaps that's why, understanding how hard it was for Vika, Vlad tried to be maximally considerate and caring with her.
